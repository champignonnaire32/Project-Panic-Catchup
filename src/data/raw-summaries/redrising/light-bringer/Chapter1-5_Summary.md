### Chapter 1: Darrow: Castaway

Darrow is shipwrecked on Marcher-1632, a moon-sized sphere of compacted space trash orbiting the sun. He and two hundred Martian survivors hide in an old Sons of Ares base buried within the waste escarpment. They spend their days welding the hull of the Archimedes, an antique fifty-meter corvette damaged during their escape from Mercury. Cassius au Bellona and his Pink accomplice, Aurae, stole the only functional relic six weeks ago to scavenge for helium-3 at Starhold. Darrow feels shorn of his myth and army after abandoning his Free Legions on the sands of Mercury.

The survivors are cut off from all communication with the Republic, leaving Darrow blind to the state of the war. He fears for his wife Virginia and daughter-turned-general Sefi, wondering if Lysander au Lune has already taken the Morning Chair. Darrow imagines choking Atalantia, Atlas, and Lysander to death while waiting in the base. He begins reading a tattered book left by Aurae titled The Path to the Vale, which contains esoteric metaphors about life's journey. Despite his skepticism, the text suggests that his current isolation might be a necessary stepping stone toward home.

Thraxa au Telemanus, managing shifts from inside the base, monitors the radiation levels that threaten the welding crew. She warns Darrow that the moon's rotation is accelerating, meaning the sun will soon boil them in their suits. Darrow calls his squad back to the airlock, counting heads to ensure every survivor makes it to safety. He pauses at the threshold to look toward the ruddy light of Mars, remembering the sounds of his family and home. As the sun blazes through his golden eyelids, he descends into the darkness of the base to prepare for the next cycle.

### Chapter 2: Darrow: The Book

Life inside the lead-lined walls of the Sons of Ares base is a slow siege of claustrophobia and radiation sickness. The survivors are pale, bald, and emaciated, surviving on water stores and freeze-dried amino mush found in the abandoned halls. Darrow clutches a gravBike key given to him by his son Pax, worrying about Virginia and the missing Sevro au Barca. Aurae previously played a lyre to fill the mess with songs of the Rim for the tired soldiers. Imperator Harnassus leads a team of mechanics in the machine shop, working to repair the Archimedes’s reactor and stealth plating.

Harnassus expresses deep suspicion of Aurae, suggesting the Raa hetaera might be a member of the Rim's Krypteia intelligence. Darrow visits Screwface in the coms chamber, finding the legendary Howler mutilated and bitter. Screwface lost a leg and his scalp during the fall of Heliopolis and now relies on a plastic peg and wool cap. He blames the fall of the Free Legions on the desertion of Sevro and the political conniving of the Republic's mobs. Darrow tries to offer comfort, but Screwface views their small group as mere debris circling a drain.

Darrow moves to the ship's sparring chamber to train with practice razors in shifting gravity. He instructs the computer to narrate Aurae's book using a voice sample of Virginia’s Sovereign addresses. As the gravity fluctuates from point two to four point five G’s, he flows through the Willow Way to maintain his lethality. His left arm remains pinned and fragile, a souvenir of the duel with Lysander that cost him his army. Training ends abruptly when the proximity alarm screams, announcing an inbound Votum torchship.

### Chapter 3: Darrow: Revenants

Alarms blare through the base as the survivors scramble to defend their hidden harbor from the Votum torchship. Darrow catches a railrifle from Thraxa, who prepares to lead a boarding party with her razor, Bad Lass. They plan to play dead and ambush any landing teams, hoping to seize the enemy vessel and fly it back to Mars. Screwface interrupts the battle stations by laughing over the coms, identifying the ship as a friendly vessel. The torchship connects via umbilical, and the legendary pilot Colloway Char emerges to meet the stunned castaways.

Colloway reports that Mars still stands and that Virginia governs the Republic from the city of Agea. He reveals that Kieran, Darrow's brother, is now the ArchGovernor of Mars following the assassination of Rollo months prior. The solar system is currently choked by jamming and sensor soup, with the Rim’s Dust Armada and Dragon Armada leading the assault. Sefi is dead, having been blood eagled by an imposter claiming to be Ragnar’s ancient father. Darrow’s niece Rhonna is missing and presumed dead after her shuttle crashed in Heliopolis during the EMP.

Cassius enters the hangar carrying four canisters of warship-grade helium-3 stolen from his mother’s operations. He is accompanied by Aurae, who helps carry the fuel despite the silent glares of the suspicious Martian soldiers. Colloway brings two thousand and eleven survivors from Mercury, all crammed into the battered, stolen Votum vessel. He recounts the horrors of the impalement road Atlas au Raa constructed from Heliopolis to Tyche for the remaining legions. Cassius halts the reunion to reveal that Sevro is alive but has been sold at a high-society Syndicate auction.

### Chapter 4: Darrow: The Sordid Affair

Darrow watches a hologram of a naked, tattooed Sevro being auctioned off with a wolf-head helmet covering his face. A second recording shows Sevro emaciated and muzzled in a slave rack, imprisoned within a Carthii hangar. Apollonius au Valii-Rath, the Minotaur, appears on screen to challenge Darrow to a final confrontation on Venus. Apollonius plans to hunt Sevro in the Hanging Coliseum of the Venusian dockyards on the ninety-first day of his captivity. While Harnassus and Thraxa urge a retreat to Mars, Darrow feels drawn to the proximity of his captured friend.

Darrow feigns agreement to return home, but privately prepares to infiltrate the Dockyards of Venus with Cassius. Aurae confronts them, revealing she is a highly trained Raa hetaera capable of killing them all with poison. She demands to join the mission as their escape pilot, asserting that her path is entwined with their rescue attempt. Cassius confesses his deep guilt over killing Fitchner and his desire to earn redemption by saving Ares's son. Darrow retrieves a thirty-megaton atomic warhead nicknamed Dominus Portobello and wraps it in a towel for transport.

The three conspirators attempt to sneak into the hangar at night but find their path barred by the entire base garrison. Thraxa realizes Darrow cannot be dissuaded and gifts him her family razor, Bad Lass, to ensure he dies with a blade. Screwface offers to join the suicide mission, but Darrow commands him to return to Mars to protect Virginia. Darrow walks through the parting ranks of his soldiers, ignoring the silent rage of a dark-skinned Red barring the way. He raises a fist and shouts a solitary "Hail libertas" before boarding the Archimedes and burning for Venus.

### Chapter 5: Lysander: Games

Lysander au Lune presides over triumphal games in Heliopolis to celebrate his victory over Darrow's Republic forces. He bears a hideous burn scar on his face from Darrow’s boot, which he keeps as a point of honor. The city is infested with radiation from Atalantia’s atomics, causing many citizens to wear eccentric, colorful wigs to hide their baldness. Glirastes the Architect and Rhone au Flavinius, a Gray commander, accompany Lysander as he observes the wild sunbloods herded through the streets. Lysander uses his popularity to secure his position, though Rhone warns that love is an expensive substitute for soldiers.

Lysander is secretly rebuilding the Lightbringer, Darrow’s crashed ship, using wreckage salvaged from the White Fleet. Glirastes expresses fear over Lysander’s secret pact with the Minotaur and the financial strain of the massive reconstruction project. Pytha, the Blue pilot who abandoned Cassius, serves as the future captain of the unpainted, monstrous vessel. In the executive box, Lysander hosts Valeria au Carthii, a ruthless young Gold seeking to reclaim her family’s stolen dockyards. He attempts to charm the Carthii delegation into an alliance, hoping to use their wealth to challenge Atalantia’s grip on power.

The games begin with a grand prix of chariots, sending the two hundred fifty thousand spectators into a frenzy. Tharsus au Rath, Apollonius’s brother, taunts the Carthii from a neighboring box with reminders of the Minotaur’s atomic threats. Cicero au Votum enters the arena as the reigning champion, riding for Team Hermes behind the horse Blood of Empire. Despite promising Lysander he would not race, Cicero dedicates his performance to the Savior of Heliopolis. Lysander leaps from his box into the common stands to place a bet, showing solidarity with the Votum legions as the race starts.
