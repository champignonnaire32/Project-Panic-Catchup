### Prologue: SHADE OF FEAR

A Shade named Durza, a magical being with crimson hair and maroon eyes, waits in ambush with twelve horned Urgals. Three elves riding white horses enter the trap, carrying a mysterious sapphire stone in a pouch. Durza uses magic to kill the two elven guards and captures the elven lady, Arya, after she attempts to flee. Before being knocked unconscious by a magical red bolt, Arya uses the stone to trigger a flash of emerald light. The stone disappears, infuriating Durza, who then executes the surviving Urgals and takes Arya captive.

Durza searches the area for the missing stone but finds nothing among the scorched trees. He mounts his horse and secures the unconscious Arya to his saddle for transport. The Shade ignites a massive ring of fire around the ambush site to prevent any escape or interference. He ignores Arya’s beauty, focusing only on the lost artifact and his orders. The fire continues to burn as Durza departs the forest with his prisoner.

The Shade travels away from the ambush site while nursing his rage over the stone's disappearance. He maintains his magical control over the area until he is certain no one else remains. The captured elf remains silent and unconscious throughout the initial journey. Durza’s thoughts dwell on the consequences of losing the stone to an unknown location. He eventually leaves the burning forest behind, heading toward his destination with the elven lady.

### Chapter 1: Discovery

Eragon, a fifteen-year-old hunter, tracks a wounded doe through the Spine, a dangerous mountain range in Alagaësia. An explosion suddenly shatters the night, scaring away his prey and leaving a charred circle of grass. In the center of the blast radius, Eragon finds a polished blue stone with thin white veins. He cautiously examines the smooth, oval object and finds it feels lighter than its size suggests. Despite his fear of magic, Eragon decides to keep the stone to trade for meat for his family.

The hunter packs the blue stone into his wooden-frame pack and hides in the forest for the night. He eats a meal of bread and cheese before falling asleep under the roots of a fallen tree. The next morning, he returns to the blast site but finds no new clues about the stone's origin. He begins the long trek out of the Spine toward his home in Palancar Valley. Eragon remains vigilant, knowing the Spine’s reputation for causing accidents and disappearing soldiers.

The journey home takes several days through rough game trails and freezing temperatures. Eragon crosses a precipitous ravine where the Anora River rushes toward the valley below. He observes the distant village of Carvahall and the small farm patches from a high slate outcropping. The weight of the stone in his pack serves as a constant reminder of the strange event. He eventually reaches the valley floor as dusk begins to settle over the landscape.

### Chapter 2: Palancar Valley

Eragon enters the village of Carvahall and attempts to trade the blue stone for meat at Sloan’s butcher shop. Sloan, a sallow man with a bloodstained smock, initially expresses interest but recoils when he learns the stone came from the Spine. The butcher refuses to touch the "sorcerer’s stone" and orders Eragon to leave the shop. Horst, the local smith with a thick black beard, intervenes and offers to pay for the meat himself. Eragon accepts the meat and agrees to work off the debt at Horst's forge in the spring.

Horst carries the heavy packages of roasts and steaks outside for Eragon to pack. Eragon offers the stone as payment to Horst, but the smith tells him to keep the strange rock. They discuss Sloan's irrational behavior and his tragic history involving the death of his wife in the Spine. Eragon delivers a romantic message from his cousin Roran to Katrina, the butcher’s daughter. He then departs the village and heads toward the farm he shares with his uncle and cousin.

Eragon reaches the farm, which consists of a shingled house, a barn with two horses, and several fields. He shows the meat to his uncle Garrow, a lean man with intense eyes and graying hair. Garrow initially reacts with anger at the perceived charity but relents when Eragon explains the work agreement. They examine the blue stone together, and Garrow advises selling it to the traders when they arrive. Eragon hides the stone under his bed and falls into a deep sleep.

### Chapter 3: Dragon Tales

Eragon wakes and reflects on his mother, Selena, who left him with Garrow sixteen years ago and never returned. He joins Garrow and Roran in the fields to complete the harvest before a winter blizzard arrives. The family spends the next several days preparing food and surviving a massive snowstorm that blankets the valley. They grow anxious when the traveling traders are late, fearing they will run out of essential supplies. Finally, Eragon discovers fresh wagon ruts in the snow, signaling the traders' arrival in Carvahall.

The family travels to the village and finds it crowded with merchants, entertainers, and colorful tents. Eragon and Garrow meet with Merlock, a trader who specializes in jewelry and odd trinkets. Merlock examines the blue stone with copper scales and a jeweler's glass, revealing it is harder than diamond and hollow. He refuses to buy the stone, claiming it is too valuable for the local market and potentially made by magic. Merlock also warns them about rising instability, Urgal migrations, and rumors of a Shade in Alagaësia.

In the village tavern, Eragon listens to Empire grain buyers who claim the Varden rebels have formed a pact with the Urgals. He publicly challenges their claims, but the crowd remains divided on the political situation. That evening, the villagers gather to watch troubadours perform and listen to the storyteller Brom. Brom recounts the history of the Dragon Riders and the betrayal of Galbatorix, who killed his fellow Riders and became king. The story of Galbatorix’s madness and his black dragon, Shruikan, leaves the audience in a somber mood.

### Chapter 4: Fate’s Gift

Back at the farm, Eragon tests the blue stone by hitting it with a wooden mallet and a leather hammer. He hears a faint squeak coming from the hollow interior but cannot find a way to open the object. That night, a loud chirping sound rouses Eragon from his sleep, prompting him to grab his knife. He searches his room but finds nothing until he realizes the noise is emanating directly from the stone. The stone begins to rock violently on its shelf before rolling onto the floor.

Cracks suddenly appear across the surface of the blue stone as something struggles to escape from within. A small dark head pokes out of a hole at the top, followed by a weirdly angled body. Eragon watches in shock as a tiny creature emerges and begins licking off a thin membrane. The creature skitters into the moonlight, revealing itself to be a miniature dragon. Eragon recoils, realizing the "stone" was actually a dragon egg that has now hatched.

The hatchling dragon stays in the moonlight, its scales reflecting the cool blue light of the room. Eragon remains frozen with his knife in hand, unsure how to react to the legendary creature. The dragon continues its movements, becoming more active as it adjusts to the world outside the shell. Eragon’s mind races with the implications of the dragon’s birth and the danger it brings. He watches the small beast, knowing his life has been permanently altered by this event.
