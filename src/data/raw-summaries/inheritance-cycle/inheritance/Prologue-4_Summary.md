### Chapter Prologue: IN THE BEGINNING:  A History of Eragon, Eldest. and Brisingr

Dragons were the original gem-scaled inhabitants of Alagaésia. The god Helzvog created the stout dwarves from the stone of the Hadarac Desert. Elves arrived across the silver sea and fought the dragons until a peace pact created the Dragon Riders. Humans and horned Urgals, along with the flesh-eating Ra’zac, eventually reached the land. Galbatorix, a young Rider, rebelled after losing his dragon, enslaving Shruikan and leading thirteen Forsworn to destroy the Riders.

Galbatorix stole the heart of hearts, known as Eldunari, from the dragons to gain their immense power. A man stole a blue dragon egg from the king’s castle and gave it to the Varden rebels. The elf Arya transported the egg for twenty-five years until the Shade Durza, a sorcerer possessed by spirits, ambushed her. Arya used magic to send the egg to the Spine, where a fifteen-year-old orphan named Eragon found it. The egg hatched for Eragon into the blue dragon Saphira, and the Ra’zac subsequently murdered his uncle Garrow.

Eragon trained with the storyteller Brom, who was killed by the Ra'zac before Murtagh, son of the Forsworn Morzan, joined the group. Eragon rescued the poisoned Arya from the Shade Durza and fled to the Varden in the Beor Mountains. In the ensuing battle, Eragon slew Durza but was left with a magical scar before traveling to the elven forest Du Weldenvarden. Eragon discovered he was actually Brom’s son, obtained the sword Brisingr, and witnessed the death of his mentor Oromis. The Varden now march toward the capital city of Urû'baen to confront Galbatorix and his enslaved dragons.

### Chapter 1: INTO THE BREACH

Eragon leads the Varden's charge into the city of Belatona while wielding his blue sword, Brisingr. He and Saphira breach the outer wall and engage two hundred Empire soldiers in a large courtyard. Saphira releases a torrent of blue flame that incinerates three soldiers while magical wards protect her from incoming arrows. The elf Arya joins the fray, her sword covered in gore as she fights alongside the elves of the guard. Eragon realizes the enemy soldiers possess powerful magical wards that prevent Saphira from burning the survivors.

Eragon cuts through the ranks of polearms and mail-clad soldiers with his superior elven speed and strength. The elven guards, led by the fur-covered Blédhgarm, follow closely to protect the Dragon Rider. Eragon attempts to throw a spear at archers on the battlements but misses the target entirely. Arya successfully impales two archers with a spear and ignites it using the spell Brisingr, causing the rest to flee. The Varden bring a battering ram into the courtyard while Roran directs a detachment of warriors near the keep.

The keep doors open, and a rider on a war-horse charges out carrying a glowing green lance. The lance is a Dauthdaert named Niernen, an ancient weapon specifically forged by elves to kill dragons. Blédhgarm uses magic to trip the horse, but the rider throws the lance and impales Saphira’s chest. Blédhgarm kills the rider and forces the supporting magician in the tower to commit suicide. A structural failure in the keep wall causes it to collapse directly onto Roran and his men.

### Chapter 2: HAMMERFALL

Eragon shouts as a twenty-foot mound of stone buries Roran and five other Varden warriors. He uses a spell to clear the thick gray dust from the courtyard to search for survivors. The collapse exposes several rooms of the keep, revealing fleeing Empire crossbowmen on the upper floors. Eragon considers using the Eldunari of the golden dragon Glaedr to lift the rubble but decides it would take too long. He remembers Roran’s last position under the doorway and realizes he can reach him from inside the building.

Eragon leaps up the rubble and breaks through the doors of the keep’s second story. He sprints through the corridors and kills a soldier by punching him into the ceiling. Upon reaching the main hall, Eragon encounters fifty soldiers and the governor, Lord Bradburn. Eragon ignites Brisingr with blue flames, terrifying the soldiers and forcing them to flee the hall. He charges through the keep's mechanisms and reaches a portcullis blocking the path to the vestibule.

Eragon uses the energy stored in the diamonds of his belt to heat Brisingr to an extreme temperature. He carves a man-sized hole through the iron grating of the portcullis to enter the collapsed area. In the dark vestibule, Eragon uses a light spell to find Roran grappling with an Empire soldier. Roran kills the soldier by driving a dagger into his jaw before standing up among the corpses. Exhausted and covered in debris, Roran speaks briefly to Eragon before losing consciousness and fainting.

### Chapter 3: SHADOWS ON THE HorizON

Eragon drops his sword to catch the fainting Roran and confirms his cousin is still breathing. Arya and Blédhgarm arrive in the vestibule to assist with the rescue operation. Saphira communicates telepathically that she will help the elves clear the remaining stones from the building. Blédhgarm uses elven magic to heal Roran’s broken wrist and various minor injuries. Roran recovers his hammer and expresses his intent to capture Lord Bradburn, the governor of Belatona.

The group returns to the main hall but finds it abandoned by the Empire's officials. They ascend the tower stairs and encounter a group of soldiers wielding pikes and spears. Eragon uses his strength to hurl enemy spears back into the formation, allowing Roran to push forward. Arya and Blédhgarm join the combat, killing the remaining twelve soldiers with lethal efficiency. The group eventually reaches the highest room of the western tower and breaks through the barricaded doors.

Lord Bradburn refuses to surrender his men and insults the elves for their alliance with the Urgals. Arya subdues Bradburn by overpowering his mind and casting a spell to put him into a deep sleep. Trumpets and cheers erupt from the Varden in the courtyard as the city of Belatona falls. Eragon looks out the window and sees smoke rising from burning buildings across the large city. He observes a host of werecats, creatures that can shift between human and cat forms, approaching from the east.

### Chapter 4: KING Cat

Nasuada sits on the governor's throne in the main hall, dressed in formal green and yellow attire. Eragon and the commander Jormundur stand on either side of her as they prepare for a diplomatic meeting. Nasuada expresses her hope to gain the support of the werecats to bolster the Varden's forces. Trumpets announce the arrival of Grimrr Halfpaw, the King of the Werecats and Lord of the Lonely Places. Grimrr enters in human form, appearing four feet tall with slanted green eyes and a vest made of animal skulls.

Four werecat guards follow Grimrr in their animal shapes, characterized by tasseled ears and black-tipped tails. Grimrr reacts with extreme hostility toward the herbalist Angela, hissing at her while his guards prepare to pounce. Angela dismisses the threat by saying "Cheep cheep," which further agitates the werecat king. Grimrr addresses Nasuada, stating that his race has decided to join the war because Galbatorix is vulnerable. He claims to speak for every able-bodied werecat in Alagaésia and asserts command over common house cats.

The werecats demand daggers, two suits of armor each, and daily rations of poultry and fresh liver. They also require a padded cushion to be placed next to the throne of any future human monarch. Nasuada agrees to the terms but insists the werecats choose only one form for their armor to save resources. She demands that a Varden spellcaster examine their memories to ensure they are not Imperial spies. Grimrr accepts the condition but explicitly forbids Angela from being the one to read his mind.
