# ELDEST: Chapter 20: CERIS to Chapter 24: ARROW TO THE HEART_Summary

### Chapter 20: Ceris

Eragon reveals his ten human toes to a dwarf to settle a wager during their journey. The group arrives at the edge of Du Weldenvarden where the Edda River meets the forest. Arya calls out in the ancient language to reveal her identity to the hidden elven guardians. Four elves garbed in moss-colored tunics emerge from the trees to greet the party. Eragon shows the silver mark on his palm and uses the ancient language to identify himself as a Dragon Rider.

The elves welcome the group to their outpost and provide a meal of fruits and vegetables. Lifaen and Narí are introduced as members of House Rílvenar who guard the forest border. Narí sings a song celebrating the return of the dragon egg and the rise of a new Rider. Thorv and the dwarf guards prepare to depart for Tarnag the following morning. Eragon leaves his horse Snowfire in Thorv's care for the duration of his stay with the elves.

The party boards two white canoes featuring seamless birch-bark hulls shaped by magic. Saphira swims alongside the boats, occasionally walking on the riverbed or submerging herself. Narí explains that large elven vessels are grown into shape through song rather than being built. The group navigates onto Eldor Lake where Eragon learns to steer using a leaf-bladed paddle. Narí quickly solves Orik's gold puzzle ring, causing Eragon to feel a brief moment of envy.

### Chapter 21: Wounds of the Past

The people of Carvahall engage in heated debates over whether to fight or surrender to the Empire. Roran adopts the name Stronghammer after his performance in the recent battle against the soldiers. Roran proposes a plan to evacuate the children and elderly to the Spine for safety. Sloan vocally opposes the plan because his wife died falling from the falls in those mountains. The village leaders eventually support Roran's strategy as the only way to protect their families.

Roran visits Katrina to ask her to join the group fleeing to the mountains. Katrina initially refuses to abandon Roran and her father to save herself. She agrees to go only after Roran swears an oath never to ask her to flee again. Roran promises that he will not separate from her in any future conflict. Katrina decides to leave in secret to avoid a direct confrontation with Sloan.

Roran finds Sloan guarding the village perimeter and decides to act behind his back. He tells Katrina that the expedition will move to the clearing above Igualda Falls. Katrina worries that her father will hate her for entering the cursed mountains. Roran promises to speak with Sloan about their engagement once the crisis has passed. The two share a moment of devotion before preparing for the secret departure.

### Chapter 22: Wounds of the Present

The villagers gather supplies and donkeys at the north end of Carvahall for the move. Sloan arrives at the staging area and discovers Katrina wearing a traveler's pack. The butcher violently attacks the pack and attempts to drag his daughter home by her arm. Roran intervenes by shoving Sloan away and announcing his engagement to Katrina. Sloan reacts with rage, disowning his daughter and stripping her of her inheritance.

Roran punches Sloan to the ground after the butcher lunges at Katrina again. Sloan flees to his shop in tears, leaving the village in a state of shock. The caravan of children and elderly begins the steep climb up Narnmor Mountain. Roran helps the group reach the slate ledge where the Anora River becomes the falls. The party establishes a fortified camp in a clearing guarded by large boulders.

Men spend the afternoon felling trees to create a wooden perimeter around the tents. Roran speaks with Nolfavrell, a boy who has already killed a man in battle. Nolfavrell promises to guard Katrina and the other children with his life. The able-bodied men bid farewell to their families and prepare to return to the village. Roran and the warriors descend the mountain, leaving Carvahall feeling empty and grim.

### Chapter 23: His Enemy’s Face

Roran returns to Horst's house where Elain warns him about the burden of Katrina's lost inheritance. Six soldiers and the two Ra'zac breach the house and storm Roran's bedroom at night. Katrina defends Roran by biting and clawing at the soldiers as they try to bind him. Roran retrieves his hammer and kills three soldiers while defending himself and Katrina. The Ra'zac seize Katrina with inhuman strength and carry her out through a window.

Roran pulls back the hood of a Ra'zac to see a black, beetle-like face with lidless eyes. The creature bites Roran's shoulder and breaks his wrist before leaping into the darkness. Roran and a group of armed villagers track the kidnappers to their camp south of town. Two Lethrblaka—giant, hairless flying beasts with long beaks—descend from the sky to the camp. The Ra'zac mount these creatures while the soldiers cower from the monsters' presence.

Roran discovers that Sloan has betrayed the village and is willingly leaving with the Ra'zac. Sloan argues with the creatures until one of the Ra'zac knocks him unconscious. The Lethrblaka fly away into the night carrying Katrina, Sloan, and the Ra'zac. Roran realizes Sloan killed the watchman Byrd to allow the soldiers into the village. Overwhelmed by his injuries and the loss of Katrina, Roran collapses into unconsciousness.

### Chapter 24: Arrow to the Heart

Eragon and Orik continue their journey up the Gaena River in elven canoes. Lifaen explains that the city of Urû'baen was originally an elven city called Ilirea. The elf details how King Palancar's human ancestors first settled in the valley eight hundred years ago. Eragon learns that the Tower of Ristvak'baen was built to watch over the banished human king. Lifaen reveals that elves are immortal beings who only die from physical injuries.

Eragon is shocked to learn that Arya is approximately one hundred years old. Lifaen warns Eragon to guard his heart against his growing attraction to the elven princess. Saphira enters Eragon's mind to comfort him regarding his desire for reciprocated love. Orik explains that humans first arrived in Alagaësia thousands of years before Palancar. The party discusses the impossibility of finding allies from the human homelands in the south.

Saphira is chased by a flock of angry birds after she hunts near the river. Eragon discovers a gyrfalcon with a broken wing struggling in a ravine. Arya shoots the bird with an arrow to end its suffering since it can no longer fly. She leaves the bird where it fell, viewing the act as a mercy rather than a hunt for food. Orik warns Eragon that elves may choose death over life if they deem a situation hopeless.
