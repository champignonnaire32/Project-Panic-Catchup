Blödhgarm and the other elven guards turn away non-urgent petitioners to preserve Eragon's strength for war. Eragon realizes he still lacks a proper sword and resolves to find a replacement soon. He and Saphira walk toward Nasuada’s pavilion to attend their scheduled meeting regarding Elva.

Eragon is surprised by the revelation of Angela's past and her connection to the ancient tower dweller. ### Chapter 18: Making Amends

Nasuada attempts to persuade Elva to remain cursed so she can serve as a tactical weapon against Galbatorix. Elva refuses, describing the constant agony of feeling every injury and death within a mile of her body. She explains that the blessing forces her to alleviate the suffering of others even if it harms herself. The girl has matured physically and mentally at an unnatural rate due to the dragon-mark on her brow. Elva insists that Eragon must attempt to remove the compulsion and the sensory overload he caused.

Eragon explains the two methods of breaking a spell: recanting the original intent or casting a counter-enchantment. He performs a long and complex counter-spell in the ancient language with Saphira’s added strength. The spell successfully removes the compulsion to help others but does not stop Elva from sensing their pain. Elva realizes she can now ignore the suffering around her without becoming physically ill. She declares she will no longer be a pawn for the Varden and will use her powers as she sees fit.

Elva threatens Eragon with his own guilt when he attempts to cast a second spell to block her senses. She leaves the tent after claiming that she is neither friend nor foe to the Rider. Angela slaps Eragon and complains that she must now spend a decade teaching the girl proper behavior. Greta, Elva's caretaker, weeps over the loss of her "lamb" and begs Eragon to protect the child. Eragon promises to watch over Elva, fearing she has become a monster capable of psychological manipulation.

### Chapter 19: Gifts of Gold

Saphira flies to the Jiet River to bathe and hunt deer while Eragon remains in the camp. Eragon uses a complex spell in the ancient language to extract gold particles from the soil. He collects three spheres of pure gold, each the size of a hazelnut, which leaves him physically exhausted. He visits Horst the smith, who is working a makeshift forge with his sons Albriech and Baldor. Eragon promises to check on Horst’s pregnant wife, Elain, who is struggling with the rigors of the military camp.

Eragon locates Gedric the tanner and repays him with a gold sphere for the ox hides he stole in Carvahall. Gedric is astonished to learn that his leather was used to create Saphira’s first flight saddle. Eragon then visits Jeod and Helen, finding them living in a cramped tent filled with historical scrolls. He recounts the full story of Brom’s death and his diamond-encrusted tomb to the grieving Jeod. Jeod gives Eragon a rare copy of *Domia abr Wyrda*, an exhaustive history of Alagaësia written by Heslant the Monk.

Eragon gives Helen a sphere of gold to help her re-establish her family's merchant business within the camp. He also promises Jeod that Saphira will allow him to fly on her back as a gesture of respect. Jeod explains that the Arcaena, a secretive sect of monks, believe all knowledge is sacred and must be preserved. Helen expresses her ambition to build a new merchant empire more successful than her father’s previous ventures. Eragon returns to his tent clutching his new book, satisfied with settling his old debts and honoring his friends.
