### 1: Chapter 1
Feyre Archeron, a nineteen-year-old human hunter, hides in a frozen thicket to hunt for her starving family. She spots a small doe and prepares to fire her bow to provide food for her father and sisters. A massive wolf the size of a pony emerges from the brush, stalking the same doe with unnatural silence. Feyre suspects the creature is a faerie—a magical, immortal being from the lands of Prythian. She draws a rare ash arrow, a heavy wooden projectile tipped with iron that can negate faerie magic.

The wolf is actually Andras, a High Fae sentry sent across the wall on a mission. Feyre fires the ash arrow into Andras's side, causing the ground to shudder as the magic fails. She fires a second ordinary arrow through the wolf's yellow eye, killing the immortal sentry instantly. Feyre skins the wolf to keep its pelt, unknowingly committing an act that triggers an ancient treaty. She wraps the bloody pelt around the doe carcass to hide the scent from other predators.

Feyre hoists the deer onto her shoulders and begins the miles-long trek home through the snow. She ignores her own hunger and freezing limbs to ensure the meat reaches her father and two sisters. The village hunters' warnings about giant wolves and strange folk in the area ring in her mind. Feyre feels no remorse for the dead wolf, prioritizing her family's survival over the creature's life. She reaches the edge of the forest as the sun sets, carrying the heavy burden of the kills.

### 2: Chapter 2
Feyre arrives at her dilapidated cottage and enters to find her sisters, Elain and Nesta, by the fire. She drops the doe on the table, and Elain immediately asks for a new cloak despite Feyre's exhaustion. Her father, who has a ruined leg from a creditor's attack years ago, praises the hunt. Nesta mocks their father and complains about needing new boots while ignoring Feyre's bloody state. Feyre explains the plan to eat half the meat, dry the rest, and sell the hides at the market.

Feyre's father notices the massive wolf pelt and questions where she obtained such a dangerous item. Nesta insults Feyre's appearance and refuses to chop wood, claiming her hands are not suited for rough labor. Feyre retreats to the bedroom she shares with her sisters, which contains an ironwood bed and furniture she has painted. She changes out of her hunting gear and joins the family for a dinner of roasted venison. During dinner, Nesta reveals that Tomas Mandray, a woodcutter's son, intends to propose to her.

Feyre argues that they have no dowry and that Tomas's family is too poor to support another mouth. Nesta mentions Feyre's relationship with Isaac Hale, a farmer's son she meets in a decrepit barn. Feyre's father defends Nesta's hope for a better life. Feyre declares that hope does not exist and points to the decaying cottage as proof. She recalls the deathbed vow she made to her mother to protect and stay with her family.

### 3: Chapter 3
Feyre walks into the village with Elain and Nesta to sell the hides on market day. They encounter a group of Children of the Blessed, human acolytes who worship the High Fae as benevolent masters. Nesta shouts her refusal to convert, showing her iron bracelet as a sign of defiance. Other villagers join in the hostility, insulting the acolytes for supporting the monsters who once enslaved humanity. The acolyte claims a human woman recently went to the border as an offering and became a High Fae's bride.

In the market square, Feyre bypasses her usual buyers to approach a scarred mercenary with many-shaded black eyes. The mercenary, a human hired to guard lands against faerie threats, agrees to buy the wolf and doe hides. She pays Feyre a high price in silver, explaining that someone once showed her similar mercy. The mercenary warns Feyre that things like martax—bear-sized monsters with lion heads and three rows of teeth—are crossing the wall. She reveals a scarred arm and leg poisoned by a faerie bite to illustrate the lethality of the immortal realm.

Nesta drags Feyre away from the mercenary, warning her that such warriors are dangerous and prone to violence. Feyre spots Isaac Hale across the square and follows him to an old barn for a sexual encounter. Isaac informs her that he will soon marry a girl with a handsome dowry, ending their casual relationship. Feyre returns home and gives a copper mark to her sisters, who spend it on supplies and a chisel. A massive roar suddenly shatters the cottage door, and an enormous beast with golden fur and elk-like horns bursts inside.

### 4: Chapter 4
Tamlin, the High Lord of the Spring Court, storms into the cottage in his beast form. High Lords are the most powerful members of the High Fae, the immortal ruling class who possess immense magical abilities. Tamlin appears as a golden feline beast with a wolf-like head and curved elk horns. Tamlin bellows "MURDERERS" at the family as Feyre stands in front of them with a hunting knife. He demands to know who killed his friend Andras, a High Fae sentry sent across the wall.

Tamlin cites the Treaty, an ancient agreement between realms that demands "a life for a life" for unprovoked attacks. He offers Feyre a choice: die immediately or live forever in his lands in Prythian. Feyre's father begs for her life, but Tamlin silences him with a roar and dismisses his offer of gold. Feyre realizes she must go to save her family from being slaughtered by the vengeful High Lord. She accepts the terms, and Tamlin snaps her remaining ash arrow in half before tossing it into the fire.

Feyre tells her father how to manage the remaining venison and gives him the money from the market. She warns Nesta that Tomas Mandray's father is abusive and tells her not to marry him. Her father pulls her aside, telling her she was too good for them and urging her never to return if she escapes. Feyre follows the beast out into the snow, leaving her home and family behind to begin her life in Prythian. She stops briefly to look back at the cottage before disappearing into the trees with Tamlin.

### 5: Chapter 5
Feyre follows Tamlin to the forest, where a white mare waits patiently for them to mount. She rides the horse northward toward the wall, the invisible magical boundary between human and faerie lands. Feyre keeps her remaining hunting knife hidden in her cloak, hoping for an opportunity to kill her captor. She reflects on the High Lords, seven powerful High Fae who rule the various courts of Prythian. Tamlin remains silent as they travel, ignoring her questions about his identity or the nature of her imprisonment.

Feyre worries about her father's inability to provide for Elain and Nesta without her hunting skills. She asks the beast for his name, but he only asks if it matters to a human like her. A charged, metallic tang—the scent of magic—hits her nose, and Feyre loses consciousness instantly. She wakes up days later, bound to the horse by invisible magical bonds that prevent her from reaching her weapon. They approach a hedge-bordered metal gate that serves as the entrance to the Spring Court estate.

The gate swings open automatically, and the horse carries Feyre into the southernmost border of the faerie realm. Feyre realizes Tamlin kept her in an enchanted sleep for the duration of the two-day journey from her home. She observes the lush, green landscape of the Spring Court, a realm of eternal spring which contains living plants despite the winter season. Tamlin continues to lead her deeper into his territory, heedless of her anger or fear. Feyre prepares herself for a life of captivity, her survival instincts focused on finding a way to escape or fight.
