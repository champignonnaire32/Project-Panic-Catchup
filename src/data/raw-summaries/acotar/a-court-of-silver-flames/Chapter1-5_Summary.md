
### Chapter 1

Cassian arrives at Nesta’s dilapidated apartment in Velaris at seven in the morning to escort her to Feyre’s new estate. He discovers Nesta in a disheveled state, wearing only a male’s shirt, and hears a hungover stranger attempting to flee through her bedroom window. After Nesta bathes and dresses, Cassian brings her tea and insists on seeing her safely to the meeting despite her hostility. Nesta reflects on her hatred for her father and her guilt over his death during the war with Hybern while she prepares to leave. She attempts to drown her inner darkness with wine, music, and sex, but finds the memories of the Cauldron still haunting her. Once they reach the new riverside palace, Nesta notes the absence of her own portrait among the family paintings and follows Cassian into a formal study.

Inside the study, Nesta finds Feyre, Rhysand, and Amren waiting to confront her about her recent self-destructive behavior and excessive spending. Amren immediately insults Nesta’s appearance and habits, leading to a tense standoff where Rhysand uses his High Lord dominance to force Nesta to remain in the room. Feyre admits she was wrong to give Nesta so much space and declares that their current arrangement is over. Nesta learns that her sister has been monitoring her activities and can no longer tolerate her spiraling out of control. The group reveals that they have already decided on a new path for her to ensure she contributes to the court. Feyre informs Nesta that she is being relocated to the House of Wind and must begin physical training immediately.

Cassian steps in to announce that he will be the one responsible for her training sessions in the Illyrian Mountains. Nesta reacts with venomous anger, but the group remains firm in their collective decision to intervene in her life. Amren explains that formal court law still considers Nesta an official member because she never resigned her post as a human emissary. The ultimatum is presented: Nesta can either move to the House of Wind and train with Cassian or be sent back to the human lands. Feyre reveals that Elain has already agreed to the plan and is currently packing Nesta’s belongings from her apartment. The chapter ends with Nesta realizing she has no choice but to comply with her sister’s demands as the apartment building is being condemned.

### Chapter 2

Cassian watches Nesta process the news that she is being forcibly moved to the House of Wind to begin training and library work. Feyre explains that Nesta will train with Cassian in the mornings at Windhaven and work for the priestesses in the afternoons. Amren informs Nesta that her apartment is already being emptied and her clothes are being sent to the palace. Despite Nesta’s claim that she is not a member of the court, Amren uses a law book to prove her official status. Feyre and Rhysand remain resolute in the face of Nesta’s vitriol, though Feyre is clearly pained by the necessity of the ultimatum. Cassian reflects on how Rhysand’s plan was finalized that morning after he received the bill for Nesta’s five-hundred-gold-mark tavern tab.

Nesta lashes out at Feyre, blaming her for the trauma of being turned into High Fae and the events of the war. Rhysand nearly loses his temper at Nesta’s insults, but Feyre intervenes and commands her mate to remain silent. The sisters argue over Nesta’s refusal to participate in the circle of family and her choice to isolate herself in a silent pit of despair. Nesta realizes that Elain has chosen to side with Feyre and has already assisted in the relocation process. Feyre reveals that she has had Nesta’s old apartment building condemned and slated for demolition to become a shelter for war-displaced families. The loss of her home and her few personal choices leaves Nesta seething with a sense of being controlled by others.

Nesta demands to speak with Elain, but Feyre states that Elain will visit only when she is ready. The tension between the sisters reaches a peak as Nesta declares she never wants to speak to Feyre again. Feyre accepts this and tells her she can talk to Cassian and the priestesses instead. The group prepares to depart for the House of Wind, leaving the riverside estate behind. Nesta feels a profound sense of abandonment as she realizes the bond with her sisters has finally frayed beyond repair. She is forced to accept that her life in Velaris has been stripped away in favor of this new, structured imprisonment. The chapter concludes with the sisters standing in a silence that confirms there is nothing left to bind them together.

### Chapter 3

Cassian meets with Rhysand in the High Lord’s two-level atrium study to discuss his new responsibilities. The room is filled with thousands of books, delicate glass cabinets, and a massive mechanical model of the world and stars. Rhysand praises Cassian’s work in settling the Illyrian war-bands during the spring but indicates that bigger tasks are coming. They discuss the dormant nature of Nesta’s stolen power and the fear that it could rise again at any moment. Rhysand reveals that he wants Cassian to investigate the human queens who are suspected of scheming once more. Cassian is tasked with playing the role of a courtier in the open while Azriel continues to work from the shadows.

In the entry hall of the riverside estate, Cassian prepares to take Nesta up to her new residence. Mor arrives from Vallahan and winnows the group to the House of Wind, dropping them into the sky above the mountain. Cassian catches Nesta in midair and flies her to the stone veranda, where she immediately pulls away from his grip. Nesta finds herself back in the red stone halls where she spent weeks after the war trying to help Elain recover. Cassian informs her that she will be staying in her old room and that he is located just one level above her. He mentions that Azriel is also staying in the House to maintain his own private space.

Nesta enters her bedroom and finds that her belongings from the apartment have already been delivered in trunks and bags. She observes the familiar wall of windows overlooking the city and the distant sea, feeling as if she is floating in the clouds. Cassian attempts to encourage her to eat a hot meal, but she flatly refuses and claims she is not hungry. He warns her that the training at Windhaven will begin the next morning and will be grueling without proper sustenance. Nesta ignores him and closes the heavy gray velvet curtains to shroud the room in total darkness. She crawls into bed fully dressed and falls into a deep sleep to escape the roaring silence in her mind.

### Chapter 4

Cassian meets Mor at a riverfront café in Velaris where she is basking in the sun and drinking tea. Mor reports that the king and queen of Vallahan have refused to sign the new peace treaty proposed by the Night Court. She explains that Vallahan expects a new war against the humans to redraw territory lines and is not interested in peace. The human queens are reportedly stirring their armies, leading Rhysand to fear a conflict the humans cannot possibly win. Mor suggests that the humans must possess a secret advantage in their arsenal to be so bold. Cassian identifies Vassa, the cursed firebird queen, as the best source of information regarding the queens’ secret plans.

Nesta wakes in the middle of the night to total darkness and momentarily confuses her surroundings with the old family cottage. She realizes she is in the House of Wind and goes to the window to watch the starlight over the city of Velaris. Memories of her mother’s death and the failed marriage proposal from a woodcutter’s son named Tomas haunt her thoughts. She reflects on how she became a wolf to survive poverty and now finds herself unable to put the wolf away. Below her, the city remains quiet while she grapples with the weight of her father’s death and her own transformation. She eventually returns to bed, ignoring her hunger and the bags of clothes she has yet to unpack.

In his own room, Cassian is awakened at three in the morning by the sound of Azriel returning to the House. He stays alert with a knife in hand until he realizes there is no threat to the new wards. Rhysand enters Cassian's mind to check on the status of Nesta and joke about his own evening with Feyre. Cassian informs him that Nesta has been asleep since their arrival and that the first lesson will commence in the morning. Rhysand confirms that Mor will be the one to winnow them to Windhaven for the training session. Cassian lies awake for the remainder of the night, bracing himself for the emotional and physical battlefield of training Nesta. He wonders how much of his own sanity will remain intact after the months of training and political maneuvering ahead.

### Chapter 5

Nesta sits in the House of Wind’s dining room at dawn, dressed in leather training pants that make her feel exposed. Cassian eats a large breakfast and insists that she consume a bowl of porridge and eggs to maintain her energy. He explains the nutritional importance of whole grains and lean meats while Nesta complains about the lack of sugar. Despite her protests that she is not hungry and will not participate in the lesson, Cassian remains unmovable. He tells her that not eating will not bring her father back, which causes Nesta to explode in a fit of rage. She eventually relents and forces down the lumpy porridge and eggs under his silent, steady observation.

Mor arrives to winnow Cassian and Nesta to the village of Windhaven in the Illyrian Mountains. The village is cold and grey, populated by warriors and females who watch Nesta with open curiosity and judgment. Cassian leads her to a dirt practice ring on a cliff edge where several other Illyrian males are already training. He introduces her to the basic concepts of a fighting stance, focusing on balance and the placement of her feet. Nesta remains stiff and defiant, refusing to follow his instructions or acknowledge the surrounding warriors. Cassian uses his own body to demonstrate the movements, forcing her to adjust her position as he circles her.

Devlon insults Nesta and questions Cassian's judgment in bringing an "outsider" to their sacred training grounds. Cassian defends his decision and reminds Devlon of the High Lord's orders regarding Nesta's rehabilitation. Nesta remains silent throughout the exchange, her eyes cold as she watches the interactions between the powerful males. After two hours of frustratingly slow progress, Cassian calls an end to the physical portion of the day's schedule. They return to the House of Wind so Nesta can begin her afternoon work in the library with the priestesses.
