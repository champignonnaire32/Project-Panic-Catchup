### Chapter 61: Darrow: The Three Masters

Darrow and Diomedes are held in a stone cell following their trial where they find Cassius already imprisoned. Cassius explains that he was captured by Black Owls after they ambushed him on his ship, the Archimedes. Diomedes criticizes Darrow's lengthy speech while Darrow mocks Diomedes for his blunt and suicidal defense during the proceedings. The three men wait in silence as the Daughters of Athena cheer loudly in the distance for their upcoming sentencing. Darrow takes out Pax’s key and prays while Diomedes reveals that a shapeshifter impersonating Helios caused the destruction of the Rim fleet.

Diomedes confesses that he sensed something was wrong with the imposter but failed to act out of fear of being mistaken. He believes his uncle Atlas au Raa is the true mastermind and that the Obsidian warlord Volsung Fá is Atlas's puppet. Darrow deduces that Atlas is planning a "closed loop" where he creates chaos to make Lysander look like a savior. Cassius defends Lysander’s character but Diomedes confirms Lysander was on the bridge of the Dustmaker when it fell. Diomedes describes how Lysander saved him by dragging him to a lifepod before Lysander himself was pulled back into the void.

Darrow attempts to warn Athena through the cell door that Fá will eventually betray her. Diomedes speculates that Lysander survived the paralytic spines used in the ambush because he had a toxin leech from a previous poisoning. Cassius is overwhelmed with guilt and relief upon learning that Lysander might still be alive. Darrow remains skeptical of the Lune prince but chooses to keep his opinions quiet out of compassion for Cassius. The trio remains trapped while the sounds of the Daughters’ chanting continue to echo through the stone walls of the prison.



### Chapter 62: Darrow: The Tyrants’ Debris

Black Owl guards escort Darrow, Cassius, and Diomedes into a war room where Athena and Aurae monitor the Obsidian fleet. Athena recounts her past as a Red rebel who was manipulated by the Golds into becoming a terrorist for a Krypteia operation. She explains that Ares eventually showed her that her clan's uprising was orchestrated to justify Gold control. Athena questions if Darrow can truly shatter the illusion of Gold power and asks if he has a plan for Atlas. A holographic display shows the Ascomanni armada moving toward Europa as the tension in the room mounts.

Sevro emerges from the shadows wearing the Twilight Helm, which allows him to disappear into total darkness. He gives a speech echoing the words of Ares and admits that he was initially angry with Darrow for losing his way. Sevro explains that he convinced the Daughters to trust Darrow by arguing that they must stop waiting for a savior and become the light themselves. He calls Darrow "boss" for the first time in years and acknowledges that they all need each other to win. Sevro asserts that they need builders like Athena, shields like the Daughters, and a "Reaper" to break the enemy.

Athena agrees to commute the prisoners' sentences and returns the black spear Pyrphoros to Darrow. Darrow swears on his son’s life to kill Volsung Fá and return the weapon once the deed is done. Diomedes is presented with a document demanding the abolition of the Gold hierarchy in exchange for the Daughters' alliance. Aurae challenges Diomedes’s sense of superiority by forcing him to look her in the eye and acknowledge her as an equal. Diomedes laughs at the absurdity but ultimately gives his oath to champion abolition in the Rim. Athena promises to open the sealifts to save the surface people now that she has her spear.



### Chapter 63: Lyria: Mashed Taters

The evacuation of Ilium continues as the sealifts bring thousands of refugees down into the undercity while newly armed soldiers head up. Lyria sneaks into a barracks to steal a Daughter uniform and successfully bluffs her way past guards into the command center. She claims to be Darrow's niece Rhonna and uses a can of stolen gun polish as a prop to bypass security. Inside, she finds Darrow, Diomedes, and Athena arguing over a map showing the red dot of a submersible traveling through the sea. Sevro catches Lyria by the ear and drags her out of the room for her security breach.

Hours later, Lyria finds Darrow eating a meal of potatoes and demands to be included in the mission to stop the Obsidians. She admits that she once hated Darrow for destroying her home in the mines but now wants to fight for Mars. Lyria argues that she is the only person who can reach Volga and guide her back to the Republic. She points out that Volga is likely lost and adapted to Fá's rule only to survive. Darrow is initially dismissive until Lyria reveals she has learned to speak the Obsidian language of Nagal.

Lyria challenges Darrow to honor his friendship with Ragnar by saving Ragnar’s daughter, Volga, from Fá’s influence. Darrow warns her that the mission is secondary and that they cannot protect her if she is captured or tortured. Sevro supports Lyria's plan, noting that turning Volga would double their chances of success and provide evidence against Atlas. He nicknames Lyria "Truffle Pig" and says she has earned the right to take her shot at saving her friend. Darrow eventually sighs and mashes his potatoes, signaling his reluctant agreement to let Lyria join the fight.



### Chapter 64: Lysander: The Noble Lie

Lysander arrives at his expeditionary fleet on the moon of Valetudo while disguised as a survivor of the massacre at Kalyke. He observes the Lightbringer, which has been repaired by Votum builders and now displays the Lune crescent inside a gold pyramid. Rhone warns Lysander to keep his soldiers in the dark and operate with clinical detachment to maintain his status as Sovereign. Lysander feels a profound loss of trust in Rhone, who previously poisoned him on Atlas’s orders. He greets thirty-three thousand Praetorians and several hundred Reformers who have gathered to support his cause.

To cement his story, Lysander bares his chest to show the wounds Atlas gave him to simulate a narrow escape from Fá. He calls for a hunt against the Obsidians and uses the Praetorians' shame over Octavia’s death to incite their bloodlust. Lysander secures the loyalty of the New Shepherds by praising their virtue and their role in defending the weak. He manipulates the fickle Gold allies by arguing that boldness is the only path to reclaiming their influence in the Rim. Cicero hands Lysander the command sceptre, an iron bundle ending in a lightning bolt, symbolizing his official imperium.

Lysander broadcasts a speech to the fleet claiming that Fá has razed Callisto with atomics and seized the agricultural Garter. He lies to his friend Pytha about the ambush, claiming he was a helpless passenger forced to evacuate the Dustmaker. Pytha comforts him during a breakdown, unaware that Lysander has secretly allied with Atlas to secure his own power. Lysander realizes that telling the truth would destroy Pytha’s love for him and turn her into a subordinate rather than a sister. He weeps in her arms, feeling the weight of the strings Atlas has attached to his soul.



### Chapter 65: Lyria: Into the Maelstrom

Lyria follows Black Owl guards through the Heraklion sealift where thousands of refugees are being processed by Gray soldiers of House Kalibar. A Gray officer attempts to block their path until Cassius and Sigurd arrive to escort Lyria to the surface. Cassius explains that he is acting as an ambassador for Diomedes to convince stubborn sealords to cooperate with the Daughters. Sigurd warns that the Ascomanni warlord Volsung Fá skins and castrates his enemies, keeping them leashed to his throne. The lift rises through the depths, illuminated by bioluminescent fish, as Lyria prepares for her infiltration mission.

Cassius reveals that he cannot join Lyria because his presence would immediately blow her cover among the Obsidians. Sigurd assures them that his friends Fenrir and Gudmund are waiting to take Lyria to Volga and redeem their honor. Lyria recalls the two rules of the mission: do not get caught by Ascomanni and remember there is no rule number two. The lift doors open to the sound of crashing waves and distant bombardment as they reach the surface station. A floating gunship hovers over the courtyard, blaring orders to the massive crowd of fleeing civilians.

As they walk toward their shuttle, Lyria spots a shadow crawling on a gun installation but it vanishes before Cassius can see it. A shimmering figure suddenly appears among the refugees and dissolves his camouflage to reveal a lean, eight-foot-tall Ascomanni warrior. The warrior wears a shark-head helmet and boots with fans on the heels, standing naked with leathery orange skin. He begins slaughtering the crowd with two long blades, causing blood and limbs to geyser into the low gravity. Sigurd identifies the attacker as a Harbinger, a specialized swimmer who bypassed the station's sonar to begin the massacre.
