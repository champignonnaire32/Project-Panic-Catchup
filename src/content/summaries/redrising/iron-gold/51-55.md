### Chapter 51: Ephraim

Ephraim sits on a hotel terrace on Luna, drinking and taking zoladone while waiting to charter a private shuttle to Earth. Volga confronts him about his drug use and his role in kidnapping the Sovereign's children, eventually knocking his drug dispenser off the balcony. Their argument turns physical when Volga pushes Ephraim, and he responds by jamming a stunner into her leg. Volga nearly throws him off the balcony in a rage but stops, calling him a monster before walking out on him. Ephraim remains alone, reflecting on his lack of connection to his deceased crewmates, Cyra and Dano, before heading to a storage unit to collect his gear.

Ephraim arrives at a private skyhook to board the Eurydice Wind, a chartered yacht, but realizes Volga is not coming. He prepares to depart alone and goes to the cockpit, only to find the flight crew and pilots have vanished from the ship. He draws his snub-nosed pistol and discovers Holiday Ti Nakamura and Lyria waiting for him in the ship's lounge. Holiday reveals she has a Stained Obsidian in the cargo hold and demands to know the location of the kidnapped children. Ephraim initially mocks them, but the conversation turns to the death of Trigg, Holiday's brother and Ephraim's former fiancé.

Lyria appeals to Ephraim's buried sense of morality by reminding him that Trigg saw him as a good man. Ephraim admits the Syndicate, specifically the Duke of Hands, orchestrated the kidnapping for a mysterious figure known as the Queen. Holiday informs him that Victra au Julii is also hunting the Syndicate and warns that the children will be killed if she finds them first. To ensure Ephraim's cooperation, Holiday shows him a video of Volga being held in a cell and threatened with death. Ephraim agrees to help rescue the children in exchange for pardons for himself and Volga, eventually speaking with the Sovereign to finalize the deal.



### Chapter 52: Darrow

Apollonius addresses the 911 remaining soldiers of House Valii-Rath on a cracked tarmac on a Venusian island. He berates them for their lack of discipline and their failure to rescue him from the Deepgrave prison. Darrow, Sevro, and Thraxa watch the speech via holo from Tharsus's library, noting the soldiers' unkempt appearance and local braids. Apollonius claims the Ash Lord betrayed their house and calls for the soldiers to rise and reclaim their glory by hunting him. The soldiers respond with a roar, and Darrow prepares for the mission despite the small size of the available force.

Sevro expresses extreme doubt about the plan, arguing that Apollonius is insane and the mission is a suicide run. He accuses Darrow of being blinded by his own myth and taking shortcuts that have led to the deaths of their friends. Darrow insists the mission is still a go, reminding Sevro that the Republic is at stake and they cannot retreat to Luna. Sevro reluctantly agrees to follow orders but declares that he will be done with war after this mission to be a father to his daughters. Darrow watches his friend leave, feeling the growing distance between them while Thraxa prepares the remaining Howlers for the assault.

Two hours later, the Valii-Rath forces, including forty ripWings and five assault frigates, lift off from the island. Apollonius visits Darrow in the hangar, wearing baroque purple armor featuring a Minotaur chestplate, to offer a mocking farewell. Darrow reminds him that his internal bomb is on a three-hour timer that will detonate if the mission fails or if he retreats. Rhonna joins Darrow as the Nessus prepares for takeoff, and Darrow assigns her to a gunner chamber for the coming battle. Darrow reflects on the mystery of the Ash Lord's three-year absence from public view as they launch toward the darkzone.



### Chapter 53: Darrow

Apollonius's fleet initiates the attack on the Ash Lord's darkzone, losing several ripWings as they engage the hostile patrols. Darrow waits inside a starShell, a twelve-foot-tall mechanized armor suit, within the spitTube of the Nessus as the ship approaches the barrier. Colloway's ripWing squadron clears a path through incoming bandits, allowing the Nessus to cross into the communications-dead darkzone. The Howlers exchange banter and honor the memory of Ragnar Volarus as they prepare to launch from the ship. Darrow summons his hatred for the Ash Lord and prepares to exit the spitTube as the launch light turns green.

Darrow launches from the Nessus at three times the speed of sound and immediately faces a barrage of particle beams and anti-aircraft fire. The Howlers have emerged directly into the teeth of the enemy's perimeter defenses, which consist of automatic turrets atop various atolls. Darrow uses his particle cannon and railgun to destroy a large particle cannon installation, clearing the way for his squad to land. The remaining starShells regroup on a rock formation while the Nessus engages the main islands with its heavy batteries. Darrow and Sevro rocket upward to survey the Ash Lord's emerald sea and volcanic island stronghold.

Darrow observes that Apollonius's legionnaires are storming a beachhead while the Ash Lord's forces pour out of subterranean barracks. To prevent enemy reinforcements, Darrow leads the Howlers on a subaquatic approach toward an airfield on a neighboring island. They navigate a minefield with help from the Nessus and surface to massacre the crews of several hovercraft carrying pilots. Darrow clears a passenger hold of Blue pilots, using his railgun to ensure no aircraft can be launched against them. With air superiority partially established, the Howlers move across the rocky island toward the main coastline to begin the final assault.



### Chapter 54: Darrow

Darrow and the Howlers fly toward the Ash Lord's tower and are intercepted by twenty Peerless dragoons from Legio XIII Dracones. The two groups clash in a high-speed aerial battle involving razors, pulseFists, and starShell weaponry. Darrow's starShell is damaged, forcing him to eject and fight in thinner pulseArmor as he collides with a Gold knight on a rocky ledge. He kills the knight with a pulseFist at point-blank range but takes a hit to the back, melting his armor. Sevro saves Darrow by crushing another Gold's head but warns that the Howlers are being overwhelmed by the maneuverable dragoons.

The remaining Howlers rally to the landing pad atop the high tower, fighting through a squad of Obsidians and Grays. Darrow deflects an Obsidian's axe and kills several enemies with his razor, but he is eventually knocked down by a pulseHammer. An Obsidian head taller than Darrow pins him to the ground and attempts to scalp him with a crooked knife. Just as the blade reaches Darrow's forehead, Apollonius and his armored knights arrive in a purple Minotaur shuttle to reinforce them. Apollonius hacks the Obsidian in half and leads a slaughter of the remaining Ash Guard defenders on the roof.

Apollonius embraces a blood-covered Darrow and celebrates their victory on the beach, despite being outnumbered four to one. He orders his bodyguards to rejoin the hunt and eliminate any surviving Golds or lurchers on the island. Sevro limps toward the group with a badly broken arm and charred flesh visible through his damaged armor. Darrow realizes that Howlers Pebble and Clown are missing and fears they have been killed in the chaotic battle. Darrow, Sevro, and Apollonius prepare to enter the tower's security door to finally confront the Ash Lord.



### Chapter 55: Lysander

Lysander is awakened on Io by an old Brown servant named Aruka, who leads him through hidden tunnels to a private sitting room. He finds Gaia, Romulus's mother, who has dropped her senile act and is accompanied by a large Obsidian bodyguard named Goroth. Gaia challenges Lysander's perceptions of the war and mentions his grandmother, Octavia, and his godfather, Lorn au Arcos. She plays a heartwood piano and encourages Lysander to play, which triggers a vivid, buried memory of his mother. Lysander realizes he has forgotten significant portions of his childhood and suspects his grandmother may have suppressed these memories.

Gaia reveals that she wants Lysander's help to free Romulus from the Dust Cells to prevent a full-scale war with the Core. She introduces Lysander to Pytha, his pilot, who has been held and tortured by Pandora but is now being treated well by Gaia's staff. Gaia explains that Romulus's wife, Dido, plans to execute Lysander and Pytha to remove any challenges to her authority during the coup. The old woman argues that Romulus is the only leader capable of maintaining peace and resisting the influence of the Core. Lysander agrees to the mission, recognizing it as their only chance for survival in the face of Dido's impending war.

Pytha joins the mission despite her injuries, and Gaia provides them with a transponder to signal loyal legions once Romulus is safe. Gaia gives Lysander an exceptionally long razor named Shizuka, which belonged to her family and serves as a symbol of her authority. Goroth is assigned to guide them through the secret mountain tunnels toward the garage where hoverbikes are waiting for their escape. As they depart, Goroth says a tearful farewell to Gaia, who urges them to hurry before Dido discovers their disappearance. Lysander takes the razor and follows the Obsidian into the darkness, committed to freeing the Sovereign of the Rim.
