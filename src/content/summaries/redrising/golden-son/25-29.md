# RED RISING: Part III: Conquer - 25: Praetors to 29: Old Man’s Wrath



### Chapter 25: Praetors

ArchGovernor Nero au Augustus gathers his remaining Praetors and advisors in the warroom of the dreadnought Invictus to address their dire situation. The Sovereign’s forces have pushed them from the Core, and Nero’s own bannermen on Mars are failing to provide adequate support. Politico Pliny suggests a tactical retreat or surrender, but Kavax au Telemanus and his son Daxo push for a more aggressive stance. Mustang arrives late to the meeting, mocking the dithering council and challenging her father’s lack of a clear cause. Darrow intervenes by arguing that the House of Mars must project power to win over the soft Galilean families who depend on Core commerce.

Darrow proposes a bold two-part plan to steal the moonBreaker from Ganymede and ransom students from various Institutes across the Solar System. He identifies a massive eight-kilometer ship commissioned by the Sovereign as the primary target for theft. The council erupts in uproar at the audacity of declaring war on the Society rather than just the Bellona. Pliny attempts to discredit the plan, but Nero is intrigued by the possibility of asserting dominance over the Gas Giant moons. Darrow emphasizes that commerce and fear are the only languages the Galilean families truly understand.

The ArchGovernor ultimately approves Darrow's aggressive strategy and dismisses the council to prepare for the raids. Nero pulls Darrow aside for a private conversation to test his loyalty and political leanings. He warns Darrow against being a Reformer or a demokrat, showing him footage of the crew's hopeful reactions to Darrow's earlier speeches. Nero reveals that he wants Darrow to approach Lorn au Arcos, the legendary Rage Knight, to secure his military support. Darrow agrees to the mission, knowing Lorn's past relationship with his sons and his hatred for Nero will make it difficult.



### Chapter 26: Puppet Master

Pliny argues that Darrow's plan will expand the war until one side is completely destroyed. Darrow counters that the students of the Institutes are the most valuable leverage for randsoming the loyalty of powerful families. Mustang initially laughs at the insanity of the plan but is silenced by a harsh rebuke from her father. Nero calls Mustang a whore for her past relationship with General Cassius, prompting a tense standoff before she reclaims her dignity. She explains that she tried to avoid war through diplomacy but now realizes the Sovereign will never allow a peaceful resolution.

Nero commands Praetor Licenus to lead the main fleet as a decoy while he takes a smaller strike group to raid Ganymede. He denies Kavax’s request to join the strike group, insisting on handling the dangerous mission with a specific set of ships. Darrow is tasked with visiting Lorn au Arcos on Europa to convince the retired warrior to join their cause. Augustus dismisses the room after declaring his intent to become the King of Mars. Darrow waits for a final word with the ArchGovernor, who remains suspicious of Darrow's true motivations.

During their private talk, Nero expresses fear that Darrow might be a secret Reformer aiming to dismantle the Society's hierarchy. He explains the difference between slow power, like the Jackal’s business empire, and fast power, which lops off heads. Nero admits that Lorn au Arcos loves Darrow like a son, which is why Darrow is the only one who can recruit him. Darrow acknowledges that Lorn hates Nero but believes the old man hates the Sovereign even more. He leaves the meeting with the heavy responsibility of forcing a man of peace back into the storm of war.



### Chapter 27: Jelly Beans

Kavax and Daxo au Telemanus intercept Darrow in the hall to offer their family’s full support for his cause. Kavax embraces Darrow, expressing that there is no shame in Pax having fallen in friendship to the Reaper. Daxo reveals his own history as a Primus of Jupiter House and identifies the Jackal as a mutual enemy. Kavax’s large fox, Sophocles, sniffs Darrow and discovers jelly beans in his pocket, which Kavax interprets as a magical omen of approval. Daxo admits to slipping the jelly beans into Darrow’s pocket to ensure his father’s enthusiastic cooperation.

Darrow accepts the Telemanus offer of aid and promises to send instructions within the hour. He then meets with the Jackal via holo to refine the logistics of the Ganymede raid and the student ransoms. Orion sets a course for Europa, a journey that will take the crew two weeks to complete. Roque joins Darrow on the bridge but remains cold and distant due to Quinn’s injury and Darrow’s deception. Darrow attempts to apologize for drugging Roque at the gala, explaining he did it to protect him from the fallout.

Roque rejects the apology, stating that friends do not make choices for one another out of fear or planning. He points out that Darrow engineered the war instead of trusting his friends to help him legally. The tension between the two remains high as Roque walks away without a smile or a sign of forgiveness. Darrow feels the weight of his decisions as he prepares to face Lorn au Arcos. The bridge is filled with a skeleton crew of Blues as the ship sails toward the stormy ocean world of Europa.



### Chapter 28: The Stormsons

Darrow arrives at Lorn au Arcos’s castle on Europa, an island fortress surrounded by ninety-kilometer-deep oceans. Lorn describes the "stormsons," lowColor boys who braved the gales in skiffs, as a metaphor for the reckless bravery of youth. The gravity on Europa is extremely low, forcing Darrow to use gravBoots to stay grounded during their conversation. Lorn reflects on his own father's hardness and his own history of surviving eight Iron Rains without valuing life. He tells Darrow that his hands have felt the lifeblood of three sons and have stolen too many dreams of youth.

Lorn leads Darrow into a massive indoor forest where he keeps Icarus, a genetically engineered griffin larger than a horse. As they walk, Darrow secretly drops micro-mines on the forest floor to prepare for a potential confrontation. Lorn explains that he left the Society because it is already dead, rotting from a cycle of greed and war. He reveals the dark history of Nero au Augustus, who secured his throne by murdering the Bellona and Cylus families. Nero had sent the head of his bride, Iona au Bellona, to her father as a message during a wedding feast.

Lorn reveals that a Praetorian death squad led by Aja au Grimmus is already inside the castle waiting for Darrow. He offers Darrow a chance to escape on Icarus to a hidden ship, hoping to avoid further involvement in the war. Darrow refuses to flee and instead signals his Howlers to rise and his fleet to ambush the Sovereign’s ships in orbit. He tells Lorn that he has no choice but to join the war because the Sovereign will now view him as a traitor. Lorn is horrified to realize that Darrow has trapped him into treason to save his own family.



### Chapter 29: Old Man’s Wrath

Darrow’s secondary fleet, commanded by Roque, ambushes the Sovereign’s ships in the sky above Europa. Lorn realizes he has been outmaneuvered and that the Sovereign will now shell his islands to glass unless they win the war. Aja au Grimmus emerges from the castle with twelve Obsidian Praetorians and Tactus au Valii-Rath, who is now a Protean Knight. Darrow detonates the micro-mines he planted, creating a series of explosions that scatter and destroy most of the Praetorians. Tactus and Aja manage to retreat back into the castle during the chaos of the tiered kinetic blasts.

The Howlers land and begin a room-to-room search of the castle to hunt down the survivors. Lorn suddenly realizes that Aja and Tactus will go after his grandchildren as a final act of spite. Darrow and Lorn sprint through hidden passages to a bunker where the children and their caretakers are hiding. They find Tactus standing over the bodies of three guards, holding his razor over a group of sobbing women and children. Tactus expresses his resentment toward Darrow, claiming he was always kept in the shadow and treated like a fool.

Darrow offers Tactus absolute forgiveness and a place as a lieutenant in his army if he spares the children. Tactus, physically ruined by the earlier explosion and emotionally broken, drops his razor and collapses into Darrow’s arms. The children and caretakers are led to safety as Roque confirms the space engagement is nearly finished. Once the room is clear, Lorn au Arcos suddenly stabs Tactus four times in the armpit with an ionDagger. Lorn leaves the room as Tactus dies in Darrow’s arms, gasping for the peace he could never find in life.
