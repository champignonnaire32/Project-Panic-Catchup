### Chapter 21

Elain interrupts a meeting at the river house to offer her services in tracking the Dread Trove with her Seer powers. Nesta vehemently refuses to allow her sister to participate, leading to a heated argument where Elain accuses Nesta of focusing only on her own trauma. Amren reveals that Nesta’s status as a Made being makes her uniquely immune to the Trove’s influence and capable of scrying for it. Feyre interjects to support Elain’s choice but admits she cannot risk the mission herself because she is pregnant. Cassian and the inner circle erupt in joy at the news, while Nesta silently processes her new role as an aunt.

Rhysand drops his protective shield around Feyre, allowing her new scent of a budding rose to fill the room. The group discusses the pregnancy, noting that Feyre is two months along and that Fae pregnancies last ten months. Nesta bows her head to Rhysand as a gesture of peace, signaling she poses no threat to his mate or the unborn child. Feyre explains that her powers might dangerously strain her body during the pregnancy, making Nesta the primary choice for scrying. Despite her fear and her recent conflict with Elain, Nesta agrees to search for the artifacts to protect her sister.

Nesta walks through the river house and encounters Feyre in front of a painting of the mountain Ramiel. Feyre privately reveals to Nesta that the baby is a boy, a fact she wanted her eldest sister to know first. She explains that the Bone Carver previously showed her a vision of this specific son during the war with Hybern. Feyre admits that she also unfairly focused on Elain's trauma in the past and hopes to bridge the gap with Nesta. Cassian joins them to escort Nesta back, and the sisters part with a tentative understanding regarding the family's future.



### Chapter 22

Azriel and Cassian discuss the feasibility of Nesta finding the Dread Trove while relaxing by the fire in their shared sitting room. Cassian expresses concern about Nesta’s volatile mental state and the trauma she experienced during her last scrying attempt. Azriel emphasizes the danger posed by Briallyn and Koschei, noting that even one artifact could tip the scales of power. They celebrate the news of Feyre’s pregnancy and the fact that the child will be a boy. Cassian reflects on his own desire for a family and the deep bond he observes between his brothers and their mates.

Nesta sits in her bed reading a novel when Cassian knocks and enters her room while still wearing his fighting leathers. He tells her that they have unfinished business from the previous night and that he intends to settle the debt. Nesta challenges his presence and questions if his friends would approve, but Cassian dismisses their opinions. He admits that his only mistake was not tasting her before she ran away during their previous encounter. Nesta agrees to his proposition on the condition that their sexual relationship remains separate from their training sessions.

Cassian lunges at Nesta and they engage in a frantic, demanding physical encounter on her bed. He strips her nightgown and kisses her body with a mix of tenderness and animalistic intensity. Cassian kneels between her thighs and uses his tongue and fingers to bring her to a forceful climax. Nesta experiences a profound release that leaves her physically spent and mentally reeling as she watches him. Once she has collapsed against the mattress, Cassian abruptly stops, gathers his clothes, and leaves the room while declaring them even.



### Chapter 23

Cassian reflects on the intensity of watching Nesta climax and the discipline required to leave her room without satisfying himself. He faces her the next morning in the training ring at the House of Wind, where they both pretend the previous night never happened. Azriel joins the session to practice hand-to-hand combat, noting his preference for swordplay over the close contact of physical brawling. Nesta observes the two Illyrian warriors with an unflinching assessment, showing no fear of Azriel's intimidating presence. The brothers decide to spar at full speed to demonstrate advanced combat techniques to Nesta.

The sparring match between Cassian and Azriel becomes a display of speed, strength, and centuries of shared training. They exchange blows using fists, feet, and wings while Nesta watches their muscled forms from just outside the chalk ring. Azriel uses a deceptive move to punch Cassian in the jaw, winning the match by exploiting Cassian’s protective instinct for Nesta. Cassian recalls how his focus on Nesta’s safety during the war with Hybern previously led to the death of his soldiers. After the lesson, Nesta ignores Cassian’s teasing about her attraction to them and prepares for her work in the library.

Nesta finds Gwyn at her desk and asks for information regarding the Dread Trove, though the priestess has never heard of the artifacts. Nesta explains that the items likely have a glamour to keep people from remembering them and asks for Gwyn’s research assistance. She encourages Gwyn to join the physical training sessions, but Gwyn insists she is an acolyte rather than a warrior. Nesta later descends the ten thousand steps of the House of Wind, using the repetitive motion to process her conversation with Gwyn. She stops at step two thousand with a sudden realization of how to reach the other priestesses.



### Chapter 24

Cassian meets with Clotho, who reports that Nesta has spent five days ignoring requests to stop practicing combat in the library. He finds Nesta on Level Five of the library using books as targets for her punches and kicks. Instead of reprimanding her, Cassian begins correcting her form and teaching her how to deliver a lethal right hook. Nesta admits she is practicing because she is tired of being weak and depending on others for her defense. They work through several combinations of strikes while priestesses, including Gwyn, watch from the railings above.

Nesta reveals that her public practice session was a calculated demonstration to show the priestesses that Cassian is a professional and respectful teacher. She intentionally irritated Clotho to ensure Cassian would be sent to the library where the other females could observe their interaction. Cassian realizes he has been used as a tool for outreach and laughs at Nesta’s crafty manipulation of the situation. Despite the successful demonstration, the sign-up sheet for the training remains blank at the end of the day. Nesta feels a sense of failure and struggles with her own internal demons while walking the stairs.

The House of Wind provides Nesta with an old leather-bound manuscript titled The Dance of Battle, which contains Cassian’s scent. Nesta spends the night reading the text and discovers the immense strategic intelligence required for Illyrian warfare and command. She learns that Cassian departs from standard military advice by fighting on the front lines alongside his soldiers. The next afternoon, Nesta enters the library prepared to admit defeat regarding her attempt to recruit the priestesses for training. Clotho points to the sign-up pillar, where Gwyn has finally scrawled her name in bold letters for the next lesson.



### Chapter 25

Gwyn arrives at the training ring for her first lesson and expresses nervousness about the open sky and her lack of proper athletic clothing. Nesta and Cassian guide her through basic grounding exercises, and Gwyn proves to be a lithe and graceful student despite her initial fear. Gwyn explains that her interest in training stems from a desire to never feel powerless again after the trauma she suffered at the temple in Sangravah. Nesta accompanies Cassian to the Illyrian village of Windhaven, where she visits Emerie’s shop while Cassian meets with Eris Vanserra. Inside the shop, Nesta encounters a drunk Illyrian male named Bellius who is bullying Emerie about her inheritance.

Nesta aggressively confronts Bellius, mocking his appearance and demanding he leave the store until he finally staggers out. Emerie reveals that Bellius is her cousin and that her family frequently harasses her to claim the shop and her wealth. Nesta shares a meal with Emerie in the back of the shop, where they discuss their shared experiences with cruel relatives and the loss of their fathers. Emerie mentions that her wings often cause her pain due to old scarring, but she remains determined to run her business independently. Nesta invites Emerie to join the training sessions at the House of Wind, offering to arrange transportation if she decides to participate.

Emerie initially declines the offer to train, citing her responsibilities at the shop and the potential for social backlash in the village. Meanwhile, Cassian meets Eris on the Illyrian Steppes to discuss the Dread Trove and the movements of Queen Briallyn. Eris confirms he has not heard of the Trove and warns that Beron would be a dangerous possessor of such artifacts. They discuss Mor’s frequent trips to Vallahan, with Eris suspecting she is avoiding him while Cassian maintains it is for treaty negotiations. The meeting ends with Eris winnowing away, leaving Cassian alone in the cold to reflect on the growing threat to the Night Court.
