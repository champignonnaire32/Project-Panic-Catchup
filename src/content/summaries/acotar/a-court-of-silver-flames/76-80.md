### Chapter 76

Mor leads Nesta into a silent river house filled with the scent of blood. Feyre is bleeding heavily while the healer Madja explains the baby is wedged in the birth canal. Madja states that an incision to cut the baby out is an enormous risk and has never been successful. Feyre, acting as High Lady, commands Madja to perform the surgery to save the child. Rhys enters Feyre's mind to block the pain as Madja uses knives to perform the procedure.

A tiny, winged male babe is removed from Feyre, but he is silent and unmoving. Rhys begins screaming as Feyre starts to bleed out and die on the bed. Cassian and Azriel restrain a frantic Rhys while Nesta senses the literal presence of Death in the room. Nesta retreats and summons the Dread Trove, consisting of the Mask, the Crown, and the Harp. She wears the Mask and Crown while cradling the Harp, appearing as a non-human being.

Nesta raises a hand to freeze Rhysand in place using the Crown's power. Feyre experiences a death-rattle as her breathing thins and her life fades. Nesta approaches the bed and reaches for the twenty-sixth string of the Harp. The twenty-sixth string represents Time, a fundamental force capable of halting the approach of death. Nesta plucks the final string to stop Time at the exact moment of Feyre's last breath.



### Chapter 77

The Harp freezes Time, allowing Nesta to hold death at bay for Feyre, Rhys, and the baby. A soft female voice, which Nesta has heard before, speaks to her through the Mask. Nesta walks through the frozen room, observing the horrified and grieving faces of her friends. She tells the mysterious voice that she wants to feel everything, including pain, to appreciate the good. Nesta kneels by Feyre and finally tells her sister that she loves her.

She begs the Mother and the Cauldron to show her how to save her family in exchange for her power. Time resumes as Nesta promises to give back what she stole from the Cauldron. Iridescent light flows from Nesta into Feyre, the baby, and Rhysand. The light heals Feyre's internal injuries and transforms her anatomy to that of an Illyrian. The baby, named Nyx, transforms from a stillborn infant into a healthy, full-term boy with dark hair and small Illyrian wings.

A bargain tattoo appears on Nesta's back, signifying her deal with the Cauldron. Feyre wakes up, smiles at Nesta, and tells her that she loves her too. Rhys falls to his knees in front of Nesta to thank her for saving his mate and son. Nesta embraces Rhys, acknowledging him as her brother and part of her family. The Dread Trove objects lie abandoned on the floor as the group celebrates the three lives saved.



### Chapter 78

Gwyn and Emerie wait in the river house parlor after being healed by the stone at the Rite's summit. Nesta joins them on the couch, and they celebrate their collective victory in the Blood Rite. Mor returns the objects of the Dread Trove to their hiding places using winnowing. Emerie explains that the stone transported them to the river house because they were needed there. The three friends agree to keep the specific details of the Rite a secret among themselves.

Cassian visits the nursery and marvels at the perfect, delicate wings of the baby boy, Nyx. Nesta finds Cassian on the lawn and confirms that most of her stolen Cauldron power is gone. She reveals that she changed her own anatomy to match Feyre's so she can safely carry winged children. Nesta clarifies that she is not ready for a baby yet and will continue using contraceptive tea. Cassian tells Nesta she could have ruled the world, but she replies that she only wants him.

Nesta requests a disgustingly ornate mating ceremony to celebrate their bond. She offers Cassian a stale biscuit as a symbolic ritual to seal their mating. Cassian laughs and promises that their ceremony will be like a coronation. They discuss the remaining threats, including Koschei and the human queens, but decide to wait. Nesta tells Cassian she loves him before they share a kiss on the riverbank.



### Chapter 79

Two days after the birth, Cassian visits Eris Vanserra in his suite in the Hewn City. Eris is reading by a fire and appears immaculate despite his recent kidnapping by Briallyn. Cassian informs Eris that Rhysand already searched his mind and knows Eris had little information. Eris admits that his father, Beron, tortured him for information regarding his disappearance. Eris lied to Beron, claiming Cassian and the Night Court were disgusted to have to rescue him.

Cassian realizes Eris is suffering from injuries hidden beneath his expensive clothes. He asks Eris why he left Mor in the woods years ago if he truly hates his father. Eris refuses to give a direct answer, stating Cassian is not the person he needs to explain himself to. Eris warns Cassian that Beron is furious and may seek an alliance with the death-lord Koschei. He notes that Mor is currently in Vallahan trying to counteract Beron's potential political damage.

Cassian feels pity for Eris, seeing him as a male born into wealth but destitute of love. He tells Eris that he grew up with monsters and knows that Eris is not one of them. Cassian asserts that Eris is actually a decent male trapped in a terrible situation. He concludes by calling Eris a coward for being too afraid to act on his good nature. Cassian leaves the room to return to his life with Nesta in Velaris.



### Chapter 80

Spring fully blooms in Velaris as Feyre and Nyx begin taking daily walks through the city. Nesta continues her morning training with the priestesses and Valkyries to correct mistakes made during the Rite. Rhysand gives Nesta and Cassian the House of Wind as their personal home. Feyre gives Nesta a mating present: a portrait of Nesta holding the line at the Pass of Enalius. Nesta, Elain, and Feyre walk to a grassy hill outside the city where their father is buried.

The sisters visit the grave marker to show the baby, Nyx, to his late grandfather. Feyre and Elain speak softly to the stone before giving Nesta a moment alone. Nesta stands by the gravestone and realizes she no longer needs to beg for forgiveness. She acknowledges that her father died for her with love in his heart. Nesta places a small, carved wooden rose on the grave as a permanent marker.

She thanks her father for his sacrifice and presses a kiss to the stone. Cassian flies overhead, watching over the sisters as they finish their visit. Nesta blows a kiss to Cassian and feels a radiant sun of love in her chest. She joins her sisters and the sleeping baby halfway down the hill. The story concludes with the three Archeron sisters walking back toward the city together.
