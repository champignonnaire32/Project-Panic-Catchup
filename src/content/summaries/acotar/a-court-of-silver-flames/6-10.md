### Chapter 6

Nesta watches Cassian train at Windhaven before Morrigan arrives to criticize her lack of participation. Morrigan expresses her desire to have sent Nesta back to human lands and references her own trauma in the Court of Nightmares. Cassian returns to the House of Wind and sends a note ordering Nesta to report to the subterranean library. Nesta meets the high priestess Clotho, whose hands are gnarled and whose tongue has been removed by past abusers. Clotho assigns Nesta to shelve books on the third level for a five-hour shift.

Nesta works in silence while avoiding memories of her transformation in the Cauldron and her sister Elain's suffering. She observes other priestesses in pale robes who have found sanctuary in the library gifted by Rhysand. When the shift ends at six o'clock, Clotho uses a magically animated fountain pen to write notes to Nesta. The priestess advises Nesta to wear more practical clothing for the dusty work and mentions that Feyre spoke highly of her. Nesta rejects the compliment and leaves the library for a solitary dinner.

The House of Wind provides Nesta with a meal of roast chicken, potatoes, and green beans in the empty dining room. Nesta demands wine from the enchanted House, but it fills her glass with water instead. She realizes the House is intentionally restricting her access to alcohol under orders. Frustrated by the high-handed interference in her life, she decides to find her own wine elsewhere. She exits the dining room with the intent to bypass the House's restrictions.



### Chapter 7

Mor winnows Cassian to the Band of Exiles' estate where he meets Lucien and learns Eris is visiting. Cassian suppresses his rage toward Eris, who left Morrigan for dead centuries ago at the Autumn Court border. Inside the parlor, Cassian encounters Jurian and Vassa, the human queen who transforms into a firebird by day. Eris reports that dozens of his soldiers vanished during patrol and his magical smokehounds detected odd human scents at the site. The group discusses the possibility of the human queen Briallyn causing the disappearances with the help of a powerful ally.

Vassa identifies the sorcerer-lord Koschei as the one likely pulling Briallyn's strings from his prison at a remote lake. Koschei is the elder brother of the Bone Carver and a death-lord who seeks to escape his ancient confinement. Eris suggests that the kidnappings are intended to sow suspicion among the Fae territories and weaken their alliance. Cassian's anger flares when he realizes Briallyn might target Nesta to provoke the Night Court. Lucien uses his power to settle Cassian's temper before the meeting concludes and they move outside.

Eris confronts Cassian outside and reveals that his father, Beron, has already pledged Autumn Court forces to Briallyn. Eris admits the missing soldiers were part of the unit that accompanied Beron to Briallyn's palace. He explains that he wants to investigate the alliance independently to protect his own interests within the Autumn Court. Eris demands to be kept informed of whatever Azriel discovers during his reconnaissance of Briallyn and the other queens. Before winnowing away, Eris mocks Cassian's lack of courtly skill and warns him to stick to fighting.



### Chapter 8

Nesta attempts to leave the House of Wind by descending the ten thousand spiral steps leading to the city. She suffers from extreme dizziness and physical exhaustion as she counts over a hundred individual steps. The memory of her father's neck being snapped by the King of Hybern haunts her during the attempt. Her legs buckle and she is forced to crawl back up the stairs, arriving at the top covered in sweat. Cassian is waiting for her at the landing and mocks her failure to reach the bottom.

Cassian recounts how he and his brothers once puked on each other while climbing the stairs as punishment. He hauls Nesta up from the floor and challenges her to attend training to fix her physical weakness. The next morning, Nesta struggles to move due to severe muscle soreness but hides her pain at breakfast. She meets Azriel, the Night Court's shadowsinger, who possesses scarred hands and shadows that flow like smoke. Nesta attempts to provoke Cassian by suggesting she would rather train with Azriel instead of him.

Nesta forces herself to walk to the veranda without showing her physical discomfort to the two males. Azriel prepares to leave for his mission to track Briallyn while Cassian prepares for an Illyrian war-band inspection. At the training ground, Nesta refuses to participate and sits on a rock until she turns blue from the cold. Cassian eventually moves her inside Rhysand's mother's house, which was the first true home he ever owned. Nesta stares into the fire in a trance until Cassian leaves her alone to visit the village.



### Chapter 9

Nesta enters Emerie’s shop in the Illyrian village and requests warmer clothing for her time at the training camp. Emerie reveals that her wings were clipped by her traditionalist father and that she cannot fly. Nesta learns that Emerie’s shop sends bills directly to the Night Court, but Nesta hesitates because she is cut off from funds. Emerie recognizes Nesta as the one who killed the King of Hybern and expresses her blunt approval. The two females share a moment of mutual respect before Nesta returns to the subterranean library.

Clotho assigns Nesta to shelve books on the fifth level, deep within the dimness of the lower library. Nesta meets Gwyneth Berdara, a young acolyte with copper hair and teal eyes who works for a demanding scholar. Gwyn challenges Nesta's unpleasant attitude and refuses to be coddled or pitied for her past trauma. The two argue over book shelving duties, but Nesta eventually concedes and finishes the work Gwyn requested. Clotho later encourages Nesta to give Gwyn the benefit of the doubt regarding her abrupt behavior.

The House of Wind provides Nesta with pork-and-bean soup and double-chocolate cake in the private library. Nesta realizes the House is sentient and responds to her polite requests with helpful actions. She enjoys the meal while watching the sunset over the city and the gold band of the Sidra river. The enchanted House dims the lights and offers a fire, which Nesta initially refuses before settling into the quiet space. She acknowledges the House’s assistance and finishes her meal in the first moment of peace she has found.



### Chapter 10

Cassian enters the library and eats some of Nesta's chocolate cake while teasing her about talking to the House. He offers to teach her how to use a fork as a weapon if she agrees to participate in lessons. Nesta later falls asleep in her room after the House heats her bed and closes the drapes for her. She experiences a nightmare about Elain's kidnapping and her father's death before waking up in a cold sweat. Driven by the need for a drink, she attempts to descend the ten thousand steps in the middle of the night.

Nesta slips on the stairs during a flashback of the Cauldron and tumbles down thirty steps, injuring her face and body. She catches herself by digging her fingers into the stone, which glows with an inner light and leaves permanent furrows. Terrified by this involuntary display of power, she flees back up the stairs and hides in her room. The next morning, she arrives at training with a black eye and a cut chin from the fall. Cassian reveals that he knows about her midnight tumble but chose not to intervene because she stopped falling.

Cassian goes through his sword routines while males from the camp watch him with a mix of awe and mockery. He offers his hand to Nesta and pleads with her to join the training session instead of sitting on the rock. Nesta remembers her initial attraction to Cassian's arrogant masculinity and her desire to test his strength against her own. She feels she deserves his disappointment and refuses to stand, even though she knows it hurts their connection. Cassian lowers his hand and tells her they will try again tomorrow before ignoring her for the day.
