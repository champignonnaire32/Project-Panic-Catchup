### Chapter 51

Nesta, Emerie, and Gwyn stand in the training ring two days after returning from the mountains. Gwyn ties a white silk ribbon to a wooden beam and declares it a Valkyrie test to cut it in half. Cassian explains that slicing the ribbon with one cut signifies readiness for battle. Emerie attempts the cut first but the blade fails to slice the silk. Cassian demonstrates by successfully cutting the ribbon with a single swipe of his sword.

Back at the House of Wind, Nesta and Cassian have sex on his desk while still partially clothed. Nesta continues to avoid visiting Feyre in Velaris despite her recent internal breakthroughs. She attempts to walk down the ten thousand steps of the House of Wind daily but always turns back halfway. The training sessions shift to focus on using heavy shields as weapons. Gwyn asks Nesta to attend an evening priestess service involving music.

Nesta confesses to Gwyn that she used alcohol and strangers to punish herself after the war. She explains that her love for music was the only reason she frequented taverns and pleasure halls. Gwyn expresses understanding and invites Nesta to the service in the underground cavern. The two females hold hands and share a moment of mutual vulnerability regarding their pasts. Nesta agrees to attend the service to hear music for the first time in over a year.



### Chapter 52

Nesta attends the sunset service in a massive red-stone cavern filled with hundreds of priestesses. Merrill leads the prayers from a stone podium while Gwyn joins the singers on the dais. The service features ancient songs accompanied by a drum, a harp, and a lute. Gwyn performs a mezzo-soprano solo that resonates through the acoustically perfect stone chamber. Nesta closes her eyes and allows the music to pull her into a deep trance.

During the trance, Nesta's consciousness travels to a mist-veiled mountain with two peaks and ancient carved symbols. She moves through the mountain's gates and follows a path past sealed doors holding terrible beings. Her soul reaches a black-stone chamber etched with spells and wards where a golden harp sits. The Harp speaks to her mind, inviting her to play and promising to unlock pathways through space and time. Nesta reaches for the strings before the music stops and the vision shatters.

Nesta collapses onto her pew as she realizes she has scried the location of the Harp. She informs Cassian that the Trove item is hidden beneath the Prison. Cassian flies to Velaris to report the discovery to Rhysand, who is researching ways to save Feyre. Rhysand explains that he will lower the magical shields around the Prison the following day. He gives Cassian the great sword that Nesta Made and instructs him to take it on the mission.



### Chapter 53

Cassian and Nesta hike up the mountain to the Prison, which is a barren island surrounded by gray seas. They enter through gates made of carved bone and descend into the lightless tunnels using a Siphon for light. Nesta identifies a specific iron door marked with a single rune as the entrance to the hidden chamber. She passes her hand through solid rock to reveal a secret hallway leading to the Harp's room. The golden Harp sits in the center of a circular room etched with constellations and an eight-pointed star.

Nesta enters the ward-heavy chamber alone to retrieve the Harp while Cassian watches from the archway. When she touches the instrument, she experiences a vision of Fae screaming as they are fused into stone walls. Her mind connects to Queen Briallyn, who is wearing the Crown in a white-stone palace. Briallyn threatens Nesta's friends before the vision shifts back to the dark Prison chamber. Nesta finds herself pinned to the floor by the Harp's oppressive magical weight.

Nesta plucks the first string of the Harp to release the wards and free herself from the pressure. She tells Cassian that Briallyn saw her through the connection and likely knows their location. They retreat from the hidden chamber and Nesta names her Made sword Ataraxia, meaning "inner peace." As they reach the main path of the Prison, they discover that the door to Lanthys's cell is open. Cassian pushes Nesta behind him and orders her to run for the exit.



### Chapter 54

Cassian confronts the mist-form of Lanthys while ordering Nesta to flee the Prison. Lanthys reveals that the Harp's power opened his cell door when Nesta released the wards. Nesta runs toward the bone gates but realizes she cannot leave Cassian behind to die. She discovers that plucking the first string allows her to transplant herself across dimensions. Ten Autumn Court soldiers appear in the Prison, sent by Briallyn to seize the Harp.

Nesta plucks the string and teleports back to Cassian's side just as Lanthys throws him against a wall. She draws Ataraxia and the blade begins to glow with silver fire, intimidating the monster. Lanthys takes a solid human form as a golden-haired male and attempts to seduce Nesta with a vision of a joint kingdom. He shows her a future where they rule together using the Trove and resurrect the Wild Hunt. Nesta rejects the vision when she sees Lanthys's greed and realizes he intends to enslave others.

Cassian stabs Lanthys with a dagger while the monster is distracted by Nesta's glowing sword. Nesta uses Ataraxia to behead Lanthys, killing the immortal being instantly. The other prisoners in the mountain begin screaming in terror as they sense Lanthys's death. Nesta plucks three strings on the Harp to teleport herself and the injured Cassian to Feyre’s river house. They land on the front lawn in Velaris where Rhysand rushes out to assist the collapsing Cassian.



### Chapter 55

Madja heals Cassian's cracked skull and broken arm while Nesta returns the Harp and Ataraxia to Rhysand. Nesta uses mind-speak to apologize to Feyre for her past behavior, and Feyre offers her forgiveness. Rhysand explains the history of the Daglan, ancient near-god monsters who once ruled and enslaved the Fae. He recounts the legend of Fionn, the first High King, who used a Made sword to overthrow the Daglan. Nesta describes a fourth object she saw in the Harp's vision, which appeared to be made of ancient bone.

Rhysand proposes that Nesta seduce Eris at the upcoming Winter Solstice ball to secure the Autumn Court alliance. Cassian objects to the plan, but Nesta agrees to the task and requests dancing lessons from Morrigan. Nesta begins practicing Mind-Stilling exercises for ten minutes twice a day to maintain her mental focus. She later asks Cassian to accompany her to the ruins of her family's old cottage. They find the building in severe disrepair with a rotting roof and overgrown weeds.

Nesta enters the cottage and views the painted dresser where Feyre had depicted stars, flowers, and fire. She collects a wood-carved rose that her father had made for Elain during a harsh winter. Nesta confesses to Cassian that she previously hated her father for his perceived weakness and failure to protect them. She acknowledges her own failures in letting Feyre hunt in the woods alone. After leaving the cottage, Cassian takes Nesta flying over the human lands to end their visit.
