### Chapter 11

The House of Wind locks Nesta out of the private library after she ignores the food it provided for her. Nesta joins Cassian and Azriel for dinner and overhears them discussing Vassa and the threat of the human queens. Azriel questions Nesta about her fading black eye and why she refuses to begin her physical training. Nesta insults Rhysand, calling him an arrogant asshole, which causes Azriel and Cassian to react with visible anger. Cassian tells Nesta that everyone hates her and declares that he is finished trying to help her.

Nesta leaves the dining room and returns to her repetitive task of shelving books in the library. Cassian sits on the roof of the House of Wind and looks down at the empty training ring. Feyre winnows to the roof and speaks with Cassian about Rhysand’s magical shield that protects her. They discuss the historical tension between Feyre and Nesta, which began when their mother prioritized Nesta over the other sisters. Cassian realizes that Nesta’s refusal to train at Windhaven is rooted in her fear of appearing vulnerable in front of Illyrian warriors.

Feyre confirms that Nesta does not actually hate Cassian despite her frequent verbal attacks. Cassian notes that Rhysand has shielded Feyre’s scent and physical body so thoroughly that she is untouchable. The group worries about the intentions of the human queens and the potential for a renewed war. Cassian decides to change his approach to Nesta's training to accommodate her pride and need for privacy. He resolves to offer her one final chance to begin the training program at the House of Wind.



### Chapter 12

Nesta arrives at the training ring atop the House of Wind and finds that Cassian has cancelled the trip to Windhaven. Cassian explains that they will train privately because the other members of the Inner Circle are too busy to winnow them. He proposes a formal Fae bargain where Nesta must train for one hour in exchange for a favor of her choosing. Nesta agrees and the magical contract manifests as an eight-pointed star tattoo on both of their spines. Cassian removes his shirt to check for the mark, revealing his heavily muscled torso and numerous combat scars.

Nesta focuses on Cassian’s physical appearance as he begins the lesson with basic balance and foot placement. He explains that she must learn to grip the ground with her toes before she is allowed to handle a sword. Nesta practices squats and hinges at the hip while Cassian corrects her posture and tells her not to hunch. They transition to breathing exercises, and Nesta discovers that exhaling during a thrust significantly increases her physical power. The session lasts for two hours as Nesta realizes the breathing techniques help silence the constant mental noise in her head.

During a cooldown stretch, Nesta asks Cassian why he loves to fly and he describes it as a form of freedom. They discuss the Illyrian tradition of clipping female wings, and Cassian identifies the shop owner Emerie as a victim of the practice. Nesta admits that the training makes her mind feel clear for the first time in months. Cassian gives Nesta two glasses of water and tells her to drink them immediately to prevent dehydration. Before she leaves, Cassian retracts his earlier statement and tells Nesta that he does not actually hate her.



### Chapter 13

Nesta experiences extreme muscle soreness while shelving books and struggles to lift the heavy tomes in the library. She meets Gwyneth, a copper-haired priestess who works as a researcher for a demanding High Fae female named Merrill. Gwyn explains that she accidentally gave Merrill the wrong volume of a history book and fears she will be severely punished. Nesta asks the House of Wind for help, and a gray leather-bound book titled The Great War appears on her cart. She goes to the second level of the library and enters Merrill’s office to perform a secret book swap.

Merrill is a stunningly beautiful female with white hair and twilight-colored eyes who is currently obsessed with multi-dimensional research. Nesta distracts Merrill by pretending to be a confused delivery girl while she searches the shelves for the incorrect volume. She successfully swaps volume seven for volume eight and leaves the office before Merrill discovers the deception. Nesta finds Gwyn in the first-level reading area and returns the correct book to her. Gwyn reveals that Merrill is writing a history of the Valkyries, a lost clan of elite female warriors from every Fae race.

The Valkyries were highly honored fighters who were mostly wiped out during the war five hundred years ago. Gwyn explains that the few survivors of the Valkyrie clan died from the shame of losing their sisters in battle. Nesta returns to her bedroom and finds a hot meal and a smutty romance novel provided by the House. She soaks in a hot bath scented with rosemary and lavender to soothe her aching muscles. A tray with a large piece of chocolate cake appears over the tub as a reward for her kindness toward Gwyn.



### Chapter 14

Cassian checks on Nesta in the morning and discovers she is still asleep and partially naked in her bed. Nesta eventually arrives at the training ring, grimacing in pain as she climbs the stairs due to intense muscle soreness. Cassian forces her to perform two hours of stretches and exercises to remove the lactic acid from her leg muscles. He helps her stretch her hamstrings by lifting her legs while she lies on a black mat. They discuss the attack on Sangravah, where Hybern soldiers slaughtered priestesses to steal a piece of the Cauldron.

Rhysand and Cassian travel to a blooming apple orchard in the Spring Court to meet with Eris Vanserra, the Autumn Court heir. Eris suggests that the Night Court should assassinate the human queens to prevent his father, Beron, from forming an alliance. Rhysand refuses the request, stating that killing the queens would trigger a catastrophic war among the Fae. Eris mentions that Tamlin remains in his beast form and has allowed his manor to fall into complete disrepair. Cassian notices that Rhysand is unusually distracted and distant during the return trip to the House of Wind.

Nesta returns to the library and attempts to climb the stairs again, reaching step one hundred fifty before turning back. The House of Wind provides Nesta with a second romance novel and a bath filled with therapeutic oils. Nesta tells the House that it is her only friend as she enjoys the chocolate cake and the warm water. She spends her evening reading in bed and successfully avoids having any nightmares. A familiar scent fills her room during the night, but she is too sleepy to identify the visitor.



### Chapter 15

Nesta stands at the railing of the sixth level of the library and peers into the unnatural darkness of the lowest levels. She feels the darkness pulsing like a living womb and hears her name being whispered from the depths of the pit. Gwyn rescues Nesta from the ledge and uses a glowing blue Invoking Stone, which channels the Mother’s power, for protection. They retreat to the fourth level, where Gwyn explains that the stone is similar to an Illyrian Siphon. Gwyn warns Nesta that something old and curious remains in the library's depths and is specifically drawn to her power.

During the next morning's training, Cassian tells Nesta about his birth in a tent during a freezing winter. He describes how his mother was shunned and forced into backbreaking labor until she died alone while he was at Windhaven. Cassian explains that physical training was the only thing that provided him with a sense of center during his traumatic upbringing. He recounts his history of capturing ancient monsters like Lanthys, a primordial being who could turn into wind or rain. He also mentions Blue Annis, a creature with cobalt skin and iron claws that nearly shredded his chest during her capture.

Nesta and Cassian have a tense dinner where they discuss the possibility that the library darkness reacted to her Made powers. Cassian goads Nesta into an argument until she corners him against a wall in the hallway. He tells her that her eyes glow with silver fire when her power rises and that he finds her beautiful. Cassian describes how he has wanted her since she was human and inhales her scent while leaning close to her neck. He ends the encounter by telling Nesta that he will be thinking about the look on her face for the rest of the night.
