### Chapter 31

Feyre returns to her family home and tries to adapt to a life of luxury and parties. During a lunch conversation, her father mentions that the Beddor family was murdered and their house burned down. Feyre realizes that the faerie she gave the name "Clare Beddor" to has likely targeted them. She warns Nesta and Elain to flee the region if they see any signs of danger. Nesta encourages Feyre to return to Prythian to help Tamlin despite the risks. Feyre gathers her hunting gear and rides north toward the wall that separates the mortal and faerie realms.

Feyre discovers an invisible magical barrier in the forest and follows it until she finds a gate. She passes through the wall and notices that the Spring Court forest is eerily silent. Upon reaching Tamlin’s manor, she finds the gates wrenched open and the front doors hanging off their hinges. The interior of the house is trashed with shredded tapestries and shattered crystal chandeliers. Feyre searches the empty rooms and calls out for Tamlin and Lucien but receives no response. She sinks to her knees in the wreckage of the entry hall when she realizes everyone is gone.

Feyre examines the blood patterns and footprints in the manor to determine what happened during the attack. She follows the tracks of Tamlin and Lucien from the dining room toward the garden. While hiding behind a door, Feyre encounters Alis, a servant with tree-bark skin and a permanent mask. Alis reveals that Tamlin and Lucien are alive but have been taken Under the Mountain. This location is a subterranean palace beneath a sacred peak in the heart of Prythian. Feyre demands to know why they were taken and who is responsible for the destruction.



### Chapter 32

Alis explains that Amarantha, a powerful faerie general from the kingdom of Hybern, has claimed the title of High Queen of Prythian. Amarantha spent fifty years infiltrating the courts before using a magical potion to steal the powers of the seven High Lords. She established her rule Under the Mountain forty-nine years ago and has kept the land in a state of decay. Alis reveals that there was never a "blight" on the land, only Amarantha’s intentional destruction of the borders. Tamlin was cursed because he refused to become Amarantha's lover and insulted her during a masquerade ball. Amarantha bound masks to the faces of everyone in the Spring Court as part of this punishment.

The curse required Tamlin to find a human girl with hatred for faeries who would fall in love with him. This girl had to kill one of his sentries in an unprovoked attack to begin the process. Tamlin sent his men across the wall disguised as wolves for decades, hoping one would be killed by such a girl. Andras was the final sentry sacrificed, and Feyre's act of killing him triggered the final countdown of the curse. The curse would have been broken if Feyre had told Tamlin she loved him before she left. Because she failed to do so, Amarantha arrived to seize Tamlin and his court as soon as the time expired.

Alis describes how Amarantha killed the families of rebellious High Lords and keeps the remaining rulers as her prisoners. She notes that Amarantha is building an army to eventually invade the human realm. Feyre realizes that Tamlin let her go to protect her from Amarantha's wrath even though it meant his own enslavement. She demands that Alis show her the way to the palace Under the Mountain so she can attempt to free him. Alis warns that no human survives the journey, but Feyre insists she must try to fix what she broke. Alis eventually agrees to lead Feyre to a secret entrance that leads into the mountain.



### Chapter 33

Feyre prepares for her journey by arming herself with a bow, a full quiver of arrows, and two daggers. Alis leads her through silent woods and steep hills as the sun sets and night falls. They arrive at a slender cave mouth that Alis describes as an ancient, sacred shortcut into the mountain. Alis warns Feyre not to drink the wine, not to make deals, and to trust no one once inside. Feyre offers Alis and her nephews refuge at her family home if they need to flee across the wall. Alis bids her stay alive and watches as Feyre enters the freezing darkness of the cave.

Feyre moves through the cave by keeping her hand on the wet stone wall to avoid falling into pits. She eventually sees orange light and hears a cacophony of hissing and guttural faerie voices. She finds a jagged fissure in the rock that opens into a fire-lit subterranean passageway. Feyre squeezes through the narrow opening and presses herself into an alcove to avoid being seen. The hallway is carved from pale stone and lined with torches, offering very little cover for a human. She decides to move forward quickly before she loses her nerve to face the Faerie Queen.

As Feyre attempts to turn a corner, she is suddenly grabbed by long, bony fingers on her arm. A leathery gray face with silver fangs and bat-like ears looms over her. Feyre recognizes the creature as the Attor, a winged, predatory faerie that previously harassed her at the manor. The Attor smiles at her and asks what a human is doing in the catacombs. Feyre stands rigid with fear as the creature's grip tightens on her upper arm. She realizes she has been captured before she could even begin her search for Tamlin.



### Chapter 34

The Attor drags Feyre into a massive throne room filled with pillars depicting the history of Prythian. Feyre sees a crowd of High Fae and lesser faeries dancing to dissonant music under jeweled chandeliers. The Attor throws Feyre onto the red marble floor in front of a dais holding two thrones. Amarantha sits on a black throne wearing a golden crown and a necklace featuring a human finger bone. Tamlin sits beside her in a second throne, wearing his golden mask and looking completely impassive. Feyre stands and publicly claims that she has come to claim the one she loves, referring to Tamlin.

Amarantha mocks Feyre and reveals that she has been torturing a human girl to keep Feyre's identity hidden. Feyre looks at the wall and sees the mangled corpse of Clare Beddor nailed high above the court. Amarantha explains that Rhysand gave her Clare's name, and she used the girl for amusement until she died. Feyre realizes her own lie resulted in Clare’s brutal death and feels a surge of guilt. Amarantha points to her ring, which contains a swiveling human eye belonging to the mortal warrior Jurian. She offers Feyre a bargain: complete three tasks over three months to win Tamlin’s freedom.

Feyre agrees to the bargain but adds a condition that Tamlin’s curse must be broken and his court freed. Amarantha also offers a riddle that, if solved, will immediately free everyone without the need for tasks. If Feyre fails any task or answers the riddle incorrectly, she will be killed. Feyre looks at Tamlin, who subtly widens his eyes to signal his continued love for her. She officially accepts the deal, and Amarantha orders the court to give her a "greeting." Several faeries repeatedly punch Feyre until she loses consciousness from the brutal beating.



### Chapter 35

Feyre wakes up in a damp, moldy prison cell with a broken nose and a swollen face. She hears the sounds of someone being whipped and screaming in a nearby part of the dungeon. Lucien sneaks into her cell during a guard rotation to check on her injuries. He criticizes her for being foolish enough to come to the mountain after Tamlin sacrificed everything to save her. Lucien uses a fraction of his remaining magic to set Feyre’s nose and heal the swelling on her face. He explains that Jurian’s soul is trapped in the eye on Amarantha’s ring as an eternal punishment.

Lucien warns Feyre that all the High Lords have been summoned to watch her trials and are forbidden to leave. He flees the cell just as the guards return for their next rotation. Feyre spends several days in isolation, eating stale bread and water while trying to solve Amarantha’s riddle. She worries that the riddle is a trap and that her human intellect is insufficient to solve it. Guards eventually return and drag her to the throne room for the arrival of the full moon. Feyre notices the Spring Court faeries in the crowd and hopes to find allies among them.

Amarantha summons Rhysand and asks him to confirm Feyre's identity and her relationship with Tamlin. Rhysand claims that humans all look alike to him and refuses to provide any useful information. Amarantha then forces Lucien forward and orders Rhysand to hold his mind so his brothers can watch. Feyre screams her name to prevent Rhysand from destroying Lucien’s mind during the interrogation. Amarantha recites a riddle about something that kills slowly and is soft-handed but difficult to defeat. Feyre is unable to solve the riddle and is dragged back to her cell to await her first task.
