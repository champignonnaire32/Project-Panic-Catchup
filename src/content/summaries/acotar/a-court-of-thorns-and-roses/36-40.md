### Chapter 36
Feyre is taken to an arena where Amarantha presents the first task: hunting a giant Middengard Worm, a pinkish-brown mouth-filled invertebrate, in a mud labyrinth. The Attor drops Feyre into the trenches, and she begins running as the blind creature pursues her by scent. She discovers the worm is blind and finds a pit filled with bones where she hides briefly. Feyre realizes she is trapped in the pit but finds heavy bones to use as a ladder. She climbs out and prepares a trap using sharp bone spikes in the muddy floor of the pit.

Feyre covers her entire body in reeking mud to mask her scent and become invisible to the worm. Rhysand explains the trap to the crowd as Feyre cuts her palm to lure the creature with the smell of blood. Lucien shouts a warning as the worm bursts through a wall, and Feyre leads it on a high-speed chase through the labyrinth. She uses bone rails embedded in corners to swing around sharp turns without losing momentum. Feyre leaps into the pit at the last second, and the worm plummets after her.

The Middengard Worm is impaled on the bone spikes and dies instantly. Feyre climbs out of the pit and hurls a bone at Amarantha, splattering her dress with filth. Amarantha reveals that only one person in her court bet on Feyre's victory. Feyre notices a bone shard protruding through her own left forearm as the guards drag her away. She is returned to her cell in extreme pain, unable to find Lucien or Tamlin before losing consciousness.



### Chapter 37
Feyre suffers for days in her freezing cell with a bone shard still embedded in her festering arm. She experiences a high fever and nausea, unable to eat the rotten food provided by her captors. Rhysand, a High Lord with violet eyes and wings, appears in the shadows and mocks her weak condition. He forces her to show him the wound and describes it as wonderfully gruesome while she wheezes in agony. Rhysand offers to heal her arm in exchange for her living at the Night Court for one week every month.

Feyre initially refuses the bargain, but Rhysand explains that Lucien was punished with twenty lashes and cannot help her. He tells her the infection is terminal and she is dying without magical intervention. To demonstrate his power, he twists the bone shard in her arm, causing her to scream and thrash. Feyre realizes she must choose between death and the bargain to save Tamlin and his people. She negotiates the term down to one week per month for the rest of her life.

The bargain is sealed with magic, and Feyre's arm is instantly healed and cleaned of mud. A permanent black ink tattoo appears on her left arm, featuring swirls, whorls, and a feline eye in the center of her palm. Rhysand admits he did not tell her about the mark and suggests Tamlin will react poorly to it. He explains the mark is custom in the Night Court to signify a binding agreement. Rhysand vanishes into shadow, leaving Feyre to contemplate the trap she has fallen into to survive.



### Chapter 38
Guards force Feyre to scrub a massive marble hallway with a bucket of filthy water and a coarse brush. She struggles with the impossible task as the water leaves a trail of brown muck across the tiles. The Lady of the Autumn Court, a High Fae with red hair and russet eyes, appears and magically cleans the water in Feyre's bucket. This act of assistance pays a debt for Feyre giving Amarantha her own name instead of Lucien's during the trial. Feyre completes the cleaning task to the guards' disappointment, avoiding the threat of being roasted on a spit.

The next day, guards lock Feyre in a dark bedroom and command her to sort lentils from a fireplace full of ash. She spends two hours picking through the soot before Rhysand enters and reveals the room belongs to him. He explains that Amarantha is playing games and testing his loyalty because he bet on Feyre. Rhysand demonstrates his remnant powers by momentarily shifting his fingers and feet into sharp, black talons. He then displays massive black membranous wings that sprout from his back, which he compares to a bat's.

Feyre asks if the High Lords can help her solve Amarantha's riddle, but Rhysand states they are magically bound to obey her orders. He uses a snap of his fingers to instantly clean the soot from Feyre's skin and the ash from the fireplace. The bucket is magically filled with sorted lentils, and the guards are summoned to take Feyre away. Rhysand uses mind control to order the guards to never touch her or enter her cell again. He warns the guards they will be forced to gut themselves with their own daggers if they disobey his command.



### Chapter 39
Feyre receives fresh meals in her cell but remains isolated, pondering Amarantha's riddle while the sounds of torture echo through the dungeons. Two shadow-shrouded Fae females from the Night Court, appearing as features made of shadow in cobweb gowns, enter her cell. They bathe her roughly and paint intricate blue-black designs over her entire body to match her bargain tattoo. They dress her in a translucent white gossamer shift that leaves her breasts and backside mostly exposed. Rhysand arrives and explains that the paint will reveal if anyone else, especially Tamlin, touches her during the party.

Rhysand escorts Feyre to a Midsummer party in the throne room where she is paraded before the gawking crowd. He reveals the terms of their bargain to Tamlin and Amarantha, showing off the tattoo on Feyre's arm. Feyre is forced to drink faerie wine that drugs her into a state of semi-consciousness and compliance. She awakens in her cell the next morning, sick from the wine and covered in smudged paint from the night's events. Lucien visits her and reveals she was forced to dance for Rhysand and sit in his lap in front of everyone.

Lucien explains he couldn't help her sooner because Amarantha magically prevented his back from healing after his lashes. Feyre continues to be drugged and forced to attend nightly parties as Rhysand's plaything for several days. On the night before the second trial, Rhysand brings Feyre to the throne room where a Summer Court faerie is being interrogated. Amarantha orders Rhysand to shatter the male's mind for attempting to escape to human territory. Rhysand instead uses his power to kill the male instantly, which Feyre recognizes as a secret act of mercy.



### Chapter 40
Feyre stands before Amarantha in a gilded cavern to begin the second trial as Tamlin watches from behind the queen's chair. The floor sinks into a pit, separating Feyre from Lucien, who is chained to the floor on the opposite side of a grate. Amarantha explains that Feyre must answer a riddle by pulling one of three stone levers while spiked grates descend from the ceiling. Feyre discovers a long inscription on the wall but realizes she cannot read any of the words. The heat from the glowing iron spikes smothers the chamber as the grates get closer to crushing both Feyre and Lucien.

Lucien screams for Feyre to answer as she panics, staring at the indecipherable letters and the levers marked I, II, and III. She tries to guess and reaches for the second lever, but the tattoo on her palm pulses with paralyzing pain. She realizes Rhysand is using the tattoo to communicate and guide her hands through the trial. Feyre follows the pulses of pain and reaches for the third lever, which causes the sensation to stop. She pulls the third lever just inches before the spikes skewer her head, causing the machinery to halt and lift.

The platform returns Feyre to the surface where she collapses in shock from the near-death experience. Rhysand uses a mental link to command her to stand up and show no weakness or tears to Amarantha. He tells her exactly how to walk away with her chin high as she is escorted back to her cell. Once alone, Feyre shatters and weeps for hours until Rhysand appears and licks the tears from her face to stop her crying. He mocks her illiteracy but promises to teach her to read during her weeks at the Night Court.
