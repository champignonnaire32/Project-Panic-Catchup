### Chapter 36

Elend and Cett ride through the camp to maintain visibility and discuss the siege of Fadrex. Cett criticizes Elend for entering the city and meeting the people, claiming his compassion is a weakness. Elend maintains that he is glad for his choice despite Cett's warnings about the psychological burden of attacking friends. Cett suggests poisoning the city's wells to force Yomen into depleting his water reserves. Elend agrees but insists on leaving messages to warn the populace, aiming to cause worry rather than mass death.

An earthquake strikes the camp, a phenomenon Elend notes is becoming increasingly frequent and unusual for the Final Empire's inner dominances. Elend and Cett retreat to the command tent where they discuss the lack of news and supplies from Luthadel. Cett worries about his daughter Allrianne, who is with Sazed and Breeze, though he tries to mask his concern with arrogance. General Demoux returns to duty after recovering from the mist sickness, appearing pale and scarred. Ham reports that the rest of the army is nearly back to full strength as the sick soldiers recover.

Demoux expresses a desire to resign his post, believing himself unworthy after falling ill to the mists. He explains a rumor that the mists struck sixteen percent of the army and that survivors remained sick for exactly sixteen days. Demoux interprets this as a sign of displeasure from the Survivor, whom he believes is Lord of the Mists. Elend argues that the numbers are coincidences and that Demoux’s faith remains proven by his devotion. The conversation is interrupted by shouting in the distance as an attack begins.



### Chapter 37

Vin awakens to screams and clashing metal as Yomen’s forces launch a surprise attack on the northern section of the camp. She flares pewter and steel to launch herself into the air, observing cavalry riding toward the supply barges. Elend’s Allomancers and regular soldiers have formed a perimeter, using Thugs and Coinshots to bog down the riders. Vin lands among the southern bank of riders, noting they use stone-tipped spears and horses without metal shoes. She uses her pewter-enhanced strength to unhorse the lead soldier and causes chaos by Pushing against metal tent stakes.

Vin spots a short Mistborn watching her through the mist and pursues him across the rooftops of Fadrex. She burns duralumin for a final Push but loses sight of her target when he extinguishes his metals and hides. Returning to camp, she finds that the attack was a distraction for Yomen to slaughter ten thousand koloss using trebuchets. Elend explains that the forces are now more evenly matched and assigns Vin a thousand koloss to control directly. Vin walks into the ash-covered koloss camp and uses a duralumin-enhanced zinc Pull to seize control of the beasts.

The koloss Human approaches Vin and asks for more koloss because their numbers have dwindled. He leads her to the triage tents in Elend's camp while carrying a dead koloss corpse over his shoulder. Human rips the skin off the corpse and retrieves four small metal spikes from the creature's body. He attempts to drive these spikes into a wounded human soldier to create a new koloss. Vin realizes that koloss are created from five human souls merged via Hemalurgy, a technology that steals the power of Preservation via metal spikes.



### Chapter 38

Spook uses his enhanced senses and pewter-granted strength to oversee skaa workers sifting through the ruins of a burned mansion. He notes that tin and pewter are complementary opposites that allow him to move across blackened debris without pain. Skaa man Franson assists him, hoping to rescue his sister from an upcoming execution by the Citizen. Spook observes that the skaa workers can barely see in the darkness while he perceives the stars as brilliant limelights. He gives Franson a pouch of coins to pay the workers before Quellion abolishes currency in Urteau.

Spook examines the nine human skulls recovered from the wreckage and remembers Durn’s cryptic advice to count them. He realizes that ten people were supposedly sent into the building to die, yet only nine skulls remain. This discrepancy proves there is a hidden way to escape the Citizen’s burning execution buildings. In a skaa bar, Sazed and Breeze discuss the unusual lack of fear the locals show toward the mists. Breeze Soothes the patrons while Sazed reflects on how the Church of the Survivor has spread throughout the city.

Sazed speaks with a group of mine workers who express skepticism toward Breeze’s promises of change. He explains that Elend Venture is a worthy leader and that the Terris people have accepted his protection. The workers mention a new figure called the "Survivor of the Flames" who they believe has come to overthrow Quellion. Sazed is shocked to learn this title refers to a new "Survivor" and not Kelsier himself. The workers promise to take Sazed to see the new Survivor at an upcoming execution near the Marketpit.



### Chapter 39

The kandra TenSoon sits on a windswept hill, horrified by the thick layers of ash smothering the landscape. He lopes through the forest in his wolfhound form, finding the capacity for movement far superior to any human shape. TenSoon reflects on the complacency of the First and Second Generations who remain hidden in the Homeland while the world dies. He worries that the First Contract has become a mere ceremony rather than a set of instructions for the end of the world. His incurable wanderlust and fear of apathy drive him to seek a more active role in the coming apocalypse.

TenSoon navigates to a rocky hollow where he hid two polished iron spikes a year prior. He absorbs these spikes into his body, dissolving and re-forming his internal organs to accommodate the metal. This act bestows the Blessing of Potency upon him, a Hemalurgic gift that more than doubles his physical strength and endurance. These spikes were stolen from OreSeur’s body after TenSoon killed his own brother to serve Zane. He now possesses two Blessings, Presence and Potency, making him one of the most powerful kandra in existence.

With his enhanced physical power, TenSoon leaps from the rock formation and begins running eastward across the ash-covered ground. The First Contract mandates that kandra must seek out "The Father" when Ruin returns to the world. Since the Lord Ruler is dead, TenSoon decides that the closest equivalent to the Father is Vin. He believes that only she can provide the guidance needed to fulfill the kandra’s ancient purpose. TenSoon abandons the laws of his people and sets out to find the Mistborn girl who once spared his life.



### Chapter 40

Elend leads a council with Ham, Cett, Noorden, and Demoux to discuss the origins of Steel Inquisitors. They conclude that Inquisitors are created by killing multiple Mistings and transferring their powers via metal spikes. Vin reveals her discovery that koloss are also created from humans through "Hemalurgy," involving four spikes and five souls. The Lord Ruler used this art to create koloss soldiers, kandra spies, and Inquisitor priests with inherent weaknesses. Elend realizes that a malevolent intelligence is currently exploiting these weaknesses to control the creatures as pawns.

The group discusses how the Lord Ruler’s fall allowed this "Ruin" to take control of the Hemalurgic beings. Vin shares that Marsh grew increasingly unstable before turning on Sazed, suggesting that Inquisitors are also susceptible to manipulation. Elend orders Noorden to research koloss movements to identify patterns that might reveal the enemy's strategic agenda. Demoux reports that the "mistfallen" soldiers who were sick for sixteen days are being ostracized by the army. Elend decides to organize these soldiers into a new separate company to maintain military cohesion and trust.

Lieutenant Conrad arrives from Luthadel with news that King Penrod is facing riots and cannot send supplies. Conrad explains that three other messengers were likely killed by koloss and he barely survived an ambush. Elend realizes that the enemy is trying to force him away from Fadrex by undermining his supply base in Luthadel. Vin and Elend acknowledge that Ruin can hear their words and silently agree to stay and find the storage cavern. Vin attempts to call upon the power of the mists but finds herself unable to access their strength.
