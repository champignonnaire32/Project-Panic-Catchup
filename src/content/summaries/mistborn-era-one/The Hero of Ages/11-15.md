### Chapter 11

TenSoon stands trial before the kandra Second Generation in the Trustwarren while wearing no flesh. KanPaar accuses him of the murder of his generation brother OreSeur during the siege of Luthadel. The accused defends his actions by citing the constraints of his Contract with the Allomancer Zane. TenSoon reveals that he was forced to imitate OreSeur to spy on the Mistborn Vin for his master. He argues that service Contracts do not explicitly forbid the killing of other kandra.

The Second Generation expresses concern that using kandra to kill their own kind sets a dangerous precedent for their people. TenSoon counters by accusing the Seconds of assigning a kandra to Kelsier, the man who eventually killed the Lord Ruler. He claims his true loyalty shifted to the First Contract and names Vin as the rightful Mother of the kandra. TenSoon asserts that Ruin has arrived and that the world is currently entering the time of the Resolution. He admits to revealing the kandra Secret of Allomantic control to Vin to ensure her survival.

KanPaar suppresses TenSoon's shouts and declares him a traitor for exposing the kandra vulnerability to humans. The Seconds refuse to acknowledge Vin as the successor to the Father despite TenSoon's insistence. The First Generation remains silent in their high alcoves and offers no clemency to the defendant. TenSoon is told his sentencing will occur in one month and he is led back to his prison pit. He hopes his words will eventually inspire his people to act before the world ends.



### Chapter 12

Elend wears a white uniform to serve as a symbol of hope while preparing for war. Vin expresses concern for Sazed's loss of faith following the death of Tindwyl during the battle for Luthadel. She notes that Breeze is overcompensating for his fear by Pushing too hard on the emotions of others. Elend accepts his role as emperor and vows to find a way to fix the crumbling state of the empire. He gathers his advisors to study a map showing the rapid expansion of the daylight-killing mists.

The map indicates that ninety percent of the empire will soon be unable to support traditional crops. Ham suggests growing mushrooms or other low-light plants as a desperate alternative for feeding the populace. Elend decides the army must march on Fadrex City to secure the final storage cache left by the Lord Ruler. King Cett informs the group that Fadrex is currently ruled by an obligator named Lord Yomen. The council discusses the possibility of finding a hidden atium stash within the city's secret vaults.

An earthquake interrupts the meeting and shakes the conference tent before quickly subsiding. Elend plans a siege of Fadrex to force Yomen into a diplomatic surrender without excessive bloodshed. He tasks Sazed and Breeze with leading a diplomatic mission to suppress a skaa rebellion in the city of Urteau. Sazed reluctantly agrees to the mission despite his desire to perform personal research into religions. Elend commands his leaders to defy the falling ash by smiling and maintaining an appearance of optimism.



### Chapter 13

Marsh sits motionless in a koloss camp near the northern region of Terris while ash buries his body. He possesses ten new Hemalurgic spikes which are metal pins driven into the body to grant Allomantic powers. Ruin's direct control over his mind has lapsed temporarily as the entity focuses its attention elsewhere. The Steel Inquisitor struggles against the sense of inevitability that comes with the end of the world. He observes the three hundred thousand koloss in his army as they stagnate and kill one another.

The practical side of Marsh remembers his time leading the skaa rebellion before Kelsier was captured. He feels bitter that Kelsier succeeded in killing the Lord Ruler when Marsh had previously given up. Marsh recognizes that Ruin can watch the world freely but cannot fully see into a man's mind. He realizes that the entity is waiting for a specific moment to unleash the koloss army. The Inquisitor plans to wait for a moment of distraction to strike back against his master.

Marsh decides his ultimate act of rebellion will be to pull the primary spike from his own back. This action will result in his immediate death but will remove Ruin's most powerful tool. He hopes this sacrifice will provide Vin and Elend with the chance they need to save the world. The Inquisitor remains still and allows the red sunlight to fade as the mists return to the camp. He prepares himself to play his final part in the unfolding destruction of the Final Empire.



### Chapter 14

Spook flares his tin metal constantly to enhance his senses to a superhuman and permanent level. He moves through the mists of Urteau using eyes that see starlight as bright as the sun. His heightened senses make the cold air feel freezing and the ground feel sharp against his feet. The young Tineye wears a thick cloak and uses a cloth wrap to protect his eyes during the day. He believes these physical sacrifices are necessary to make him a useful member of Kelsier's former crew.

The city of Urteau is built around dry canals called streetslots where residents walk between ten-foot-high walls. Spook infiltrates a guardhouse used by the Citizen to listen to secret government meetings. He watches Beldre, the Citizen's sister, as she sits alone in an ornamental garden with auburn hair. The Citizen, a man named Quellion, discusses Elend's recent conquest of a storage cache in the south. Quellion declares that Urteau must remain a skaa-ruled nation and refuses to submit to a nobleman's rule.

The Citizen orders a genealogical survey to root out and execute any citizens with noble parentage. Spook takes detailed notes on the new laws regarding prohibited clothing and government funding. He watches Beldre from his vantage point and feels a connection to the sadness in her eyes. The Tineye chooses not to reveal himself to the girl despite a sudden impulse to speak with her. He retreats into the shadows of the guardhouse to maintain his position as a silent spy.



### Chapter 15

Vin watches the daylight mists burn away in the red sunlight as the army prepares to march. She notices that the mists now pull away from her Allomancy instead of protecting her as they once did. Elend and Ham argue over the necessity of forcing the soldiers to march through the mist sickness. The emperor insists that the army must be immunized to ensure they are not vulnerable during the morning. Vin agrees with Elend and states that the army cannot remain dependent on the whims of the weather.

The camp collapses its tents and prepares the canal barges for a journey into the Western Dominance. Vin finds the koloss named Human sitting on the edge of the camp and covered in falling ash. Human is an eleven-foot-tall blue creature with skin that is too small for its muscular frame. He tells Vin that he wants to live in a house and possess a human identity. The creature claims that Vin is more like a koloss than she is like the other humans.

Human states that he hates the mists because he feels that the mists hate him in return. Vin realizes that she shares this sensation and acknowledges that the mists have become her enemy. She recalls drawing upon the mists to defeat the Lord Ruler but finds the power currently inaccessible. The Empress feels a growing sense of dread as she contemplates the supernatural nature of the Deepness. She joins Elend as the army finally begins its long march toward the city of Fadrex.
