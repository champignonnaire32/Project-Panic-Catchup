### Chapter 1

Fatren and his brother Druffel observe falling ash and a red sun from the earthen bulwark surrounding their town, Vetitan. They discuss the impending arrival of a koloss army and the fact that the mists are lingering until noon, which is killing their crops. A lone rider in a dark cloak approaches the city gates, and the guards realize he is a human on a horse rather than a koloss. The stranger uses Allomancy to leap onto the top of the wooden gate and reveals a brilliant white uniform. He disarms Druffel by pulling his sword through the air and then hands the weapon back hilt-first. The man introduces himself as Elend Venture, the emperor, and begins commanding the soldiers to prepare for a koloss attack.

Elend orders the townspeople to hide the children and elderly in the southernmost buildings to delay the koloss during their blood frenzy. He questions Fatren about the weapons scavenged from the local lord's manor and assesses the training of the town's two thousand peasant soldiers. Fatren, realizing his people are doomed without help, decides to accept Elend's leadership and pretends he sent for the emperor. Elend sets non-negotiable conditions, demanding that Fatren swear fealty to him if they survive the battle. He explains that he needs the city's soldiers and resources for a larger purpose he will reveal later. Fatren observes that Elend possesses a regal aura that makes the soldiers obey him without hesitation.

Elend gathers the soldier captains and provides specific tactical instructions for fighting the koloss, who range from five to twelve feet in height. He explains that koloss are stronger than humans but uncoordinated, so the men must gang up on individual creatures in small groups. He warns the soldiers to attack the legs of large koloss and avoid getting weapons caught in the loose skin of smaller ones. When Fatren points out they are outnumbered five to one, Elend insists they can win because they have him. A scout sightings the koloss camp, and Elend decides to launch an immediate assault while the creatures are distracted by making camp. He leads the charge himself, and the town's army follows him off the bulwark into the ash-scattered battlefield.



### Chapter 2

TenSoon, a kandra of the Third Generation, sits in a pitch-black stone pit prison without a skeleton to support his translucent muscle mass. He possesses the Blessing of Presence, a spike that grants him heightened consciousness and prevents him from succumbing to madness despite his confinement. He reflects on his status as a Contract-breaker, having betrayed his master's will and eventually causing his master's death. However, his most significant crime is revealing the kandra's greatest secret, which could lead to his people being enslaved or controlled. His captors provide him with just enough food and water to survive while washing his waste through a small drainage hole. He expects a death sentence but knows the Second Generation needs more information about who he told the secret to.

Vibrations signal the arrival of his captors, who use hooks to rip his flesh and pull him out of the pit. They spill his body onto the prison floor, where he tastes dirt and the pungent scent of acid in a gold-lined bucket. Instead of acid, they drop a human skull onto his mass, providing him with a framework to build a rudimentary body. TenSoon realizes the guards have orders to execute him but must technically follow the First Contract by giving him a chance to speak. He works with desperate speed, far faster than younger kandra, to form a tongue, lungs, and a trachea around the skull. He manages to croak out the word "Judgment" just as the acid begins to dissolve his outer muscles.

The pouring of acid stops as TenSoon invokes his right to a public trial, which is mandated by kandra law. A guard whispers that he is a fool and suggests he should have accepted the chance to die with some measure of respect. TenSoon understands that a public trial will expose the full extent of his betrayal to all kandra, likely resulting in an eternity of imprisonment. He weighs the option of a quick death against the potential for further harm to his people if he speaks openly. Despite the certainty of damnation and pain, he chooses to pursue the trial for reasons he keeps to himself. He repeats the request for judgment in a whisper, ensuring the legal process must proceed.



### Chapter 3

Elend Venture flares pewter to enhance his strength and speed as his army crashes into the surprised koloss camp at Vetitan. The koloss, who are unaccustomed to being attacked by outnumbered humans, struggle to react as Elend cuts down a smaller creature with blue skin. Elend uses emotional Allomancy to soothe the fear of his soldiers and pull on their bravery to keep them fighting. He encounters a large koloss with tight, ripped skin and uses steel to spray coins at it before dodging its massive blade. He shears through the creature's leg and decapitates it, noticing that his white uniform is now sprayed with bright red blood. The battle intensifies as the koloss enter a blood frenzy, screaming as they charge the buckling human lines.

Vin arrives at the battlefield by leaping from the sky and using duralumin-enhanced steel to blast away hundreds of koloss with a massive shockwave. She uses pewter to maneuver through the horde, killing several creatures with a massive iron sword she pulls from the ground. Vin targets a thirteen-foot-tall koloss, blocks its swing by planting her sword in the earth, and kills it with a pewter-enhanced kick to the head. She begins to take mental control of individual koloss by pushing on their emotions, turning them against their own kind. Elend notices her arrival and follows her lead, snatching control of more creatures to bolster their thinning ranks. Together, they create a pocket of chaos within the army, though they are still vastly outnumbered by the remaining ten thousand beasts.

A Steel Inquisitor with spikes through its eyes and an obsidian axe appears, bounding over the battlefield to confront Vin. Vin burns electrum to protect herself from atium and attempts to pull a spike from the Inquisitor's back, but finds it protected by a metal plate. Elend joins the fight, using a violent steel push to throw the Inquisitor back despite the creature's attempts to brace itself. The Inquisitor moves with unnatural, blurring speed and attempts to kill Elend with a long metal spike, but Vin turns a koloss against it. The controlled koloss crushes the Inquisitor's head with a heavy sword, killing the creature instantly. Elend burns duralumin and brass to send a massive wave of soothing across the entire battlefield, causing all ten thousand koloss to stop fighting and submit.



### Chapter 4

Sazed, the Terris Feruchemist and ambassador, reviews his notes on the Canzi religion while waiting in Lekal City. He concludes that the religion is logically inconsistent because it claims spirits choose their bodies while also stating that physical disabilities are divine punishments. Since the death of Tindwyl a year ago, Sazed has been systematically analyzing and rejecting hundreds of religions from his metalminds to find the truth. He sits with Breeze, a Soother who is helping him negotiate a treaty to bring King Lekal's lands into Elend's empire. Breeze uses his Allomancy to nudge Sazed's emotions, though Sazed notices the touch and confronts him about his hidden kindheartedness. They discuss the strategic necessity of bullying King Lekal into submission to provide protection against the gathering koloss.

Sazed reflects on the loss of the Terris Synod and the fact that the Lord Ruler was himself a Terrisman who oppressed his own people. He admits to Breeze that he has stopped wearing his copperminds because he no longer believes in the religions he spent his life collecting. He feels unworthy of leading his people and worries that the power of Feruchemy might die with him because he is a eunuch. Sazed touches the scars on his abdomen where the Inquisitor Marsh nearly killed him with metal rings at the Well of Ascension. He tells Breeze that if a God existed, he would not have allowed the world to fall into its current state of decay and ash. Breeze insists that Sazed is not meant to be an atheist and will eventually regain his faith.

An aide enters the ornate room with a signed treaty, confirming that King Lekal has agreed to become a vassal of Emperor Elend Venture. The treaty ensures that Elend's armies will protect the city, but at the cost of its independent governance and freedom. Sazed remains bitter, noting that while Breeze's loved one Allrianne is alive, Sazed has lost everything and feels his faith has failed him. He looks out the window at the perpetual falling ash, which is a constant reminder of the world's deteriorating condition. The signature on the parchment is small and cramped, reflecting the king's realization that he was thoroughly beaten by Elend's political pressure. Sazed packs his portfolio of "dead" religions, preparing to move on to the next one in his search for meaning.



### Chapter 5

Elend and Vin inspect the body of the fallen Inquisitor and discover it had ten spikes, including a new pewter one through its heart. Vin is disturbed by the creature's unnatural speed during the fight, which she believes was not caused by Allomantic pewter or duralumin. She takes a pewter spike from the corpse and notes that they are still playing a game with rules they do not fully understand. Elend attempts to comfort Vin as she expresses her fear that a malevolent force is directing the Inquisitors and the koloss armies. They acknowledge that the mists are killing crops and that the world is becoming a frozen wasteland as the sun grows redder. Elend maintains his hope, adhering to the Church of the Survivor's command to survive despite the collapsing environment.

The group enters Vetitan's Steel Ministry building, where Vin's koloss servant rips the boards off the doors to reveal a hidden wine cellar. Elend and Vin use Allomancy to pull a massive stone door open, uncovering an enormous secret storage cavern filled with food and supplies. Fatren is amazed by the wealth of resources, but Elend informs him that the city and the cavern now belong to the empire. Elend explains that the Lord Ruler created these supply depots centuries ago in anticipation of the mists returning to destroy the world. They find a metal plate inscribed by the Lord Ruler that mentions a new metal called malatium and instructions for controlling koloss. The plate also contains a map leading to a final cache in Fadrex City, which they believe contains the Lord Ruler's hidden atium hoard.

Vin notices a small inscription at the bottom of the metal plate warning that a force can hear their words and read their writing. The warning states that only thoughts are safe, implying that the enemy they released from the Well of Ascension is constantly watching them. Vin realizes she cannot discuss her plans with anyone without alerting this force to her intentions. Elend decides that the people of Vetitan must be moved to Luthadel because their city is outside the empire's defensive perimeter and their crops are doomed. He feels like a tyrant for seizing the city and forcing the people from their homes, but Vin insists it is necessary for survival. They prepare to head to Fadrex City to claim the final cache and search for the atium that could save their empire.
