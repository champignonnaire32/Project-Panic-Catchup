### Chapter 26

Vin wakes up at Clubs’s shop in Luthadel suffering from a severe headache caused by pewter dragging. This condition occurs when an Allomancer pushes their body to physical exhaustion while burning pewter and then stops. She joins the crew in the kitchen where they discuss the recent destruction of their rebel army. Ham explains that the Luthadel Garrison is currently hunting down the survivors of the failed rebellion. Kelsier remains unexpectedly cheerful and insists that the loss of the army is merely a temporary setback.

Breeze accuses Kelsier of being more interested in his own legend than the success of the job. Spook interrupts the tension with news that the Ministry has organized a massive execution in the central fountain square. The crew travels to the square disguised as skaa and finds a vantage point on a nearby rooftop. Clubs uses his Allomancy to hide the group from the sensory reach of the Steel Inquisitors. Vin recognizes her father, the high-ranking obligator Tevidian, standing prominently among the Ministry officials.

The Lord Ruler arrives in a black carriage and suppresses the emotions of the massive crowd with powerful Soothing. Kelsier and Vin work together to Riot the crew's emotions to counteract the Lord Ruler’s numbing influence. Steel Inquisitors begin executing random skaa men, women, and children with obsidian axes to punish the city. The blood from the victims is sprayed into the square's fountains, turning the water a deep red. Kelsier declares that the horror justifies their war and reveals his plan to eventually bribe the returning Garrison.



### Chapter 27

The crew returns to the shop to strategize their next moves following the public executions. Kelsier decides to shift their focus toward inciting a house war among the noble families of Luthadel. Dockson explains that they must create financial instability to make the most powerful houses look vulnerable. Kelsier instructs the crew members to use their various aliases to spread misinformation and increase existing tensions. They decide to target House Venture specifically because its fall would destabilize the entire Final Empire.

Sazed delivers a secret message from Kelsier’s brother, Marsh, who has successfully infiltrated the Ministry. Kelsier and Vin travel to the impoverished slum known as the Twists to meet with him. While they wait, Vin questions Kelsier about the mysterious Eleventh Metal and his knowledge of its properties. Kelsier admits he does not yet know how to use the metal but hopes the logbook contains a clue. He also explains the mechanics of Feruchemy, which involves storing physical and mental attributes in metal jewelry.

Marsh arrives at the meeting point wearing the distinctive tattoos of a Ministry obligator. He reveals that he has joined the Canton of Inquisition and is serving as a Seeker. Marsh reports that the Ministry maintains hidden Soothing stations throughout the city to keep the skaa population compliant. He notes that the Steel Inquisitors are actually modified humans who are long-lived but eventually die of old age. Kelsier asks for a list of the Soothing stations so the crew can plan a coordinated strike against them.



### Chapter 28

Vin attends a lavish ball at Keep Lekal and begins her mission of spreading targeted rumors. She targets Lady Kliss and suggests that the powerful House Hasting is planning to withdraw from Luthadel. This lie is intended to make the house appear weak and encourage its enemies to attack. Vin encounters Elend Venture in a secluded alcove, where he expresses deep concern for her safety. He warns her that the city is becoming a war zone and urges her to flee the capital immediately.

Elend reveals that House Venture maintains its dominance by secretly mining the Lord Ruler’s atium supply. He admits that his father is struggling to meet the Lord Ruler's quotas, putting the house in jeopardy. Vin attempts to warn Elend that Shan Elariel is planning an assassination attempt against him. Elend dismisses the warning as he does not believe Shan is a serious threat to his life. He leaves Vin to join his group of friends who meet to discuss unconventional political philosophies.

Jastes Lekal and Telden confront Elend about his relationship with Vin and express their suspicions that she is a spy. Jastes reveals that he had Vin’s carriage followed and discovered she was lying about where she lives. Back at Keep Venture, Straff Venture orders his son to attend a luncheon to secure an alliance with House Tegas. Straff mocks Elend’s incompetence and expresses a desire to see him dead before he can inherit the house. Elend agrees to his father's demands in exchange for two spies to follow Vin and uncover her secrets.



### Chapter 29

Vin reads the final translated pages of the logbook, which detail the Hero's arrival at the Terris Mountains. The author describes the extreme cold of the environment and the supernatural resilience of his Terris packmen. He writes extensively about the young man named Rashek, who expresses a violent hatred for all non-Terris people. The author worries that the power at the Well of Ascension will tempt him toward selfishness and destruction. The journal entries end abruptly on the eve of the author’s attempt to reach the Well and save the world.

Vin meets with Sazed in the mansion's kitchen and expresses her deep frustration with the logbook’s lack of a conclusion. Sazed explains that the Lord Ruler likely stopped writing once he achieved immortality and established his new empire. He informs Vin that he has sent copies of the translation to his fellow Keepers for safekeeping. They walk through the simplified beauty of the eastern gardens while discussing the inevitable end of their current life. Vin fears that the crew will fail and she will be forced to return to her previous life.

Sazed attempts to encourage Vin by sharing the philosophy of the Astalsi, who believed that ill luck was a limited resource. Vin asks to test the limits of Allomancy by burning a piece of metal that Sazed has used for Feruchemy. Sazed gives her a pewter earring backing that contains a significant amount of his stored physical strength. Vin swallows the metal and burns it, discovering that she can sense a faint reserve she cannot actually use. They return to the mansion just as the carriage arrives for their final meeting with the crew.



### Chapter 30

Vin enters the Venture ballroom and notices that the atmosphere is unusually cold and defensive. Sazed informs her that multiple great houses have canceled their future balls, signaling the start of the house war. Vin realizes that the guests are staying within their own factions and are no longer interested in social mingling. She sees Elend sitting with his father at the host's table, which is a significant departure from his usual behavior. Elend eventually approaches Vin but tells her that their relationship was merely a passing dalliance and he must prioritize his house.

Vin is devastated by Elend's rejection and feels as though she has been betrayed by everyone she trusted. The informant Lady Kliss approaches Vin and reveals that she has known about Vin's deceptions for some time. Kliss demands payment to keep Vin's secrets and reveals that Shan Elariel is planning to assassinate Elend that night. She explains that Straff Venture has intentionally lowered the guard to allow the Elariel assassins to kill his own son. Vin uses emotional Allomancy to force Kliss to reveal that the attack will take place in Elend’s personal quarters.

Vin strips off her ball gown and races across the keep's rooftops to reach Elend’s rooms before the assassins. She interrupts the group of assassins, including Shan Elariel, who is revealed to be a trained Mistborn. Vin kills the accompanying Thugs and Coinshots before engaging in a lethal duel against Shan on the high battlements. Both women burn atium, but Vin uses a clever trick with a broken arrow to bypass Shan’s defenses. After killing Shan, Vin Pushes herself away from the keep and disappears into the mists as the city erupts into chaos.
