### Chapter 1

Vin hides in a watch-hole of a thieving crew's safe house in Luthadel while ash falls from the sky. Her brother Reen has betrayed and abandoned her, leaving her with the lesson that anyone will betray anyone else. Ulef, a young crewmember, finds her and tells her that the crewleader Camon is angry and searching for her. Vin joins Camon for a scam involving Lord Jedue, an impoverished nobleman seeking Ministry contracts. Camon strikes Vin for her perceived rebellion, but she uses her "Luck" to dampen his anger and calm him.

The scam takes place in a rented hotel suite where Camon impersonates Lord Jedue before Prelan Laird of the Steel Ministry. Vin advises Camon to use skaa servants instead of well-dressed ones to better sell the image of desperation. Laird initially rejects the contract for transporting acolytes, but Camon offers to cut the former fee in half. Vin senses Laird’s suspicion and reaches out with her Luck to dampen his negative emotions. The manipulation works, and Laird agrees to take the proposal to the Ministry Council for further review.

A short epigraph mentions Terris prophecies and the heavy burden of power that can save or destroy the world. The narrative shifts to Kelsier, a legendary thief with scarred arms, who observes Luthadel from the city wall. Kelsier meets his associate Dockson and admits to killing Lord Tresting in a northern plantation. They discuss forming a crew for a major job, but learn that their former "Smoker," Trap, was recently executed. Kelsier suggests recruiting Clubs as a replacement and insists that his brother Marsh will join them eventually.



### Chapter 2

Vin sits in the lair of Camon’s crew, keeping to the shadows to avoid being noticed by the other thieves. She reflects on how Reen’s abuse made her resilient and how Camon now exploits her as a good-luck charm. Camon leads Vin and several others to the Canton of Finance building to finalize the contract scam. Inside, Camon meets with High Prelan Arriev instead of Prelan Laird, and Arriev demands a personal persuasion. Vin uses her Luck on Arriev to make him less suspicious, leading him to grant the contract and a three-thousand boxing advance.

Kelsier and Dockson watch from the Canton waiting room as Camon and Vin leave with the money. Kelsier observes that Vin is at least a Misting and is unknowingly using Allomancy on Ministry officials. They see a Steel Inquisitor with metal spikes through its eyes emerge from the building and begin following Camon. Kelsier realizes the Ministry detected Vin's emotional tampering and has sent the Inquisitor to track her back to the lair. Dockson leaves to deal with the Ministry scouts while Kelsier prepares to divert the Inquisitor using his own Allomancy.

The text includes a brief note about the beauty of the Terris mountains and the agrarian nature of the land. Back in the city, Camon counts his boxings in the lair, oblivious to the danger approaching his crew. Vin attempts to convince Ulef to flee with her because she fears the Ministry is coming. Ulef betrays her by informing Camon of her plan to leave, leading Camon to attack her in a drunken fury. Kelsier arrives at the lair just as Camon prepares to kill Vin, and he uses an unseen force to throw Camon backward.



### Chapter 3

Kelsier enters the lair and intimidates the entire crew by revealing that an Inquisitor was following them. He demands the three thousand boxings as payment for saving their lives and orders Milev to take over the crew. Kelsier singles out Vin and tells her she is a Mistborn, someone who can use all eight Allomantic metals. He gives her a vial of metal flakes to test her abilities and proves she can both dampen and inflame emotions. Vin realizes her "Luck" is actually Allomancy and that Kelsier is a survivor of the Pits of Hathsin.

Kelsier explains that the Steel Ministry hunts half-breeds and that Vin's father is likely a high-ranking obligator. He warns her never to use emotional Allomancy on obligators because they are trained to detect such manipulations. Vin experiences a massive surge of power after drinking the metals, finding she has access to vast reserves of strength. Kelsier invites her to join his crew for a specific job that involves challenging the Lord Ruler. Vin is hesitant and suspicious of his friendliness but recognizes that she needs to learn how he became so powerful.

The chapter ends with a note about a quest into the cold highlands of Terris requiring local packmen. Inside the lair, Vin listens to the crewmembers discuss Kelsier’s legendary escape from the Pits of Hathsin. They mention his wife died there and that he gained his signature arm scars during the escape. Vin learns that Kelsier was not an Allomancer before entering the Pits and some believe he is no longer human. She decides to stay with him to gain the power and knowledge she needs to eventually find her own freedom.



### Chapter 4

Kelsier’s specialized crew arrives at the lair, including a muscular "Thug" named Ham and an emotional "Soother" named Breeze. Breeze uses Allomancy to make Vin fetch him a drink, which she resents once she realizes she was being manipulated. A gnarled man named Clubs arrives to serve as the crew’s "Smoker," whose copper burning hides them from Inquisitors. Yeden, a leader of the skaa rebellion, joins the meeting and reveals he has hired the crew for thirty thousand boxings. Kelsier shocks everyone by announcing the job is to overthrow the Final Empire and seize the Lord Ruler’s atium.

Kelsier argues that the rebellion has failed for a thousand years because it lacks the vision and organization of thieves. He plans to neutralize the Luthadel Garrison, throw the city into chaos, and then have Yeden’s army seize the palace. The crew is promised half of the Lord Ruler’s secret atium reserves, which are supposedly hidden in the palace treasury. Kelsier reveals the atium comes from the Pits of Hathsin, where he was once a prisoner and slave. He insists that a small, specialized team of Allomancers can succeed where large-scale rebellions have always been crushed.

Breeze and Ham eventually agree to join the job despite its extreme risks and Kelsier’s apparent madness. Kelsier further stuns the crew by declaring his personal intent to kill the Lord Ruler using a legendary "Eleventh Metal." He shows them a thin bar of silvery-white metal that he claims is the Lord Ruler’s specific weakness. Vin watches the exchange with skepticism, believing the Lord Ruler is an immortal force of nature that cannot be killed. Kelsier concludes the meeting by ordering the crew to start thinking about the specific steps needed to cause the city's collapse.



### Chapter 5

After the meeting, Kelsier finds a hidden bottle of expensive wine behind a false panel in Camon’s old bar. He and Dockson discuss the need for another Smoker since Clubs initially walked out on the plan. Clubs returns with a teenage boy and agrees to join the crew because he wants to spit in the Lord Ruler’s face. Kelsier tells Vin that people are attracted to vision and that the job is too grand for a man like Clubs to ignore. They prepare to leave the lair as the mists begin to rise over the blackened city of Luthadel.

Kelsier dons a mistcloak made of ribbon-like strips and prepares to infiltrate Keep Venture to steal atium. He leaves his shoes and stockings behind to minimize metal on his person and avoid detection by other Allomancers. Using Steelpushes and Ironpulls, Kelsier travels across rooftops and leaps over the walls of the noble keep. He kills a guard on a service walkway and proceeds to Lord Venture’s third-floor study to find the safe. Kelsier uses his enhanced strength from burning pewter to rip a small safe directly out of the stone wall.

Eight hazekillers trained to fight Mistborn attack Kelsier with wooden dueling canes and shields to negate his Allomancy. Kelsier uses a heavy metal paperweight as an improvised weapon, Pulling and Pushing it to strike the men from unexpected angles. More Allomancers arrive, forcing Kelsier to leap out of a shattered window while tethered to the safe by an Ironpull. The safe smashes on the cobblestones below, and Kelsier retrieves a small pouch of atium beads from the wreckage. He flees into the mists with his prize, leaving the mangled bodies of the noble guards behind him.
