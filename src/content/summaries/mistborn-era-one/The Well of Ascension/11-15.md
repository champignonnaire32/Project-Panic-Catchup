### Chapter 11

Elend and Clubs stand on the Luthadel city wall and observe the arrival of Lord Cett’s army. They note that Cett’s forces are flying his banner and have set up camp near the Luth-Davn Canal. Vin identifies a lone rider fleeing Cett’s archers and recognizes him as Breeze. She uses duralumin, a metal that flares all other active metals in a single massive burst, to launch herself from the wall. Landing between Breeze and the archers, she uses her Allomancy to deflect arrows and disable horses.

Breeze enters the city safely and reveals that he spent months manipulating Cett into marching on Luthadel. He explains that a three-way standoff between Straff, Cett, and Elend is strategically superior to a two-way conflict. This arrangement forces the larger armies to hesitate, as attacking Luthadel would leave them vulnerable to the third party. Breeze mentions that he delayed Cett’s arrival by poisoning the camp’s water supply with a mild laxative. Elend welcomes Breeze back but remains wary of the growing military pressure outside his walls.

Captain Demoux informs Elend and Vin about a discovery in the palace visitor chambers. They find a second set of kandra-cleaned bones hidden in a back closet, indicating another impostor. OreSeur sniffs the remains and determines that the kandra likely replaced someone in the palace very recently. Vin realizes that any member of the core crew or palace staff could be a spy. The group concludes that a kandra-eaten corpse means someone important has been killed and replaced.



### Chapter 12

Sazed and Marsh arrive at the Conventical of Seran, an Inquisitor fortress hidden within a massive crater. Sazed uses his ironmind to decrease his weight and fall slowly to the crater floor. He uses his tinmind, a metalmind that stores and enhances senses, to study the silent structure. They discover that the fortress is constructed with stark, blocky steel architecture and lacks any artistic adornment. Sazed uses his pewtermind to gain the strength necessary to push open the massive steel front doors.

Inside the fortress, they find the bodies of slaughtered servants who were killed by the Inquisitors before they abandoned the site. Marsh senses a bad air about the place and instructs Sazed to explore the lower levels alone. Sazed enters a room filled with bloody metal tables and various metal spikes used to create new Inquisitors. He notes that some of the spikes are made of different metals and still bear traces of blood. This discovery confirms Sazed’s suspicions about the secretive and violent nature of the Inquisitor transformation process.

Sazed finds a hidden chamber at the back of the basement that contains a large steel plate. The plate is inscribed with text in an old Terris dialect written by a philosopher named Kwaan. Kwaan admits he discovered Alendi and initially proclaimed him the Hero of Ages but later chose to betray him. He writes that he set the words in steel because anything not recorded in metal cannot be trusted. Sazed takes a charcoal rubbing of the text, realizing it is a foundational religious document from before the Lord Ruler's reign.



### Chapter 13

Vin sits on a rooftop with OreSeur and observes that the mists are appearing earlier in the evening than usual. She questions OreSeur about kandra motivations and learns they are strictly bound by their Contracts and cannot kill humans. OreSeur reveals that kandra are completely immune to mental Allomancy like Rioting and Soothing. Vin realizes she can use this fact to test members of the crew for impostors by attempting to manipulate their emotions. She also learns that a kandra cannot use Allomancy even if they ingest the bones of an Allomancer.

Vin senses a strange bronze pulse and discovers a mist spirit watching her from a nearby abandoned building. The spirit is formed entirely of swirling mists and appears to have a vague humanoid outline in the darkness. When Vin demands to know why it is watching her, the spirit steps forward and grabs her arm. The touch is intensely cold and causes a sharp, agonizing pain to shoot through Vin’s head and mind. She loses her voice and falls backward out of the third-story window toward the cobblestone street.

Vin flares pewter to survive the impact and finds that her arm remains chilled by the spirit’s touch. She flees the area with OreSeur but refuses to explain her encounter with the phantom to the kandra. Later, Elend meets with his inner council to discuss the threat posed by the two invading armies. The crew suggests making a strategic alliance with one army, but Elend proposes playing both sides against each other. He intends to manipulate Straff and Cett into a conflict that will weaken both forces while Luthadel remains safe.



### Chapter 14

Captain Demoux catches a Terriswoman named Tindwyl eavesdropping on Elend’s council meeting from an adjacent room. Tindwyl, a tall and sturdy woman with elongated earlobes and numerous earrings, reveals she is a Keeper of Terris. She specializes in the biographies of great leaders and was prompted to visit Luthadel by Sazed’s correspondence. Tindwyl immediately critiques Elend’s poor posture and lack of regal presence, calling his appearance slovenly. She dismisses the council members and insists on speaking with Elend alone to evaluate his potential as a ruler.

Tindwyl explains that a king only has as much authority as his subjects are willing to give him. She argues that Elend’s reliance on books has isolated him from the practical actions required to lead people. Elend accepts her offer of instruction, hoping she can help him navigate the complexities of his new throne. Vin enters the room through a window, displaying hostility toward the newcomer, but Tindwyl remains calm and observant. The Terriswoman notes that Vin and Elend are an interesting couple before withdrawing to her new palace quarters.

Vin and Elend discuss the Lord Ruler’s final warning about the doom he supposedly averted for mankind. They reflect on the nature of the Deepness, a forgotten evil that the Hero of Ages was meant to destroy. Vin wonders if the red sun and brown plants are related to the Deepness and if the Well of Ascension still exists. Elend reassures her that no monsters are currently rampaging across the land, but Vin remains uneasy. She leaves to continue her patrol, determined to uncover the secrets of the mist spirit and the palace spy.



### Chapter 15

Sazed wakes up to find that Marsh has abandoned their camp and disappeared without a trace. He decides to continue his journey alone and heads toward a village called Urbene in the Eastern Dominance. Upon arrival, he finds the village filled with the stench of rotting bodies and no signs of living inhabitants. He discovers that the skaa in the village have starved to death inside their hovels despite animals roaming free. Sazed finds a single survivor hiding among the corpses in one of the wooden buildings.

The survivor is a crazed man who explains that the mists came during the day and stayed for weeks. He claims that the mists killed those who tried to leave and caused others to fall and writhe on the ground. The villagers were so terrified of the daytime mists that they stayed indoors until their food and water ran out. The survivor admits to cannibalism, having eaten the rotted meat of the dead to stay alive. Sazed is horrified to learn that the mists have become active and lethal during the daylight hours.

The man asks Sazed why the Lord Ruler abandoned them and expresses a belief that the god-king’s absence allowed the mists to kill. Sazed forcefully removes the man from the hovel to show him the sun, but the survivor eventually flees in terror. Realizing that the world faces a threat far greater than invading armies, Sazed prepares for a rapid journey. He puts on a steelmind, which stores physical speed, and prepares to sprint toward Luthadel to warn his friends. He understands that the changes in the mists represent a catastrophic danger to all mankind.
