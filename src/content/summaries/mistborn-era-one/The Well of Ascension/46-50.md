### Chapter 46

Breeze receives a secretive invitation from Sazed and arrives at a meeting guarded by Captain Demoux’s soldiers. Inside, he finds Sazed, Clubs, Ham, and Dockson gathered to discuss the impending fall of Luthadel without Elend or Vin present. Clubs explains that Straff Venture is likely planning to withdraw his army so that the koloss will attack and weaken the city's defenses. If the koloss slaughter the defenders and noblemen, Straff can then return as a liberator and establish control over a broken Luthadel. The crew realizes that they cannot win a direct fight against the combined threats and will likely die defending the city walls.

Sazed argues that their deaths will serve as a necessary precedent to show that the skaa will no longer be bullied by tyrants. He explains that while the Lord Ruler’s death caused chaos, it was the only way to allow society and science to eventually progress. To ensure the survival of their cause, Sazed proposes sending Vin, Elend, and Spook away from the city before the final siege begins. He believes Vin is a vital symbol of the Survivor and must be preserved to lead future resistance efforts against the warlords. The crew agrees to trick Elend into believing the mission to the Well of Ascension is a tactical necessity rather than a flight from death.

Vin visits the abandoned palace of Kredik Shaw and reflects on her reluctance to be the ruthless killer Kelsier trained her to be. In the camp of Straff Venture, Zane survives an assassination attempt by his father’s soldiers and confronts Straff in his tent. Zane reveals that he is leaving with Vin and tells Straff to follow through with the plan to let the koloss attack Luthadel first. He spares Straff’s life because of a twisted sense of filial love and retrieves his mistcloak and a stash of atium from a hidden location. Zane prepares to leave the camp forever, driven by the voice in his head to find Vin and escape their service to others.



### Chapter 47

Vin sits on her balcony and feels the thumping of the Well of Ascension growing more demanding as its power returns to the world. Zane arrives and urges her to flee with him to Terris, arguing that Elend does not understand her and only fears her power. He claims that by leaving, she will remove Straff’s only Mistborn threat and give her allies a better chance at survival through fear. Vin initially considers his offer but ultimately refuses because she realizes that Elend truly trusts and loves her for who she is. Enraged by her rejection, Zane attacks Vin and attempts to kill her with his superior Allomantic skill and atium.

During the struggle, Vin calls for OreSeur to help, but the wolfhound instead obeys Zane’s command and joins his side. Zane reveals that TenSoon, a kandra under his contract, killed the original OreSeur over a year ago and has been spying on Vin ever since. The kandra admits to manipulating Vin into suspecting her friends while he provided Zane with details about her new metal, duralumin. Zane uses duralumin to counter Vin’s own powerful Pushes, slamming her against the walls and ceiling of her chambers. He pins her down and stabs her in the chest while mocking her trust in Elend and the crew.

TenSoon provides a cryptic hint about kandra secrets, allowing Vin to use a duralumin-enhanced Soothing to take control of his body. She forces the wolfhound to tackle Zane, then retrieves a bead of atium from the kandra’s shoulder and swallows it to even the fight. Vin realizes that atium allows a user to see the future, but she can change that future by reacting to Zane’s own anticipated movements. She clears her mind and strikes at where Zane will be rather than where he is, successfully driving her dagger into his neck. Zane dies realizing that the voice in his head was not insanity but a literal entity that has now abandoned him.



### Chapter 48

Vin flees the palace and arrives at Elend’s quarters, bloodied and crying, to confess that she killed his brother Zane. Elend takes her to Sazed’s rooms, where the Terrisman uses his medical knowledge and pewter-enhanced speed to treat her deep puncture wounds. While being stitched, Vin tells Elend that Kelsier’s true greatness was his ability to trust and inspire others, a trait Elend possesses instinctively. She asks Sazed to perform a wedding ceremony, choosing the simple Larsta love oath that requires only a profession of mutual devotion. Sazed witnesses their vows and declares them married, marking their commitment to each other despite the chaos surrounding the city.

Sazed informs the couple that the Hero of Ages must return the power of the Well of Ascension to the world to defeat the Deepness. He provides a map from his metalminds indicating that the Well is located north in the Terris Mountains atop the peak of Derytatith. Elend realizes that by leaving for the Well, they can potentially destroy the koloss and Straff’s armies while ensuring their own survival. Sazed lies to them by claiming he knows exactly what the power will do, hoping to save his friends from the upcoming massacre in Luthadel. The plan is set for Vin, Elend, Spook, and Tindwyl to depart immediately to seek military aid and divine intervention from the north.

Elend carries the exhausted Vin back to their rooms, feeling a newfound sense of clarity and purpose in their relationship. Sazed remains behind and reflects on his decision to fabricate a religious quest to ensure the safety of the young crewmembers. He acknowledges that he has manipulated history and mythology, but believes the lie is justified to preserve the lives of those he loves. The chapter concludes the fourth part of the narrative as the city prepares for the inevitable arrival of Straff Venture's forces. Sazed begins preparing the fraudulent map and instructions for the journey to the Terris Mountains.



### Chapter 49

Tindwyl confronts Elend as he prepares his horses, accusing him of being a coward for abandoning Luthadel in its hour of need. Elend defends his decision, stating that he is seeking a larger solution to their problems and that his people have already rejected his leadership. He expresses total trust in Vin’s belief that she must reach the Well of Ascension to fulfill her role as the Hero of Ages. Tindwyl gives him a package for the Keeper Synod in Tathingdwen before departing in anger, refusing to join the exile. Elend, Vin, and Spook prepare to leave through the Tin Gate while the city's inhabitants watch with a mixture of fear and despondent acceptance.

Vin addresses a group of soldiers at the gate, promising them that they are not being abandoned and that help will eventually return. The group gallops out of the city and is quickly intercepted by a contingent of Straff Venture's cavalry and archers. Vin uses her immense Allomantic power to launch coins with lethal speed, killing many of the attackers before they can engage. She then leaps from her horse to create a massive cloud of pewter dust and ash, using a Push to blind and disrupt the enemy archers. This tactical distraction allows Elend and his companions to break through the blockade and reach the safety of the northern highway.

Allrianne Venture joins the group unexpectedly, revealing that Breeze influenced her to leave the city for her own safety. However, once they are clear of the initial pursuit, she veers off to find her father, Ashweather Cett, in the western hills. Back in Luthadel, the remaining crew members watch from the walls as their friends disappear toward the horizon. Sazed and Ham discuss the mysterious smoke rising from the koloss camp, which appears to be coming from a large, centralized fire. Straff Venture falls violently ill in his camp, suffering from tremors and dizziness caused by a long-term addiction to a drug disguised as an antidote.



### Chapter 50

Allrianne Cett wanders the western hills for a day before encountering a group of bandits led by a man named Hobart. She uses her Allomancy to Riot the bandits' emotions, convincing them to escort her safely to her father’s military camp. Upon arrival, Allrianne finds Lord Cett arguing with his generals about the failing supply lines and rebellions occurring in the Western Dominance. She informs her father that Elend and Vin have officially fled Luthadel, leaving the city vulnerable to occupation. Cett realizes that with the Mistborn gone, he can finally move his army into the city to claim the throne before Straff Venture recovers.

In Straff’s camp, the king discovers that his mistress Amaranta has been poisoning him and keeping him addicted to her "antidotes" for years. Straff kills Amaranta in a fit of rage but then collapses as he realizes he no longer has access to the regulated doses of the drug. He desperately mixes a random assortment of herbs from his cabinet and drinks the concoction, hoping it contains the substance his body craves. He falls into a deep unconsciousness, leaving his army without clear leadership as the koloss continue to wait outside Luthadel’s walls. The power dynamic of the siege shifts as both rival kings are incapacitated or focused on internal crises.

Jastes Lekal sits in his camp and struggles to maintain control over his twenty thousand koloss as his supply of fake wooden coins runs low. He watches the massive bonfire in the center of the camp, where his soldiers are burning the bodies of their fallen kin to stay warm. The koloss grow increasingly restless and violent, sensing the weakness of their master and the proximity of the city's inhabitants. Jastes realizes that the creatures will soon ignore his commands and storm Luthadel to satisfy their bloodlust and desire for spoils. The stage is set for a catastrophic three-way battle for the city as the various factions reach their breaking points.
