### Chapter 36

Sazed visits skaa refugees in a warehouse and treats a sick baby for a woman named Genedere, a grieving mother. Tindwyl arrives and finds Sazed exhausted from his long journey and his ministrations to the wounded. Breeze sits in the corner and uses emotional Allomancy to Soothe the refugees' despair from a distance. Elend and Ham visit the warehouse to observe the worsening conditions of the city's poor and sick. Elend wears a special white uniform made of ash-resistant fabric to maintain a regal image amidst the soot.

Breeze travels to the palace and meets with Clubs, a Smoker who uses his coppercloud to hide their conversation. Clubs remains immune to Breeze's Soothing and provides a rare moment of honest companionship for the Soother. Allrianne enters the room and Riots Breeze's emotions to increase his attraction toward her blond beauty. Vin watches the interaction with OreSeur and realizes Allrianne is a Misting or Mistborn using Allomancy. Vin follows Demoux as he sneaks out of the palace to attend a secret gathering in the city.

She finds Demoux leading a meeting of the Church of the Survivor in a city square. Demoux preaches that Vin is a divine figure who will eventually stop the ash and bring back the sun. He uses Kelsier's survival as a model for the skaa to endure the hardships of the current siege. Vin confronts Demoux after the meeting and learns that he truly believes in the religion's promises. She decides not to arrest him as a spy, concluding his devotion is genuine rather than a kandra's act.



### Chapter 37

Sazed and Tindwyl study Keeper records all night to investigate the ancient nature of the Deepness. They find a text mentioning King Wednegon, who blamed the Deepness for his lack of food and defeat. Tindwyl and Sazed conclude that the Deepness was actually the mists that once covered the world. Sazed fears that the Lord Ruler was the only force keeping the mists from killing people in the past. Tindwyl challenges Sazed's passivity and encourages him to take a leadership role among the Keepers.

Tindwyl decides to stay in Luthadel with Sazed instead of leaving to advise other kings in the region. She expresses deep resentment toward the Breeders who used her for childbearing for three decades of her life. Tindwyl holds Sazed's hand and describes him as the man who truly freed the Terris people. Sazed asks her to stay with him, and she agrees to continue their research into the mists together. Elend walks the city walls in the morning frost as the winter season begins to settle in Luthadel.

Ham observes that the soldiers have begun to respect Elend as a genuine military commander and leader. Elend watches skirmishes between Straff's and Cett's scouting patrols in the distance outside the city walls. He realizes that his previous rule lacked the necessary self-confidence and arrogance to maintain authority effectively. Elend reflects on his academic interest in the skaa and his genuine commitment to their survival. He tells Ham that he has developed a new strategy to secure his throne before the upcoming vote.



### Chapter 38

Elend and Vin arrive at the Assembly Hall early to greet representatives before the kingship vote begins. Elend wears a new white uniform while Vin wears a white gown designed for hidden mobility in combat. Penrod opens the meeting and invites the candidates to make their final addresses to the gathered Assembly. Elend shocks Vin by publicly joining the Church of the Survivor to win over the skaa representatives' votes. A Thug assassin suddenly leaps onto the stage and attacks Elend with a wooden dueling cane.

Vin intercepts the attacker and kicks him away as five more assassins join the fight on stage. Six assassins, consisting of Thugs and Coinshots, attempt to isolate and kill Elend and Vin during the chaos. Vin Pushes Elend’s sword and sheath as distractions while avoiding the use of coins near the crowded audience. She kills two assassins but suffers a shoulder wound and cracked ribs during the brutal struggle. OreSeur provides Vin with a hidden vial of metals after the Thug cracks the kandra's skull.

Vin burns duralumin and pewter to kill the remaining assassins, eventually head-butting the final Thug's head to pieces. She collapses from exhaustion and her wounds as Elend watches the blood-splattered scene in visible horror. The Assembly reconvenes at Penrod’s mansion later that day to finalize the voting process in private. The merchants and some skaa representatives switch their allegiances and vote for Penrod to become the new king. Elend officially loses his throne and hands his crown to Penrod before leaving the building in silence.



### Chapter 39

Straff Venture rides toward Luthadel in the mist accompanied by Zane and fifty of his soldiers. Straff reprimands Zane for wasting six valuable Allomancer children in the failed assassination attempt at the Assembly. Zane mutters to himself and reveals his growing mental instability to his increasingly frustrated father. They meet with Ferson Penrod, the newly elected king, near the city gates during the night. Penrod agrees to turn Luthadel over to Straff in exchange for noble titles and future stability.

Straff demands that Penrod locate the Lord Ruler’s hidden cache of atium within the city's walls. Penrod admits he is sad to see Elend’s idealism fail but accepts his role as a subject king. Straff dismisses Penrod and worries that Vin will seek retribution for the attack on Elend’s life. Zane mocks Straff's fear and checks his watch to see if his latest poison has taken effect. Straff feels the poison weakening his body and realizes Zane has successfully dosed him once again.

He gallops back to his camp in a panic, leaving his guards behind in his desperation to survive. Straff commands his mistress Amaranta, a healer who provides his antidotes, to prepare a concoction immediately. Amaranta provides a mixture that neutralizes the poison and restores Straff's physical strength and composure. Straff realizes that Zane has become too dangerous and unpredictable to remain a useful tool for long. He decides that he must find a way to kill his Mistborn son to ensure his own survival.



### Chapter 40

Vin wakes up in her bedroom with Elend sitting nearby in his old nobleman's clothing and vest. Elend explains that Penrod is now king and that he has accepted the Assembly's legal decision. He admits he was arrogant to believe the people would reinstate him without a serious fight. Vin notices Elend flinch when she touches him, realizing he is disturbed by her extreme violence. Elend leaves her to rest after bringing her a bowl of drugged broth prepared by Sazed's sedatives.

Zane enters Vin’s room through the open shutters while she is semi-conscious and slowly healing. Zane reveals that the assassins were actually sent by Lord Cett, not by his father Straff Venture. He explains that Cett faked an attack on himself to avoid suspicion after the merchants' betrayal. Zane gives Vin a nugget of atium and urges her to join him outside of the crew's control. Vin reflects on the choice between the safety of Elend's love and the freedom of Zane's power.

OreSeur visits Vin in a new wolfhound body and explains the function of the kandra, shape-shifting servants who mimic the bones they consume. He explains that kandra were created by the Lord Ruler as servants with built-in methods of control. OreSeur reveals that kandra theology views the Lord Ruler as their Father, despite his many flaws. Vin impulsively burns duralumin and Pushes on OreSeur's emotions, causing him to howl in physical pain. A shaken OreSeur warns Vin that her power is like that of the Allomancers of old before he leaves.
