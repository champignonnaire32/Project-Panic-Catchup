### Chapter 30: The Tolling of the Bell
Eragon recovers his sword Brisingr and his jewelry from the cathedral floor after the battle with the High Priest. He discovers that the belt of Beloth the Wise, a silk belt containing twelve diamonds for storing energy, is missing. Angela retrieves her sword Tinkledeath while Arya finds her own elven blade among the corpses. Eragon attempts a locating spell for the belt but finds only a gray emptiness indicating magical wards. Arya insists they leave immediately because the priory bell is alerting Murtagh and Thorn to their location.

Eragon, Arya, Angela, and Solembum flee the cathedral and run down the front steps into the city streets. Eragon contacts Saphira mentally and shares his memories of the underground tunnels and Wyrden's death. Saphira acknowledges his memories and prepares to descend into Dras-Leona to provide a distraction. Eragon instructs her to tell Nasuada to begin the Varden's full-scale assault on the city. The mental link with Saphira is fully restored as they coordinate their next moves.

Eragon abandons the search for the belt and follows Arya through the winding streets. Saphira tips into a steep dive from her high altitude to strike at the city. The four fugitives navigate through the waking city as the alarm continues to sound. Eragon runs toward the south gate to reach it before the arrival of the enemy dragon rider. They avoid the main thoroughfares to stay hidden from the gathering Empire soldiers.



### Chapter 31: Black-shrike- Thorn-cave
Saphira dives toward Dras-Leona while carrying the elf Blödhgarm, who is magically disguised as Eragon. She lands on the roof of the cathedral, which she identifies in her thoughts as the black-shrike-thorn-cave. The dragon looses a jet of blue and yellow flame to destroy the priory bell tower and silence its ringing. Blödhgarm uses a single word in the ancient language to kill a human magician who attacks them from the square. Thorn and Murtagh rise from the city battlements to challenge Saphira in the morning light.

Thorn dives at Saphira and breathes a wall of fire that Eragon's wards successfully deflect. Saphira leaps at Thorn, and the two dragons fall from the cathedral roof into the streets below. The impact jars Murtagh and cracks a bone in Thorn's shoulder, but the red dragon remains functional. Saphira takes flight again and sets fire to a row of wooden buildings to create further chaos. She returns to the cathedral and begins ripping the stone roof apart with her claws and teeth.

Saphira bats the cathedral's central spire three times until the stone spike collapses onto Thorn. The falling rubble buries the red dragon and forces Murtagh to expend energy on protective spells. Saphira proceeds to demolish the supporting buttresses, causing the entire cathedral structure to cave in. Thorn eventually lunges free from the debris with crumpled wings and bleeding gashes. Saphira hears Eragon's mental shout and retreats toward the southern arch-gate with Thorn in pursuit.



### Chapter 32: Hammer and Hflm
Roran watches from the Varden's camp as Saphira and Thorn engage in an aerial battle over the city. He pushes himself off a log and joins his men as they form a wide rectangular formation. Roran puts on his helm and draws his hammer while securing his shield to his left arm. Horst, the smith from Carvahall, approaches Roran to serve as his second-in-command for the assault. Roran struggles with nausea and stomach cramps from bad food but refuses to let the discomfort stop him.

The Varden's horns sound the advance, and Roran leads hundreds of men across the open fields. Four other battalions move in parallel with Roran's group toward the outskirts of Dras-Leona. Cries of alarm and the sounds of clanging bells erupt from the city as the defenders prepare. Roran observes the dragons occasionally appearing above the rooftops during their violent struggle. The army approaches a maze of ramshackle buildings that surround the main city walls.

Roran leads his men into the narrow, gloomy streets where the threat of ambush is high. The stench of the hovels and the shadows of the buildings exacerbate Roran's sense of physical unease. He realizes that street fighting in such close quarters will be exceptionally brutal for his troops. Roran licks his lips and prepares to attack as the sound of battle grows louder. The battalion waits in the shadows, knowing they will be trapped if the entrance remains closed.



### Chapter 33: And the Walls Fell...
Eragon and his companions witness the cathedral spire collapse into a column of dust as they navigate the streets. They encounter dozens of werecats in animal form watching them from the soot-coated chimneys and rooftops. Upon reaching the southern gate, Eragon finds hundreds of Empire soldiers and a massive mound of rubble blocking the way. Eragon and Arya charge the soldiers, using Brisingr and elven blades to cut through the surprised defenders. Angela throws glass vials that release a green vapor, causing lethal mushrooms to sprout on the soldiers' skin.

Saphira and Thorn continue their battle directly above the square, and dragon blood scalds the men below. Murtagh shouts from the sky that the soldiers are under his magical protection, nullifying Eragon's death spells. Eragon decides to tap into the massive store of energy within his ring, Aren. He releases a torrent of power and commands the rubble to erupt into the sky as a solid pillar. The flying debris strikes Thorn in the side, shredding his wing and knocking him out of the city.

Eragon directs the cloud of rubble toward Lake Leona before the energy in the ring is finally depleted. The city gates hang open and damaged, allowing the Varden to pour into Dras-Leona. Eragon sprints to rescue Arya after she is swarmed by twenty soldiers attempting to drag her away. He kills the attackers with his bare hands and a broken sword before reclaiming Brisingr from the ground. Murtagh and Thorn retreat to the north after swearing that they will have Eragon's blood in the future.



### Chapter 34: By THE BANKS OF LAKE LEONA

The Varden leaders meet to discuss the capture of Dras-Leona and inform Queen Islanzadí of Wyrden's death. Eragon hunts through the camp and confronts Elva about her refusal to prevent the tragedy at the cathedral. He blames her for Wyrden's death and Arya's injury, demanding she use her talents to help the Varden. Elva breaks down in tears, apologizes for her spite, and promises to assist in the fight against Galbatorix. Nasuada orders the army to camp north of the city by the lake to avoid potential Imperial agents.

Eragon and the elves return to the ruined cathedral to retrieve Wyrden's body but fail to find the belt of Beloth the Wise. They bury the fallen elf on a knoll and plant an acorn that magically grows into a twenty-foot oak tree. Eragon personally frees the slaves found in the city's manors and auction houses by cutting their chains. Arya visits Eragon's tent later that night and brings a wooden flask of modified elf liqueur called faelnirv. They drink the potent liquid together to cope with the trauma and exhaustion of the recent battle.

The faelnirv induces intense sensory hallucinations, causing Eragon to see flashing lights and hear lakeside insects with extreme clarity. Arya becomes intoxicated and reveals that Blödhgarm was unable to restore feeling to a patch of skin on her hand. She begins to dance a frenzied, languorous dance in the tent to the sound of a distant reed pipe. Eragon scratches an itchy palm and feels a ward flare just as a roar echoes from above. A dragon that does not belong to Saphira screams in the night, ending their brief moment of peace.
