### Chapter 10: No REST FOR THE WEARY

Roran waits outside Nasuada’s headquarters in Belatona while observing the Nighthawks, her personal guard of dwarves, men, and Urgals. He enters the room and watches Nasuada command her warriors and logistics officers with intense authority. Nasuada promotes Roran to the rank of captain and assigns him a mission to capture the city of Aroughs. She explains that Captain Brigman has failed to take the city, and the Varden needs its forces returned quickly. Roran receives maps of Aroughs and Fenmark Province along with his official commission and written orders.

The mission requires Roran to reach Aroughs in four days by riding fresh horses provided every ten miles to Feinster. Nasuada grants Roran’s request to take the magician Carn and several men from Carvahall with him. She criticizes Roran for his inability to read, noting that it makes him easy for others to manipulate. Roran asks to see Eragon for new protective wards, but Nasuada refuses his request. She states that Eragon is spread too thin and must conserve his strength for Galbatorix and Murtagh.

Roran accepts that he must fight without magical protection, as he did when the Ra’zac attacked Carvahall. Nasuada warns him not to burn down Aroughs during the siege because cities are difficult to replace. Roran prepares to depart immediately for the four-day ride to the southern city. He gathers his selected men and prepares for the grueling journey across the Empire. The chapter concludes with Roran leaving Belatona to begin his first command as a captain of the Varden.



### Chapter 11: Dancing With Swords
Eragon, Saphira, and Arya wait outside Belatona's eastern gate as the Varden army organizes for its march. The army consists of thousands of men, dwarves, and Urgals, accompanied by King Orrin’s cavalry and a long supply train. Eragon feels restless and begins practicing basic sword maneuvers with his blue blade, Brisingr, on a flat stretch of grass. He asks the elf Wyrden to spar with him using blunted blades and magical barriers to prevent injury. Wyrden defeats Eragon by landing four touches in quick succession on his shoulder, ribs, and abdomen.

Eragon then challenges Arya to a duel, noting that he has improved since they last fought in Farthen Dûr. Arya smiles to distract Eragon, then stabs him in the midsection and knocks him to the ground. Eragon recovers and they continue fighting, but Arya quickly traps him with an arm around his neck and her blade at his jaw. She tells Eragon he has grown lax in his defense and relies too much on brute strength against weak soldiers. Arya agrees to continue sparring with him and suggests he also seek instruction from the elf leader Blödhgarm.

A sudden mental presence from the golden dragon Glaedr touches the minds of Eragon, Saphira, and Arya. Glaedr speaks a single cryptic instruction to learn to see what they are looking at before withdrawing back into his sorrow. The three attempt to reach out to Glaedr’s heart of hearts in the saddlebags, but he ignores them completely. A horn signals the Varden to advance, and Saphira leaps into the sky to begin the journey toward Dras-Leona. Eragon and Saphira discuss Glaedr's grief and his refusal to communicate while they fly high above the marching army.



### Chapter 12: No Honor, No Giory, ONLY BLISTERS IN UNFORTUNATE PLACES

Roran and his five companions flee from manor guards and a pack of hunting dogs after stealing fresh horses. The group includes the magician Carn and the Carvahall villagers Mandel, Baldor, Delwin, and Hamund. They ride their horses at a gallop through rough terrain and hills to distance themselves from their pursuers. Roran ducks under thick branches and pushes his gelding to its physical limits despite the animal's labored breathing. The hunting hounds close the gap to within a quarter mile, forcing Roran to search for a place to hide.

The riders descend into a gorge, cross a stony creek, and find a hollow behind a wall of ferns. Roran and Baldor drag a fallen tree into place to create a makeshift barrier for their defense. Carn casts a spell that causes the hunting dogs to lose their scent and ignore the hollow. Roran observes that he and his men are covered in painful blisters from riding for three days with minimal sleep. He decides not to ask Carn to heal his sores yet to preserve the magician’s strength for the upcoming siege.

The hounds eventually follow a false trail upstream, allowing Roran and his men to emerge from their hiding spot. Hamund argues with Carn about the timing of the spell, but Roran intervenes to stop the dispute. Carn explains that while he can fool dogs, he cannot hide their physical trail from the human guards. Roran orders his men to remount their exhausted horses and continue the journey toward Aroughs. They gallop away from the ravine to resume their mission to replace Captain Brigman at the siege. Roran remains uncertain about how he will actually capture the city once they arrive at their destination.



### Chapter 13: Mooneater
Eragon walks through the Varden's camp near Leona Lake and observes a fishing village to the north. He reflects on Glaedr’s advice to see what he is looking at and tries to notice every small detail. This focused observation helps Eragon win a few matches against Arya, though he still considers her his superior. Eragon encounters a group of twelve Urgals, including Nar Garzhvog, sitting around a campfire with several dozen werecats. The werecats, including a white female resting on a Kull's head, interact peacefully with the large Urgals.

Angela the herbalist sits with the group, spinning wool and telling a story about a dragon named Mimring and a killer rabbit. Angela finishes her tale, and the Urgals grunt in approval while the werecats purr. Garzhvog gives Angela a sea-green crystal as payment for her story and invites her to hear Urgal chanters. Eragon asks about a place called Stavarosk, which triggers an angry reaction from Garzhvog and the other Urgals. Garzhvog explains that Stavarosk was the site of a massive Urgal victory where they slaughtered half of Galbatorix’s army.

Eragon realizes that humans only knew the king lost men in the Spine but never knew the Urgals were responsible. After the Urgals leave, Eragon sits with Angela and pets the white werecat, who is named Shadowhunter. Angela explains that Shadowhunter is the queen of the werecats and is mated to Grimrr Halfpaw. She tells Eragon that a group of dragons is called a "thunder" and explains why she is called "Mooneater." Angela recounts how she once cursed Grimrr Halfpaw to chirp like a bird for a week as punishment for torturing a robin.



### Chapter 14: Rumors and Writing
Eragon returns to his tent and greets Saphira before entering a deep state of meditation to observe his surroundings. He uses his enhanced hearing to listen to a group of Varden soldiers talking around a nearby watchfire. The soldiers express their distrust of the elves, describing them as beautiful but uninviting and potentially dangerous. One soldier, Svern, defends Eragon's loyalty, but others point out that Eragon looks more like an elf than a human. They discuss the lack of Riders and dragons to protect them from magical threats like Shades.

The men speak of a cruel imperial earl named Lord Barst who reportedly pulled a man’s head off with his bare hands. They speculate whether Barst’s unnatural strength comes from magic or if he is simply as strong as an Urgal. Eragon records these rumors in his mind and ends his meditation to begin his nightly studies. He takes out writing supplies and a copy of the history book Domia abr Wyrda given to him by Jeod. Eragon practices writing runes from his native language and records his thoughts on the day's events.

He also practices the Liduen Kvaedhí, the intricate writing script of the elves, to maintain his familiarity with it. Eragon continues compiling a dictionary of the ancient language to ensure he does not forget the spells he has learned. He retrieves the chest containing Glaedr’s heart of hearts and attempts to reach the dragon’s mind once again. Failing to get a response, Eragon reads aloud to Glaedr about dwarf rituals until the late hours of the night. He finally rests for a short period before waking at dawn to begin the next day of the march.
