### Chapter 20: I Need a Sword!

Eragon visits the Varden's armory and meets Fredric, the weapon master, to find a replacement for Zar'roc. Fredric explains that a sword should feel like an extension of the warrior's arm and is essential even for magic users. Eragon tests several blades but rejects them due to poor balance or improper hilt shapes for his hand. Fredric warns that non-enchanted blades will shatter if used edge-to-edge and suggests a metal mace, which Eragon refuses. They settle on a dwarf-made falchion, a single-edged sword featuring a hard tempered edge and a softer annealed spine for durability.

Eragon practices with the falchion, performing lunges and spins to test the weapon's speed and balance. Fredric provides a black leather scabbard and insists on teaching Eragon how to properly sharpen a battle-ready edge with a whetstone. While they work, Saphira lands nearby, having waited for the instruction to finish before joining Eragon. Eragon fastens the new weapon to the belt of Beloth the Wise and flies with Saphira high above the camp. They soar above the clouds and the Jiet River, enjoying a moment of solitude and discussing Roran's upcoming wedding.

Saphira performs a steep dive and roll for sport, pulling up just before hitting the ground. Eragon visits the healer Gertrude to learn and practice the marriage rites he will perform. He dines with King Orrin and his entourage before returning to his tent for the night. Eragon and Saphira walk back to his tent while discussing their past and future possibilities. They bid each other good night and settle in for the night's rest.



### Chapter 21: Unexpected Guests

Eragon performs the Rimgar exercises behind his tent and then practices with his new falchion for thirty minutes. He joins the villagers of Carvahall to help prepare for Roran and Katrina's wedding feast. Elain instructs Eragon to knead twenty loaves of bread dough while she oversees the cooking over beds of coals. The village women initially fall silent but eventually include Eragon in their conversation about the couple. Saphira basks on the grass while children play around her and she occasionally growls at barking dogs.

Roran struggles with a mule-driver and appears visibly nervous and short-tempered before his wedding ceremony. The village women make bawdy jokes about Roran, causing Eragon to flush and feel uncomfortable. Eragon thinks of Garrow and the villagers who died because of the Empire during the trek. Three loud, unnatural horn notes suddenly ring out across the land, followed by the Varden's war-drums. Chaos erupts in the camp as people scramble for weapons and Eragon quickly sprints to Saphira.

Eragon contacts the spellcasters of Du Vrangr Gata and Angela the herbalist to prepare for battle. Angela reports that Elva is overwhelmed by sensing great pain and must be put to sleep until the violence ends. Eragon quickly dons his mail hauberk, coif, and helm before mounting Saphira and flying north. At the camp's edge, Nasuada, King Orrin, and other leaders observe five black boats landing upon the Jiet River. Murtagh and the red dragon Thorn appear above the willow trees on the far side of the river.



### Chapter 22: Fire in the Sky

Arya warns Nasuada that the small force of soldiers is a trap designed to capture Eragon and Saphira. King Orrin leads his cavalry in a charge against the three hundred Imperial soldiers marching toward the camp. Nar Garzhvog sends the Urgals to assist the cavalry while Nasuada deploys swordsmen and archers. The soldiers emit a constant, high-pitched laughter and refuse to die even when suffering fatal wounds. Murtagh uses magic to amplify his voice and challenges Eragon to a duel in the sky.

Saphira launches into the air supported by the mental strength of thirteen elves led by Arya. Eragon offers Murtagh a way to break his oaths by changing his true name, but Murtagh refuses. Saphira breaks Thorn's wing with her tail, but Murtagh uses an enchanted object to instantly heal the dragon's bones and hide. Murtagh stabs Eragon in the hip with Zar'roc, but the elves quickly mend the wound with magic. Saphira catches Thorn in her mouth and clamps down on his wing while Eragon and Murtagh wrestle mentally.

Eragon casts a spell to restrain Murtagh, and the two Riders engage in a direct test of energy. Murtagh eventually yields and flees toward the Empire's army with the wounded Thorn. King Orrin beheads a laughing soldier who continued to crawl forward after being riddled with arrows. Nasuada orders the beheading of all Imperial soldiers to ensure they stay dead and to boost morale. Despite the victory, the Varden suffer hundreds of casualties against the magically altered soldiers who feel no pain.



### Chapter 23: Man and Wife

Eragon conducts the wedding of Roran and Katrina on a flower-covered hill by the Jiet River. The villagers sing traditional songs of Palancar Valley accompanied by an elven harpist. Roran and Katrina advance toward Eragon while two white doves place a circlet of daffodils on the bride's head. Horst and Birgit act as the representatives for the families since the parents are deceased. Roran publicly pledges to return to Carvahall and rebuild his farm, effectively vowing the Empire's downfall.

Birgit presents a dowry consisting of a metal casket filled with gold jewelry and diamonds provided by Nasuada. Eragon ties a red ribbon around the couple's wrists and declares them man and wife as a Dragon Rider. After the ceremony, Eragon gives Roran and Katrina two enchanted gold rings for protection and communication. Eragon also gifts Snowfire, Brom's former stallion, to Roran to serve as his warhorse. The wedding guests celebrate with mead, wrestling, archery, and songs performed by the elves and Arya.

Eragon and Nasuada visit the tents of the wounded and dying warriors after the sunset. Eragon mutters spells to ease pain and repair bones for those the regular healers cannot help. A maimed swordsman with pure white eyes describes seeing Eragon and Murtagh as blazing suns. The man warns that Murtagh is illuminated by hidden beings who project a sense of intense hostility. Eragon returns to his tent and reflects on the soldiers' sacrifice as Saphira sleeps.



### Chapter 24: Whispers in the Night

Roran wakes in his tent during the night and finds Katrina sitting by the entrance. He sits beside her and they watch the stars while discussing their future together and the coming child. Katrina expresses fear for their baby's safety and states her intention to leave the army after giving birth. Roran argues that they cannot hide from Galbatorix and that the Varden must achieve a complete victory. They decide that Katrina will eventually seek sanctuary in Dauth or the Beor Mountains if the camp remains unsafe.

Roran vows to kill Galbatorix and Murtagh before the baby is born to remove the need for flight. A miniature ship woven of dry grass sails through the air and hovers in front of their tent. The grass ship resembles a living creature and has a prow shaped like a dragon's head. It darts across the path and chases a moth before returning to hover near Katrina's face. The couple watches as the ship eventually flies off toward the morning star and disappears from view.

Roran and Katrina return to their cot as the first light of dawn begins to spread. They remain silent while contemplating the mysterious grass ship and the magic behind its flight. Roran feels a renewed sense of purpose in protecting his family from the Empire's reach. The morning star remains the only light in the sky as the rest of the stars fade away. They fall back into a light sleep together before the activities of the camp resume.
