### Chapter 10: A Delicate Matter

Roran heaves boulders in the practice field to maintain the strength he built as a blacksmith. He watches Lang disarm Baldor and Albriech during a sparring session with wooden swords. Roran checks his right shoulder and confirms the injury from the Ra’zac bite has fully healed. Birgit approaches Roran and warns him that she still intends to collect compensation for her husband's death. He cleans himself with a polished stick and water before meeting Katrina outside Horst’s large tent.

Katrina reveals that Elain is suffering from a difficult pregnancy and requires Eragon’s magical assistance. Roran walks to Nasuada’s command pavilion, which is guarded by humans, dwarves, and Urgals. Nasuada identifies Roran as a skilled leader but expresses concern over his independent streak and political influence. She refuses to let him lead the villagers of Carvahall until he proves he can obey military orders. Roran is offered a scouting mission under Martland Redbeard to destroy a patrol of twenty-three Empire soldiers.

Roran asks to delay the mission so Eragon can marry him and Katrina in a grand ceremony. He explains that he wants a Dragon Rider to perform the wedding to honor Katrina’s lost status. Nasuada grants the request and offers her own collection of gold and jewelry as Katrina’s dowry. She stipulates that Roran must be in the saddle the morning after the wedding takes place. Roran accepts the terms and vows to fight the Empire with the same determination he used in Palancar Valley.



### Chapter 11: Bloodwolf

Nasuada reviews inventory reports and military finances inside her red command pavilion while Elva hides behind a panel. A messenger sprints into the camp to announce the arrival of twelve elves from Queen Islanzadí. Nasuada rides her stallion, Battle-storm, to the northern entrance to greet the approaching spellcasters. Angela the herbalist joins her and discusses a man who cheated her at a game of knucklebones. The elves run across the plain with unnatural speed, appearing like a pack of predators chasing prey.

Captain Garven of the Nighthawks scolds Nasuada for riding through the camp without her full escort. Nasuada agrees to adopt a new motto for the guards and tasks them with devising a noble phrase. The lead elf, Blödhgarm, reveals a body covered in midnight-blue fur with yellow eyes and animalistic fangs. He emits a powerful masculine musk that causes Nasuada to experience intense physical and emotional reactions. Blödhgarm announces that the elven army has successfully captured the city of Ceunon and destroyed its gates.

Nasuada insists on verifying the elves' identities by having Garven touch their minds to detect human imposters. Garven performs the mental check but emerges in a dazed, sleepwalking state after contacting the elven consciousness. Elva tells Nasuada to concentrate on the taste of horehound to block the effects of Blödhgarm’s musk. Blödhgarm explains that he chose his animal appearance for its beauty and may one day become a sea creature. King Orrin arrives with a massive train of nobles just as Saphira lands and flattens the nearby grass.



### Chapter 12: Mercy, Dragon Rider

Eragon and Arya detect a patrol of fifteen Empire soldiers while traveling across an open, flat plain. They smear dirt on their skin and hunch their shoulders to appear like common refugees to the riders. The patrol commander halts his roan charger and mocks the pair for their lack of weapons and status. A soldier discovers Eragon’s fine chainmail hauberk hidden in his pack and reports the find to his superior. The commander declares they will be forced to swear the oath of service to Galbatorix and the Empire.

Eragon kills the commander by throwing a pebble through his helm with superhuman strength and precision. He stabs a soldier through the heart with a stolen spear before dodging seven incoming projectiles. Arya leaps between the horses and kills multiple soldiers using her hands and feet with lethal efficiency. Eragon snaps the spines of two men and staves in the chest of a fourth with a single punch. The final soldier flees on foot while weeping and begging for mercy for his youth and parents.

Eragon catches the fleeing man and strangles him to prevent the Empire’s magicians from learning their location. Arya washes the blood from her arms and questions Eragon’s choice to kill the soldier but spare Sloan. They use spears and swords to mutilate the corpses so the deaths appear to be from an ordinary skirmish. Arya uses the ancient language to heal Eragon’s disjointed knuckles and mangled skin after the fight. They leave the road and head southwest as carrion birds begin to circle the site of the massacre.



### Chapter 13: Shadows of the Past

Eragon and Arya eat a meal of raw roots and seeds gathered from the surrounding countryside. Eragon uses magic to grow thick, hornlike calluses on his knuckles to protect his hands during unarmed combat. He tests the new growths by punching a domed rock and confirms his fingers remain fully mobile. Eragon examines Brom’s ring, Aren, and discovers it contains a massive reservoir of energy stored over decades. Arya explains that the sapphire thrumming with power is an incredibly valuable resource for his future needs.

Arya describes her first kill as an ambassador and her method of finding peace through meditation. She recounts her torture in Gil’ead and how a soldier once left a white rose in her cell. Eragon sings a spell to grow a pale blue lily from a papery disk for Arya as a gift. A group of multicolored spirits arrives and forms a whirling wall of light and humming energy around them. Arya touches an orb and experiences radiant joy while the spirit scans her mind and memories.

Eragon touches a black orb that scours his consciousness and fills him with a sense of transcendent happiness. The spirits transform the blue lily into a living golden plant with ruby and diamond details before flying away. Arya explains the Banishing of the Names, a spell where dragons stripped the identities of the thirteen Forsworn dragons. She creates a tiny grass ship with rowers and railings using her hands and elven magic. Arya blows on the ship and sends it sailing forever into the night sky, powered by the plants below.



### Chapter 14: Amid the Restless Crowd

Eragon and Arya arrive at the Varden camp and view the separate Urgal camp led by Nar Garzhvog. Eragon reconnects with Saphira through a mental bond and they exchange memories of their time apart. Saphira dives from the clouds and breathes fire to celebrate her reunion with the Rider. Eragon climbs onto Saphira’s neck and settles into the hollow at the base of her head. Saphira touches Arya’s brow with her snout in a gesture of deep affection and family inclusion.

Twelve elven spellweavers, led by the fur-covered Blödhgarm, formally assume their duties as protectors of the Rider. Saphira notes that Blödhgarm’s musk attracts human women but that Nasuada is protected by a magical ward. Eragon enters the camp and is hailed by a cheering crowd as Argetlam and the Bane of the Ra’zac. He greets the villagers of Carvahall, including Horst, Birgit, and the families who fled Palancar Valley. Birgit thanks him for the death of the Ra’zac but reminds him that his personal debt remains unpaid.

Roran and Katrina embrace Eragon, though Roran scolds his cousin for abandoning the group at Helgrind. Eragon kisses the couple on their brows and promises to act as a brother to them forever. Nasuada stands before her red pavilion in a green silk dress with her arms still covered in bandages. King Orrin provides a formal welcome to the returning travelers while the Varden cheers for Nasuada’s leadership. Eragon gives a brief, fumbling speech to the multitude before retreating in embarrassment as Saphira chuckles.
