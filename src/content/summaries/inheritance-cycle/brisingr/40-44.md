### Chapter 40: Ascension

The Drums of Derva signal the start of Orik’s coronation as King of the Dwarves in Tronjheim. Eragon and Saphira join the gathering of clan chiefs and guild leaders in the central chamber of the city-mountain. Artisans complete the reconstruction of the star sapphire, known as Isidar Mithrim, which rests in a wooden framework. Orik marches through the eastern gate wearing the gold mail hauberk and the war hammer Volund. Thousands of dwarf warriors and Orik’s wife, Hvedra, witness the king-elect kneel before the black granite throne.

Gannel, the chief of Dûrgrimst Quan, invokes the dwarf god Gûntera using the ancient language. A shimmering, shaggy-haired apparition with massive arms and a wide head materializes above the crowd. This figure places the gem-encrusted helm of King Hrothgar onto Orik's head before vanishing. The assembled dwarves strike their shields and shout their allegiance to their new monarch. Eragon and the various clan chiefs, excluding the banished Vermûnd, formally pledge their service to Orik.

Eragon presents Orik with an enchanted gold armlet designed to repel physical weapons and hostile magic. Saphira uses her own magic to mend the fractures within the massive star sapphire. The mended jewel takes on a deeper red hue with streaks of dusky gold embedded in the petals. Orik thanks Saphira and declares that her name will be engraved alongside the jewel's original creator. The day concludes with a boisterous banquet where Orik confirms the coronation was a genuine divine encounter.



### Chapter 41: Words of Wisdom

Eragon uses a scrying spell to contact Nasuada through a basin of water in Tronjheim. Nasuada congratulates Eragon on securing Orik's kingship and maintaining the alliance between the Varden and the dwarves. She reports that Roran survived a recent military engagement but Captain Edric’s company suffered heavy casualties. Nasuada explains that the Varden are currently advancing on the city of Feinster and require a dragon Rider's presence. Eragon insists that he and Saphira must return to Ellesméra to consult Oromis and Glaedr regarding Galbatorix’s power.

Nasuada expresses concern that Murtagh and Thorn might destroy the Varden if Eragon departs. Eragon argues that victory is impossible without further training and knowledge from their elven mentors. Nasuada eventually agrees to delay the final assault on Feinster for several days to buy them time. She warns Eragon that every day of delay risks the lives of the Varden and their supplies. Eragon calculates that the flight to Ellesméra and then to Feinster will take at least a week.

The conversation ends with Nasuada urging Saphira to fly with the speed of a diving hawk. Eragon terminates the scrying spell and the water in the basin returns to its normal state. Saphira prepares for the long journey across Alagaësia while Eragon reflects on the sacrifice being made. The duo plans to bypass the normal speed of travel to reach the forest of Du Weldenvarden. They must depart Tronjheim immediately to maximize the narrow window of time Nasuada granted them.



### Chapter 42: The Whipping Post

Nasuada informs Roran that he must be punished for his insubordination despite the success of his mission. She offers him the choice of fifty lashes to remain in the Varden or thirty lashes followed by a discharge. Roran elects to stay and accepts the penalty of fifty lashes in public view. The sorceress Trianna examines Roran and heals a crossbow wound on his shoulder before the punishment. Katrina visits Roran in his holding tent and unsuccessfully begs Nasuada to pardon him or reduce the sentence.

Roran is tied to a six-foot-high post on a rise just beyond the Varden camp. He bites down on a leather-wrapped dowel provided by Jörmundur to prevent himself from screaming. The man wielding the whip delivers the fifty lashes while Jörmundur reads the official charges. Roran loses consciousness during the ordeal but refuses to beg for the flogging to stop. Afterward, the herbalist Angela and the sorceress Trianna apply medicinal ointments to the raw stripes on his back.

Nasuada visits Roran’s tent and assigns him a new mission to lead a raid on a supply train. This raid will consist of equal numbers of humans and Urgals to test the stability of their alliance. Nasuada explains that Roran’s status as Eragon’s cousin and his battlefield reputation make him the best candidate. She orders the magicians to heal his back more than usual so he can depart the following day. Roran accepts the command and prepares to lead the integrated force of warriors.



### Chapter 43: Among the Clouds

Eragon and Saphira travel through the miles-long tunnel leading east from the base of Farthen Dûr. They emerge in Odred Valley and stop at the lake Fernoth-mérna to replenish their water supplies. Saphira flies northward toward the grasslands, keeping a high altitude to move through the thinner air. Eragon uses magic to shield himself from the freezing wind and the friction of high-speed flight. They land at night near a stream to rest Saphira’s wings before continuing the journey.

A fierce headwind slows their progress across the plains the following morning. Saphira refuses to stop or use the energy stored in the ring Aren, insisting on flying through the gale. Eragon offers a prayer to Gûntera, the dwarf king of the gods, asking for the wind to abate. The wind eventually dies down late that night, allowing them to reach the Hadarac Desert by morning. Saphira catches a brace of ducks with her fire to sustain herself during the final leg.

The duo reaches the edge of Du Weldenvarden as the sun sets and Saphira enters the forest on foot. Eragon scries Oromis and asks if their training can be conducted at a distance through the pool. Oromis refuses and insists that they must return to Ellesméra to discuss certain subjects in person. Eragon agrees and prepares to search for a new weapon under the roots of the Menoa tree. Saphira takes flight once more, carrying Eragon toward the elven capital of Ellesméra.



### Chapter 44: Butting Heads

Roran and his combined force of humans and Urgals successfully ambush a supply train in a ravine. The Urgals stop the wagons from the front while the horsemen strike the line from the side. Three warriors are wounded in the skirmish, but the Varden force captures the entire procession of goods. Roran finds an Urgal named Yarbog torturing a captive soldier and kills the man to end his suffering. Yarbog challenges Roran’s leadership, claiming that humans are too weak to command the Bolvek tribe.

The two combatants strip naked and are covered in bear grease for an unarmed wrestling match. Roran chooses to fight without his hammer, shield, or helmet to match Yarbog's lack of weapons. Yarbog uses his horns to ram Roran’s shoulder and toss him across the dirt square. Roran evades several charges before grabbing Yarbog’s horns and pinning the Urgal to the ground. He twists Yarbog's head and maintains the hold for ten minutes despite his own exhaustion.

Yarbog eventually submits and acknowledges Roran as a worthy chieftain in front of the assembled warriors. Carn wraps Roran in a blanket and provides him with wine to recover from the physical strain. The Urgals remain subdued but respectful of Roran after he proves his strength in the duel. Roran orders the men to inventory the wagons and prepare to return to the Varden camp. He realizes that this victory has preserved the alliance between the two races for the time being.
