### Chapter 50: Hunting for Answers

The bald man, Egraz Carn, leads Eragon and Murtagh into a white marble room and secures the door with a bolt. Warriors take Arya to the healers after Eragon explains she was poisoned with Skilna Bragh, a deadly toxin. Egraz Carn demands to inspect Eragon’s mind to ensure he does not serve King Galbatorix. Saphira joins her mind with Eragon’s to provide strength during the painful mental probe. Eragon allows the bald man to see most of his memories but has Saphira conceal information about Angela’s fortune and Murtagh’s true identity.

After the probe, the bald man declares Eragon is not an enemy and turns his attention to Murtagh. Murtagh refuses to open his mind and maintains his resistance even when threatened with death. The bald man attempts to force his way into Murtagh’s mind, causing Murtagh visible physical agony. Orik, a dwarf warrior, intervenes and stops the bald man, arguing that they have no right to torture Murtagh. The bald man communicates silently with Ajihad before ordering Eragon and Murtagh to stay in the room for the night.

Alone in the room, Eragon uses the ancient language to heal the arrow wounds on Saphira’s wings. Murtagh confesses to Eragon that he is the son of Morzan, the first and greatest of the Forsworn. He explains that his mother, Selena, died shortly after his birth and Morzan gave him the scar on his back. Murtagh fled Galbatorix’s court after the King ordered him to slaughter the innocent people of Cantos. Eragon and Murtagh eat the provided meal and fall asleep under the protection of Saphira’s wing.



### Chapter 51: The Glory of Tronjheim

Eragon and Murtagh are woken by warriors and told they have been summoned to meet Ajihad. Orik leads them through a massive, perfectly square tunnel that has been mined into the heart of the mountain. They ride single file toward a soft white glow that marks the center of the subterranean complex. The tunnel ends at two colossal black doors decorated with silver depicting a seven-pointed crown. Eragon mounts Saphira as the doors open to reveal the massive volcanic crater of Farthen Dûr.

Farthen Dûr is a hollow mountain with walls that narrow to a small opening miles above the floor. In the center of the crater stands Tronjheim, a city-mountain made of polished white marble that is over a mile high. A dense crowd of thousands of humans and dwarves lines the cobblestone path leading to the city. Eragon waves to the crowd, prompting a wild roar of cheers that echoes throughout the crater. Saphira parades toward the city, exhaling smoke to the delight and awe of the gathered Varden.

They reach the base of Tronjheim, which is guarded by two thirty-foot-high gold griffins at the timber gate. The group enters a mile-long hall lined with fluted pillars of blood-red jasper and statues of strange creatures. The hall ends in a circular room a thousand feet across that reaches up to the peak of the mountain. A giant star sapphire called Isidar Mithrim, carved like a rose, caps the ceiling a mile overhead. The bald man leads Eragon and Murtagh on foot to a massive cedar door where Ajihad waits.



### Chapter 52: Ajihad

Eragon enters a two-story study and meets Ajihad, the ebony-skinned leader of the Varden. Ajihad reveals that the bald man has an identical twin brother and that the two possess powerful magic. Upon seeing the scar on Murtagh’s back, Ajihad identifies him as the son of Morzan. Murtagh refuses to be mind-probed, so Ajihad orders him imprisoned in a windowless room for security. Eragon spends hours recounting his journey, including the death of Brom and his battle with the Shade Durza.

Ajihad explains that Durza survived the encounter because a Shade can only be killed by a thrust through the heart. He details the history of Saphira’s egg and the agreement to rotate it between the Varden and the elves. Arya is the daughter-courier of the egg who disappeared during an ambush in Du Weldenvarden. Ajihad reveals that Galbatorix is gathering an army of Urgals at Ithrö Zhâda, a place where Galbatorix is gathering an army, to destroy the Varden. The Varden have been struggling since the elves withdrew their support following the egg's disappearance.

Ajihad warns Eragon that he is now a political figure and must be careful with his influence. He returns Zar’roc to Eragon but advises him not to carry it openly because of the hatred for Morzan. Eragon receives Brom’s ring, a sapphire carved with an elven symbol, as his inheritance. Saphira and Ajihad establish a mutual respect, though she threatens to destroy Tronjheim if Eragon is harmed. Orik is assigned as Eragon’s guide and instructed to take him to the training fields.



### Chapter 53: Bless the Child, Argetlam

Orik explains to Eragon that Ajihad cleverly punished him by making him a guide, which keeps him in a position of power. Saphira flies to the dragonhold, a series of marble caves at the open peak of Tronjheim. Eragon and Orik eat a meal of mushrooms and fish at stone tables designed for dwarves. Orik describes the subterranean world of the dwarves and their ability to travel under the entire Beor Mountain range. The dwarf explains that Tronjheim is a city-mountain built to house the entire dwarf nation during times of war.

Eragon learns that the Varden are supported by King Orrin of Surda, a human nation to the south. Orik identifies himself as a member of the Ingietum clan, who are renowned metalworkers and smiths. A dwarf in the hallway bows to Eragon and calls him Argetlam, which means "silver hand" in the elven tongue. Eragon bathes in a warm, salty subterranean pool to wash away the grime of his long journey. Orik provides Eragon with new clothes, including a fine linen shirt and breeches, to replace his torn rags.

An old woman stops Eragon near the city gate and begs him to bless her orphaned baby for luck. Eragon uses the ancient language to intone a blessing that he hopes will forestall tragedy for the child. Saphira touches the baby's forehead with her snout, leaving a silvery, star-shaped mark on the skin. Saphira explains to Eragon that the blessing has given the child a future and will lead to a new legend. They retire to a cave in the dragonhold where Eragon finds a bed placed next to Saphira’s cushion.



### Chapter 54: Mandrake Root and Newt’s Tongue

Eragon wakes up in the dragonhold feeling secure for the first time since leaving home. He sees Solembum the werecat, a creature that can shift between a boy and a small red-eyed cat. Solembum leads Eragon down the Vol Turin, the Endless Staircase, which spirals around the central chamber of Tronjheim. They enter a hidden, plant-filled suite where Eragon finds the herbalist Angela. Angela is hiding in the upper levels of the city to avoid being pestered by the Varden’s magic users.

Angela reveals she knew the Varden’s egg had hatched and traveled to Tronjheim to witness the upcoming events. She explains that Brom was cursed to fail at almost every task except for the killing of Morzan. Angela clarifies that a Shade is created when a sorcerer is possessed by evil spirits they cannot control. She expresses a violent hatred for Shades and suggests they are the greatest immediate threat to the Varden. Angela refuses to let the Twins probe her mind, threatening to turn them into frogs if they try.

Eragon summarizes his adventures for Angela, including his travels with Murtagh and his escape from Gil'ead. Angela warns Eragon to remain cautious of Murtagh despite his help during their flight. She mentions that she has been coming to Farthen Dûr long before the Varden began their security mind-probes. Angela dismisses Eragon so she can attend to a boiling potion made of mandrake root and newt’s tongue. Solembum guides Eragon back to the dragonhold before disappearing into the corridors of the city.
