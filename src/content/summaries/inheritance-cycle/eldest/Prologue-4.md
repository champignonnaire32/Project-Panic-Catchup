### Prologue: Synopsis of Eragon, Book One of Inheritance

Eragon is a fifteen-year-old farmboy who finds a blue dragon egg in the Spine mountains. He lives with his uncle Garrow and cousin Roran on a farm near the village of Carvahall. The egg hatches into Saphira, a blue dragon, and a silver mark appears on Eragon's palm. The Ra'zac, beetle-like agents of King Galbatorix, destroy the farm and kill Garrow while searching for the egg. Eragon flees with Brom, the village storyteller, who reveals himself as a former Dragon Rider.

Brom gives Eragon the sword Zar'roc and teaches him swordplay and magic before being killed by the Ra'zac. Eragon is joined by Murtagh, the son of the traitor Morzan, and they rescue the elf Arya from the city of Gil'ead. They escape to Farthen Dûr, a hollow mountain that houses the Varden rebels and the dwarf city Tronjheim. Murtagh reveals his identity and is imprisoned by the Varden leader Ajihad because of his father's crimes. Eragon is tested by the Twins, two bald human magicians, and meets King Hrothgar of the dwarves.

An Urgal army sent by the Shade Durza attacks Farthen Dûr through the dwarves' tunnels. Eragon fights Durza alone and kills him by stabbing him through the heart while Saphira and Arya distract the Shade. The battle leaves Eragon with a massive scar stretching from his shoulder to his hip. While unconscious, a being named Togira Ikonoka contacts Eragon telepathically and tells him to come to the elven city Ellesméra. Eragon decides to seek out this "Cripple Who Is Whole" to complete his training as a Dragon Rider.



### Chapter 1: A Twin Disaster

Eragon walks through the battle-scarred plains of Farthen Dûr three days after killing the Shade Durza. Saphira follows him as he observes women removing bodies and Urgal corpses being burned in massive pyres. Eragon suffers from agonizing back pains caused by the scar Durza left on him during their duel. He meets Jörmundur, Ajihad’s second-in-command, who informs him that Ajihad is returning from hunting remnant Urgals. Eragon joins Orik the dwarf and Arya to wait for Ajihad’s arrival at the west gate of Tronjheim.

Ajihad emerges from a tunnel with a group of warriors, including Murtagh and the Twins. A group of Urgals suddenly ambushes the party from behind, and Eragon watches helplessly from Saphira’s back as they are surrounded. The Urgals quickly kill several men and force the survivors to cluster around Ajihad. A strange mist-like magical effect swirls around the combatants, leaving only four standing when it clears. The Urgals retreat back into the tunnel, dragging the Twins and Murtagh underground with them.

Eragon finds Ajihad dying in the center of the massacre with his breastplate torn by numerous gashes. Ajihad makes Eragon promise not to let the Varden fall into chaos before he passes away. Arya blesses the dead leader and immediately pursues the Urgals into the tunnels alone. Jörmundur and Orik arrive to find the aftermath, but they cannot send reinforcements into the maze-like tunnels yet. The Varden warriors carry Ajihad’s body back to Tronjheim on their shields while mourning their fallen leader.



### Chapter 2: The Council of Elders

Eragon wakes in his new quarters on the bottom level of Tronjheim and watches Saphira practice breathing fire. Arya returns from the tunnels and reports that she found the bloodied clothes of Murtagh and the Twins near a deep chasm. She states that they were likely killed and thrown into the pit, as she could not find them with magic. Eragon attempts to scry Murtagh using a tear in his palm but sees only darkness. He realizes that his only path forward is to travel to the elves for further training.

A second crippling seizure strikes Eragon, causing him to collapse in pain while attempting to lift a saddle. Once the fit passes, a boy named Jarsha arrives to summon Eragon to the Council of Elders. Eragon meets the council, which consists of Jörmundur, Umérth, Falberd, Sabrae, and Elessari. The council members explain that they need Eragon’s support to maintain legitimacy and prevent the Varden from splintering. They reveal that they have chosen Nasuada, Ajihad’s daughter, to be the next leader of the Varden.

The council admits they want to use Nasuada as a figurehead while they guide her through her inexperience. They demand that Eragon attend the appointment ceremony and swear fealty to the Varden to secure public confidence. Eragon realizes they are trying to trap him but feels he has no choice and agrees to their terms. Saphira notes that the council excludes her from the oath, meaning she remains technically independent. Jörmundur calls for Nasuada and Arya to join them, and Nasuada formally accepts the offer to lead.



### Chapter 3: Truth Among Friends

The Council of Elders celebrates Nasuada's acceptance while Arya gives a neutral blessing to the new leader. Nasuada dismisses the council members, but asks Eragon and Saphira to remain behind in the chamber. Eragon casts a privacy spell to prevent any magical or physical eavesdropping by the dwarves or elves. Nasuada reveals that she knows the council intends to use her as a puppet but plans to assert her own authority. She states her intention to follow her father's strategy and eventually send Eragon to the elves for training.

Eragon kneels before Nasuada and offers her his blade, Zar'roc, as a symbol of his personal fealty. He explains that he is swearing to her specifically to circumvent the council's attempt to control him. Nasuada accepts his vow and instructs him to repeat the oath in public during the upcoming ceremony. She reminds him that as her vassal, she is responsible for his actions and he is required to serve her. Nasuada offers her condolences for the loss of Murtagh before dismissing the Dragon Rider to his duties.

Saphira leads Eragon to the library of Tronjheim, where they find Arya sitting in a reading alcove. Arya is initially hostile, accusing Eragon of siding with the council to control Nasuada and undermining her work. Eragon explains the true nature of his secret oath to Nasuada, which pacifies the elf’s anger. Arya reveals that she has spent seventy years serving the Varden and the elven queen as an ambassador. She tells Eragon that he must wait until after Ajihad’s funeral before departing for his training in Ellesméra.



### Chapter 4: Roran

Roran returns to the ruins of his family farm in Palancar Valley for the first time since its destruction. He finds the house flattened and the fields overgrown with weeds and wild vegetables. The sight fills him with rage and grief, as the farm represented his entire past and planned future. Roran blames Eragon for the death of his father Garrow and for abandoning the valley with Brom. He decides that he must rebuild the farm from nothing if he hopes to marry the butcher’s daughter.

Roran walks back to the village of Carvahall and takes up residence in the home of Horst the blacksmith. He works in the forge and hunts deer in the valley to contribute to the household's food supply. At the Seven Sheaves tavern, a trapper shares news of Urgal disappearances and troop movements within the Empire. The trapper also mentions rumors of a new Dragon Rider and a Shade haunting the northern territories. Roran dismisses the Rider stories as wishful thinking but worries about the possibility of an upcoming war.

Katrina, the daughter of the butcher Sloan, meets Roran at the edge of the village under the stars. Roran tells her that they must delay their marriage until he can harvest a crop and rebuild the house. Katrina warns him that her father is pressing her to accept a more eligible suitor with better prospects. She demands that Roran ask for her father's permission immediately to secure an official engagement. Roran refuses because he has no means to provide for her yet, and Katrina leaves him in tears.
