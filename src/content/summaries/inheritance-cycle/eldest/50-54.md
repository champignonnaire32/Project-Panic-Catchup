### Chapter 50: Landfall
Roran stands on the poop deck of the Red Boar, a flat-bottomed transport barge, as it nears the coastline. The sailor Clovis identifies a landmark near Teirm and Roran orders the three barges to beach for a camp. Villagers settle into temporary tents while Roran offers encouragement to Morn and Tara, the despondent tavern owners. Felda requests that Roran speak to her son Mandel, who has grown willful and is gambling with the sailors. Roran promises to address the issue and notices other villagers forming problematic bonds with the boat crews.

Roran gathers his trusted allies and instructs Horst to command the camp while he scouts the city. He threatens Clovis to ensure the barges remain present or face Roran's personal vengeance upon his return. Clovis questions if Roran will ever return to farming after his experiences leading the village flight. Roran admits his primary goal after reaching Surda is to hunt for his kidnapped fiancée, Katrina. He visits Felda's tent and finds Mandel practicing with a hunting knife, which Roran dismisses as an ineffective weapon.

Roran commands Mandel to join the expedition to Teirm to keep him away from the influence of the sailors. Felda assists her son in packing his kit while asking Roran to ensure the young man's safety. The scouting party, including Loring, Gertrude, Birgit, and Nolfavrell, meet on a hill past a local stream. Birgit objects to Mandel joining because he was previously seen by Empire soldiers in the town of Narda. Roran overrules the objection and the six companions begin walking south toward the fortified city of Teirm.



### Chapter 51: Teirm
The group treks through muddy hills between the ocean and the Spine, a massive range of snowcapped mountains. They bypass the city to the east before approaching from the south to disguise their path from Narda. Roran fears that soldiers or the Ra’zac, beetle-like monsters in black cloaks, have alerted the local guards. He selects himself and the healer Gertrude to enter the city despite the high risk of being recognized. The party reaches the white walls of Teirm at evening and observes soldiers patrolling the stone battlements.

Roran identifies the Dragon Wing, a massive three-masted ship with twelve ballistae, as the ideal vessel for the villagers. The companions enter the gates among the evening traffic while Mandel follows separately to avoid drawing attention. They secure floor space at the Green Chestnut, a cheap and flea-infested tavern, to preserve their coin. Roran sends Mandel to guard Gertrude as she seeks the herbalist Angela to purchase specific medicinal ingredients. Roran scouts the docks and learns the Dragon Wing is currently owned by the Blackmoor Shipping Company.

He realizes the village lacks the gold necessary to hire a ship or buy adequate travel provisions. Loring mentions their need for a vessel to a baker’s assistant while the group buys a loaf of bread. A local man overhears and suggests they visit Jeod, a merchant whose property is being sold at auction. Roran decides to approach Jeod immediately to negotiate a deal before the public sale begins. The group enters Jeod’s mansion near the citadel after a tense confrontation with a butler named Rolf.



### Chapter 52: Jeod Longshanks
Roran meets Jeod, a tall merchant with graying hair and a white scar running from scalp to temple. Jeod offers the travelers apricot brandy and pastries while the group provides various false names as aliases. Roran explains that they represent a large group of people who need to purchase supplies for a voyage. He proposes buying items directly from Jeod before the upcoming bankruptcy auction scheduled for the following day. Jeod explains that his assets are controlled by creditors and he cannot legally sell them without permission.

The butler Rolf serves refreshments to the group while Roran lists the items the villagers require for survival. Jeod listens with growing intensity as the list of provisions reveals the group must consist of hundreds. The merchant deduces that Roran is hiding a large host nearby, though Roran refuses to confirm the suspicion. Jeod recommends several honest merchants, including Galton and Hamill, who might provide food and ship equipment. The conversation turns to the shop next door, which Jeod explains was recently vacated by the herbalist Angela.

Nolfavrell mentions that Gertrude intended to visit Angela, which causes Roran to glare at the boy in warning. Jeod reacts with extreme shock at the mention of the name and begins studying Roran's face closely. The merchant grips his chair until his knuckles turn white as he connects the clues of the party's identity. Roran prepares for a potential conflict while the merchant compares his features to those of his cousin Eragon. Jeod breaks the silence by correctly addressing the young man as Roran Garrowsson, his true name.



### Chapter 53: An Unexpected Ally
Roran draws his hammer while his companions pull knives to threaten Jeod for revealing his knowledge. Jeod remains calm and explains he is a friend of Brom, the former storyteller from Roran's home village. The merchant reveals that Eragon is a Dragon Rider and that Saphira, his dragon, has already visited Teirm. Roran is stunned by the news and barricades the study door to prevent any interruptions from the servants. Jeod promises to explain why the Empire is hunting Roran if the group shares their own story first.

Roran describes the siege of Carvahall, the kidnapping of Katrina, and the difficult journey the villagers have endured. Jeod’s wife Helen pounds on the door and insults her husband, but Jeod commands her to be silent. Jeod admits he is a secret agent for the Varden, a rebel group dedicated to overthrowing King Galbatorix. He explains that Eragon’s dragon egg was stolen from the king and hatched specifically for Roran's cousin. Roran reacts with hysterical laughter at the absurdity before feeling intense rage toward Eragon for his father's death.

Jeod identifies the Ra’zac’s lair as Helgrind, a bare black mountain of stone located near the city of Dras-Leona. He warns Roran that a normal human cannot defeat the Ra’zac or their flying mounts without a Rider's help. The merchant suggests the villagers travel to Surda to meet the Varden and eventually reunite with Eragon. Jeod reveals that his cover is blown and he must flee Teirm to avoid arrest by the local governor. He proposes stealing the Dragon Wing, a fast ship fully provisioned with food, to transport the entire village.



### Chapter 54: Escape
Roran moves the party into Jeod’s mansion while the merchant confesses his secret rebel life to Helen. Birgit confronts Roran about his responsibility for her husband's death and warns him that she still seeks revenge. Mandel is sent back to the camp with instructions to disable the barges and bring the villagers to Teirm. Roran reviews the plan to seize the Dragon Wing with the scouting party and Jeod's trusted sailor allies. The group gathers at the mansion entryway before dawn, muffled in cloaks to hide their identities from guards.

They navigate to the wharf and meet five sailors, including a large brute named Uthar who will captain. Birgit kicks Uthar to prove her combat prowess after the sailors object to a woman joining the fight. Roran and Uthar swim through freezing harbor water and climb anchor chains to infiltrate the ship's deck. They knock out the sentries with clubs and capture the remaining crew members before Helen arrives to join. The team dumps heavy cargo like raw wool overboard to make room for the hundreds of arriving villagers.

Nolfavrell uses a lantern to light and fire flaming javelins from the ballistae, setting the waterfront buildings ablaze. The fire creates a smoke screen and a physical barrier that prevents city soldiers from reaching the ship. The villagers board the Dragon Wing in a frantic rush and the ship pulls away under oar power. A Ra’zac appears on a flying mount over the coast, but Baldor wounds it with a longbow shot. Roran watches the burning city and realizes the Empire now knows their location and their intended destination.
