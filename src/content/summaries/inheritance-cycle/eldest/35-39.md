### Chapter 35: A Maze of Opposition

Nasuada acts as a judge in Aberon to resolve a theft case involving a Varden member named Gamble and a local farmer. She orders Gamble to pay a copper for each of the thirteen chickens he stole and consumed. The Varden organization struggles with short rations and a lack of funds while residing in Surda. Nasuada laments the difficulty of integrating the Varden into Surda while preparing for an upcoming offensive against Galbatorix. She orders Gamble reassigned to a quartermaster position where he will receive full rations and utilize his verbal skills.

Nasuada visits King Orrin of Surda in his castle laboratory where he conducts natural philosophy experiments. Orrin demonstrates a trick where he blows smoke from cardus weed, a common smoking plant, out of his unhealed eardrum. He shows Nasuada a vacuum created within a glass tube of quicksilver to prove the existence of "nothing." Nasuada criticizes the king for focusing on scientific curiosities while his country prepares for a massive war. Orrin defends his hobby as a necessary mental break to maintain his sanity amidst the pressures of leadership.

Orrin and Nasuada discuss reports indicating that Galbatorix is emptying garrisons and confiscating livestock throughout the Empire. Nasuada reveals she is spreading rumors of Eragon’s prowess to encourage imperial cities to surrender without a siege. Orrin explains the logistical difficulties of raising his own army and gathering necessary armor and supplies. When Nasuada asks for more gold to feed the Varden, Orrin refuses and tells her she must raise the funds herself. Nasuada departs in a fury and accidentally spills a jade bottle of yellow liquid onto her expensive overgown.



### Chapter 36: Hanging by a Thread

Nasuada returns to her chambers and realizes that the liquid spilled in Orrin's lab is burning through her clothes. Farica helps her remove the disintegrating overgown, which was a gift from the dwarf women of Dûrgrimst Ingeitum. The acid-like substance leaves the gown in ruined, chalky cobwebs, forcing Nasuada to consider her limited wardrobe. Farica suggests altering a casual linen dress to make it grand enough for Nasuada to wear at court. Nasuada paces the room in frustration, worrying about the Varden's poverty and her own inability to provide for them.

While helping Farica rip out stitches, Nasuada accidentally cuts a piece of valuable bobbin lace on the ruined dress. She reflects on the extreme value of lace, which is derived from the immense amount of time required for human production. This realization triggers a plan to use the magic of Du Vrangr Gata to manufacture lace for profit. Nasuada summons Trianna, the brunette leader of the Varden's magic users, to her chambers for an urgent meeting. She ignores the initial hesitation of her handmaid regarding the use of magic practitioners.

Trianna arrives and Nasuada questions her on the fundamental principles and energy requirements of magic. Nasuada orders the sorceress to develop a spell that can mass-produce high-quality lace with minimal effort. Trianna initially scoffs at the idea of using magic for seamstress work until Nasuada explains the economic potential. The plan involves selling the magically created lace throughout the Empire to fund the Varden’s war effort. Nasuada instructs Trianna to find an experienced lace maker to consult on patterns and to report back the following morning.



### Chapter 37: Elva

Jörmundur visits Nasuada to report a strange message from a boy who appeared to be a werecat. He leads Nasuada to the west side of Borromeo Castle to visit the infant Eragon previously blessed in Farthen Dûr. They encounter a room draped in dark fabric and lit by multicolored dwarf lanterns. Nasuada finds Angela the herbalist and Solembum the werecat guarding a pale girl who appears to be three or four years old. The child introduces herself as Elva, revealing a dragon mark on her brow and speaking with an adult's voice.

Elva explains that Eragon’s "blessing" actually cursed her to sense and feel the pain of everyone around her. The magic in her blood forces her to protect others from suffering, often at the cost of her own well-being. She has aged rapidly to better cope with the physical demands of this magical compulsion. Elva correctly identifies Nasuada’s deepest fears and provides comforting words that move the leader to tears. Despite the comfort, Nasuada feels a deep sense of loathing for the child’s unnatural and intrusive abilities.

Elva offers to act as an assassin for Nasuada to ensure the war ends quickly and stops her constant exposure to agony. Nasuada witnesses the girl eating meat with a ravenous, wolf-like intensity to sate her accelerated metabolism. Angela explains that Elva can only predict events two or three hours in advance and often stays indoors for safety. Nasuada decides to use Elva as a tool to discern the weaknesses and troubles of her political opponents. She tasks Angela with supervising the girl and reporting her status regularly to the leadership.



### Chapter 38: Resurgence

A violent storm strikes Ellesméra, waking Eragon as wind and rain lash against the tree-dwelling's openings. Eragon attempts to close the portals but struggles to keep his balance as the great tree sways in the tempest. Saphira provides her tail as a handrail to help Eragon reach and lock the cloth membrane over the main portal. The room goes silent as the fabric holds firm against the external elements, appearing taut like a drum. Saphira directs Eragon to the upstairs study to prevent the destruction of his books and writing materials.

In the study, Eragon fights through a whirlwind of flying paper and quills to reach the upper portal. Just as he secures the opening, a sudden and agonizing seizure from his back wound strikes him. The pain from the scar left by the Shade Durza causes Eragon to scream until his voice goes hoarse. He collapses to the floor and loses consciousness as his mental connection with Saphira recedes. Saphira howls in frustration below because she is too large to reach him through the narrow staircase.

Eragon eventually wakes with a sour taste in his mouth and finds his limbs knotted from the physical trauma. He crawls down the stairs and encounters Saphira, who has jammed her head into the passage in her panic. Saphira becomes stuck in the wood, and Eragon must push against her nose to help her extract herself. The dragon’s scales gouge the tree's bark and ruin the delicate patterns grown into the walls of the stairwell. Eragon huddles against Saphira’s warm belly to rest for several hours before their next lesson with Oromis.



### Chapter 39: Why Do You Fight?

Eragon wakes to Oromis’s buzzing timepiece and prepares for his lessons despite his lingering physical soreness. Oromis contacts Eragon mentally and instructs him to bring the sword Zar’roc for their daily training. Eragon and Saphira fly to the Crags of Tel’naeír, observing fallen pines and storm damage across the forest. Oromis challenges Eragon to a sword duel using the silver-maned elf’s bronze blade, Naegling. Despite using magic to protect his blade's edge, Eragon finds himself completely outmatched by the elf’s superior speed and reflexes.

During the duel, Oromis suffers a brief fit from his own illness, but Eragon fails to capitalize on the opening. Eragon overextends himself and triggers another debilitating seizure from his back wound, falling into the mud. After the fit passes, Oromis sends Eragon to meditate on a stump and listen to the forest’s thoughts. Eragon observes a colony of red ants and watches them heroically overpower a spider that invaded their territory. He realizes that the ants see the world primarily through smell and view the spider as a dragon-sized monster.

Oromis explains that the goal of meditation is to condition the mind to sense the presence and intent of others. This mental awareness serves as a defense against anonymous magicians who might attempt assassination through magic. The elf teacher defines logic as the most important mental tool a person can possess for making analytical decisions. He argues that clear thinking is a better protection against evil than a noble but clouded disposition. Finally, Oromis challenges Eragon to provide a logical justification for fighting a war that causes widespread suffering to innocent people.
